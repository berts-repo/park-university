I wanted to explore how Network Address Translation (NAT), and Classless Inter-Domain Routing (CIDR) are interrelated to Variable Length Subnet Mask (VLSM) subnetting. I have tried to simplify this to establish my understanding of what is going on. What I discovered is that NAT was later developed as a means to make a private network only use one public IP address, this helps to control the amount of unused IPv4 address's. VLSM is the process of subnetting the network, and CIDR notation is the block of IP ranges available in each subnet. All three of these protocols are used in conjunction to help limit the waste of IPv4 address's.
