# Final Exam - General Information 

When:

2 hours, between Monday and Sunday of Unit 8.

It may be a good idea to take the exam by Saturday (or even Friday) to give you some buffer time, just in case something may happen in the last minute. 

It’s also a good idea to set aside a 2.5-hour window for the actual test, as the pre-test check-in may take at least a few minutes. 

 Format: 

Allowed

handwritten notes on either a 5x7” card or a half sheet of 8.5 x 11” paper (US letter size: 8.5 x 11''). You may use both sides. You must show your notes to the camera, two seconds per side at the beginning of the test as part of the room scan.

Scratch Paper: you're allowed a small whiteboard (or one blank piece of scratch paper inserted into a plastic sleeve), and a dry erase marker. You must show your scratch paper and erase it on camera before submitting your exam.

An on-screen basic calculator will be provided through the proctoring system (see the Basic calculator at  [link: https://honorlock.kb.help/students-during-exam/honorlock-calculator/] Honorlock calculator). 

No other resources are allowed. You will only be allowed to open the final exam (a longer quiz) and type up your answer on the quiz page.  You will not be allowed to use another application/additional browser tab/window/device. 

Online Proctoring Requirement

Be in a private, well-lit room free from other people and noises;

Clear your workspace;

No break is allowed during the test. Refresh yourself before starting the exam. Please follow  [link: https://www.park.edu/terms-and-regulations/disability-guidelines/] Park's Disability Guideline if you require accommodation.

A practice quiz has been set up to help you get acclimated to the online proctoring system. Please find it through the Honorlock link in the left hand side Canvas menu. The practice quiz is optional and may be taken repeatedly by the end of Unit 7. 

Final Exam “Show one question at a time” Setup

The final exam is set up to display one question at a time.  All unit assignments (in quiz format) are set up in this style to help you prepare for it. 

You may use the Next/Previous buttons at the bottom of the page or the sidebar on the top right corner of the page to navigate through questions.  Questions are not locked so you may go back to an answered question and revise as often as you need. See Canvas Student Help page “ [link: https://community.canvaslms.com/docs/DOC-10535-421291043] How do I take a quiz where I can only view one question at a time?”  

Types of questions:

Concept questions: explain, compare/contrast, and/or illustrate with examples.

Problem solving

Topics: 

Comprehensive (i.e. unit 1 – 7).  Review all quizzes, assignments, and discussion questions.   

Here are the overall learning outcomes for the course, which provide a big picture guide for final exam questions:

Solve problems involving Boolean algebra, integer computer representation and arithmetic (such as binary, octal, hexadecimal, 2’s complement), and floating-point computer representation and arithmetic.

Design combinational and sequential circuits. Explain various circuitry components including gates, multiplexers/demultiplexers, encoders/decoders, latches, flip-flops, registers, counters, and adders.

Explain and analyze Memory and I/O designs.

Explain and analyze CPU designs.

Additional guideline regarding specific content will be provided near the end of the course.
