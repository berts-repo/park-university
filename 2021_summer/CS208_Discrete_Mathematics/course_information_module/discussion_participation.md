Q & A under units 1 to 8: This is optional, thus will not be graded. It is used to help you communicate with your fellow classmates.

Feedback under unit 8: This is optional, thus will not be graded. However, the instructor appreciates your feedback from your experience in going through the entire course.

Discussion under units 1 to 7: This is required and graded. You must follow the rules specified below to obtain the maximum discussion score for each unit.

Proposing a problem (6 points):

Prepare a problem according to the Problem Proposer description, and post your problem. The proposed problem should be thoughtful and clear, fit the theme of the topic, make sense logically, display originality, and be substantial so that your post can generate additional thoughts, discussions, concepts, etc. The problem should be between 50 words to 300 words in length. Use your own words, no plagiarizing. You may copy and paste a sentence or two from another site with quotes and appropriate attribution to support your work, but in general these posts must be your own writing.

Your problem should not be the same as or a slight modification to the examples or exercises provided in the textbook. Your instructor has the full authority in judging the similarity of your proposed problems to the existing examples/exercises provided in the textbook. Penalty may be imposed should such a similarity is found.

When you post your problem in the discussion thread, mark it as "Problem: ........." so that it is clearly identified as a problem rather than a reply or a comment.

You must post your problem by Thursday 11:59 p.m. Central Time. Note: A zero is earned if your initial post to a discussion question is not posted by 11:59 PM CT on Thursday

Managing the proposed problem (3 points):

You are the owner of the problem that you propose. This means that you must monitor the posts made by your classmates for the problem that you propose. In particular, you should answer their questions, and let your classmates know whether they answer your question correctly or explain the reason why their answers are incorrect. Your posting should be clear, thoughtful, and between 50 words to 300 words in length. It is preferable if you can respond to the peers’ response postings as timely as possible to contribute to their learning on the subject. 

In order to get any response from your classmates, you should try to post your problem as early in the unit as possible. As it is not reasonable to expect to get responses from your classmates when the problem is posted toward the end of the unit (such as Saturday and Sunday), the deadline (Friday 11:59 p.m. Central Time) for posting your problem is imposed.

If you don’t get any response from your classmates, you will not receive points for managing your problem. If you post your problem early in the unit but do not get any response from your classmates, try to make your problem more interesting or post new problems. The posting of additional problems does not have the Friday 11:59 p.m. CT deadline restriction.

If for some unforeseen reasons such that there are not enough problems to go around so that every student has a chance to answer a problem, the instructor may solicit additional problems from the class. In this case, extra credit will be given for the additional problem posts. The solicitation of problems will likely be toward the end of the unit (i.e., Saturday or Sunday.) 

Responding to a peer’s problem (3 points):

Select one problem posed by your classmates, solve the problem, and post your response.

You are not to make posts to solve your own problem.

You should provide a justification that is clear and detailed. Your step-by-step reasoning should make sense logically. Your response should be constructive and provide value to the problem. Statements such as "I agree" or "excellent point" do not provide value to the discussion. Those comments and praises are certainly welcome in the discussion, but you also need to provide comments that promote learning. The posting should be between 50 words to 300 words in length.

In general, to ensure that most of the problems get addressed, no more than one student can answer any single problem posed by your fellow classmate. This means that, in most cases, the responses given by the students other than the first one will not be graded.

For the case in which a student is trying to answer a proposed problem while other student is working on the same problem:

Since the above rule (d) only counts the first student answering the problem, it will be ok for you to post a notification before you start to work on a problem. Your notice will say something like you are working on the problem and asking other students not to work on the same problem. However, if you do not post your solution within 2 hours, then your notification is invalid and anyone can choose to answer the problem 2 hours after you post the initial notification.

You must post your response by Sunday 3:00pm Central Standard Time.

Summary of the unit discussion requirement:

The grading in this discussion thread is based on your proposed problem (6 points), how well you manage the posts by your classmates to your problem (3 points), and your response to one problem posed by one of your classmates (3 points). Therefore, a minimum three constructive posts are required in each unit discussion. In total you are REQUIRED to have three discussion postings to earn the full 12 points on each discussion forum in Units 1 - 7.
