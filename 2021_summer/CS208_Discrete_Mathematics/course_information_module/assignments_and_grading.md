Late Submission Policy

It is unfair to other students to allow some individuals to submit assignments after the scheduled due date. Therefore, all assignments are expected to be completed by set deadlines. A zero (0) is assigned for any homework, quiz, discussion forum, and or Final Exam that is not completed on-time as listed in the scheduled set in Canvas. Please note even these reasons are only acceptable at the discretion of your instructor.

A medical emergency or a serious acute illness. All medical emergencies and illnesses must be verified by a note on letterhead by an M.D., D.O., P.A., or R.N. I will not normally accept a note from other health professionals (e.g., Ph.D., MSW, D.C., Physical Therapist) because their professional functions rarely involve medical emergencies or acute illnesses. I will accept late work for students who can provide evidence of a verified medical emergency (but not acute illness) involving a child, spouse, parent, sibling, or grandparent.

An Accident or Police Emergency. I will require an accident report or note on letterhead from an appropriate law enforcement officer to accept late work due to accidents or police emergencies (e.g., assault on student, student taken hostage, detained witness of a crime).

Unforeseen Jury or Witness Duty. I will require a note on letterhead from a judge or attorney stating you had no advance notice of duty to accept late work due to jury or witness duty.

Unforeseen Military Deployment or Activation. I will require a note on official letterhead from your commanding officer stating you had no advance notice of deployment or activation.

Funerals for Immediate Family Member (e.g., parents, siblings, grandparents, aunts/uncles, first cousins). I will require a copy of the obituary or a note from a minister or funeral director.

Problem proposal

Deadline: 11:59 pm Thursday, CT (Central Time). Note: A zero is earned if your initial post to a discussion question is not posted by Thursday, 11:59 PM CT – this ensure students are not waiting until Sunday to complete the initial discussion forum posting and responses can be done by the listed due date in Canvas.

Post count: One problem proposal.

Word count: A post should be between 50-300 words.

Content: The proposed problem should be thoughtful and clear, fit the theme of the topic, make sense logically, display originality, and be substantial so that your post can generate additional thoughts, discussions, concepts, etc. Use your own words, no plagiarizing. You may copy and paste a sentence or two from another site with quotes and appropriate attribution to support your work, but in general these posts must be your own writing.

English Syntax: Proper use of English and free of typographical errors and grammatical mistakes. When posting a problem, begin the description with "Problem: ........." so that it is clearly identified as a problem rather than a reply or a comment.

Problem Management

Deadline: A problem management post is due by 11:59pm Sunday, CT (Central Time). However, you should try to monitor and respond to the peers’ response postings as timely as possible to contribute to their learning on the subject. 

Post count: One problem management post.

Word count: A post should be between 50-300 words.

Content: You are the owner of your problem. This means that you must monitor the posts posed by your classmates for the problem that you propose. In particular, you should answer their questions, and let your classmates know whether they answer your question correctly or explain the reason why their answers are incorrect. Your posting should be clear and thoughtful, make sense logically, and provide meaningful justification.

English Syntax: Proper use of English and free of typographical errors and grammatical mistakes.

Chapter Homework

Chapter Homework is to be done via zyBooks. Depending on the number of tasks that are required to complete correctly, zyBooks will report the percentage of correct completion. In Canvas, the point earned will be the percentage earned in zyBooks multiplied by 26.  For example, if one earns 75% correct completion, he/she receives 75% * 26 points (which is 19.5 points.)

Deadline: 11:59pm Sunday, CT (Central Time). Note: Completion of any work in the zyBook is taken as of the end of the unit (i.e. 11:59 PM CT on Sunday). Any additional work completed in the zyBook on a chapter after the unit is completed is not figured into the score for a grade.

Chapter Quiz

Each chapter quiz consists of 8 multiple choice, multiple answer, true-false questions, and questions requiring you to write your calculation answers. The following guide shows how to earn points depending on the types of the questions. 

Deadline: 11:59pm Sunday, CT (Central Time). Note: The chapter quiz automatically closes in the class portal at 11:59 PM CT of when it is listed due in the syllabus. Quizzes are not accepted late in the course.

Rubric

2 points

Multiple-Choice or True-False: Answer correctly.

2 points

Question requiring you to write your calculation answer: Your answer must not only be correct, but also, in general, be in its final, most reduced form. For example, if the solution is 9, but your answer is 4+5 which is not in its final, most reduced form, then your answer will be considered incorrect.

Variable points

Multiple-Answers question: There are multiple correct answers per question. If there are n correct answers in a question, each correctly answered choice is worth (1/n) multiplied by 2 points for the question. For a choice that is answered incorrectly, a deduction of (1/n) multiplied by 2 points is imposed. The minimum point that a student will receive for a question is 0.

Final Exam

This exam is comprehensive, covering all materials from unit 1 to unit 7.

The time length of the final exam is two hours.

It is proctored via Proctor U.

The exam is conducted in unit 8. It must be taken before Sunday 11:59 p.m. (Central Time) of unit 8.

Exam question styles: True-False, Multiple-Choice, Multiple-Answer questions, and questions requiring you to write your calculation answers. 

There are around 24 questions in the exam.

The table below shows the point calculation. 

The exam is taken online via Park University Online Canvas website.

If a question requires you to write your calculation answer, you must compute to the final value. For example, If the answer to a question is -9, and you answer is (1-10+4-4), then your answer is considered incorrect as the system is looking for an exact match to the answer -9.

It is closed books, notes, websites, and electronic devices, except to use a computer to access the online exam.

You are allowed one blank piece of scratch paper inserted into a plastic sleeve, and a dry erase marker. You will be required to show your scratch paper to the proctor and erase it on camera before you will be permitted to exit the testing session.

Deadline: 11:59pm Sunday, CT (Central Time) of Unit 8.

Rubric

2 points

Multiple-Choice, True-False, or Write-in question: Answer correctly.

2 points

Question requiring you to write your calculation answer: Your answer must not only be correct, but also, in general, be in its final, most reduced form. For example, if the solution is 9, but your answer is 4+5 which is not in its final, most reduced form, then your answer will be considered incorrect.

Variable points

Multiple-Answers question: There are multiple correct answers per question. If there are n correct answers in a question, each correctly answered choice is worth (1/n) multiplied by 2 points for the question. For a choice that is answered incorrectly, a deduction of (1/n) multiplied by 2 points are imposed. The minimum point that a student will receive for a question is 0.
