Course Details

Title: Discrete Mathematics

Course Description: This course introduces the student to selected finite systems pertinent to the study of computer science. Course topics will include combinatorial problem solving, logic, Boolean algebra, combinatorial circuits, sets, relations, functions, proofs, mathematical induction, recurrence relations, graphs, trees, and counting techniques.

Credit Hours: 3:0:3

Prerequisites Courses: A grade of C or better in any math course >= MA125, or a ACT math score >= 23, or an SAT math score >= 510, or a COMPASS score >= 66 in the Algebra placement domain, or a COMPASS score 0-45 in the College Algebra placement domain.

Core Learning Outcomes

At the conclusion of the course, learners will be able to:

Explain and solve problems involving logics, Boolean algebra, combinatorial circuits, sets, relations, and functions, proofs, mathematical induction, and recurrence relations.

Explain and solve problems involving graphs, paths, circuits, graph coloring, directed graphs, shortest path algorithms.

Explain and solve problems involving trees, spanning trees, rooted trees, binary trees, and tree traversal algorithms.

Explain and solve problems involving counting techniques such as permutations, combinations, binomial theorem, and probability.

Course Assignments

Course Information Policy Quiz– There is a policy quiz under “Course Information” testing your understanding of the policies and administration rules in this class. You can take this quiz multiple times. It is due by 11:59 p.m. Sunday, CT (Central Time) of unit 1.

Discussion Participation - There is one discussion question in each unit. You are to make a minimum of 3 posts identified as a problem proposal post, a problem response post, and a problem management post in each unit. Each post should be 50-300 words.

A problem proposal post is due by 11:59 pm Friday, CT (Central Time). 

A problem response post is due by 3:00pm Sunday, CT (Central Time).

A problem management post is due by 11:59pm Sunday, CT (Central Time). However, you should try to monitor and respond to the peers’ response postings as timely as possible to contribute to their learning on the subject. 

Chapter homework – In each chapter, there will be one homework assignment which consists of all activities marked as “Participation Activity” and/or “Challenge Activity” embedded within the chapter text material in the zyBook. You can perform each activity as many times as you wish before the deadline. The deadline is 11:59 p.m. Sunday, CT.

Chapter Quizzes– There will be one quiz for each chapter. The quiz will be taken via Canvas. The quiz length is 30 minutes. The deadline is 11:59 p.m. Sunday, CT.

Final Exam – There will be one final exam in unit 8. The final exam will be 2 hours long taken via Canvas. The exam length is 1 hour. The deadline is 11:59 p.m. Sunday, CT.

Course Feedback – The Park University Student Opinion of Teaching Survey is available for completion in Units 7 and 8. Refer to  [link: https://canvas.park.edu/courses/61678/pages/assignments-and-grading#sots] Assignments and Grading for more information on the Park University Student Opinion of Teaching Survey. 

Student Opinion of Teaching Survey

Starting in Unit 7, you will have access to the online Student Opinion of Teaching Survey for your courses.  When you open the Survey page, you will see “Load Student Opinion of Teaching Survey in a new window” – please click this button. In the new window that opens you will see a list of courses for which you have surveys to complete. Each course evaluation should take no longer than 15 minutes to complete. You will be able to access your surveys multiple times to edit answers or to complete saved surveys. Surveys must be completed by the last day of the course, 11:59 p.m., central. 

Please be assured that your responses are presented anonymously to your instructors, and will be compiled for the university via a third party provider, Campus Labs.  All student responses will be summarized and reported to your instructor(s) after the term is over and grades have been posted.
