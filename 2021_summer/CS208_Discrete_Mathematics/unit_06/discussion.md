Proposed Problem

In this unit, you learn many concepts and techniques in Discrete Mathematics. Choose one concept or technique covered in the unit and propose a problem for your classmate to solve. For example, some example problems provided in the book may be using a technique to solve a situation for the airline industry. You can propose a problem that uses a technique covered in the unit to solve a situation occurred in a different context (such as restaurant, office, or zoo) or to solve a situation created from your imagination.

The proposed problem should be thoughtful and clear, fit the theme of the topic, make sense logically, display originality, and be substantial so that your post can generate additional thoughts, discussions, concepts, etc. The post should be between 50 to 300 words. Use your own words, no plagiarizing. You may copy and paste a sentence or two from another site with quotes and appropriate attribution to support your work, but in general these posts must be your own writing.

Problem Response

Pick one problem proposed by your classmate, solve the problem, and post your response. The problem response should be thoughtful and clear, fit the theme of the topic, make sense logically, and provide meaningful justification. A simple response of “I agree” or “I do not agree” without giving any reason or justification is not sufficient. The post should be between 50 to 300 words. Use your own words, no plagiarizing. You may copy and paste a sentence or two from another site with quotes and appropriate attribution to support your work, but in general these posts must be your own writing.

Problem Manager

You are the owner of the problem that you propose. This means that you must monitor the posts made by your classmates for the problem that you propose. In particular, you should answer their questions, and let your classmates know whether they answer your question correctly or explain the reason why their answers are incorrect. Your posting should be clear, thoughtful, and between 50 words to 300 words in length. It is preferable if you can respond to the peers’ response postings as timely as possible to contribute to their learning on the subject. 

Directions

View  [link: https://canvas.park.edu/courses/61678/pages/discussion-participation] Discussion Participation for more information about rules and requirements.

A problem management post is due by 11:59 p.m. Sunday, CT (Central Time). However, you should try to monitor and respond to the peers’ response postings as timely as possible to contribute to their learning on the subject.
