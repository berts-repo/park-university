Course Details

Title: Technology in a Global Society

Course Description:  This course presents the social, political, economic, multicultural, and ethical issues surrounding the use of computers and computer technology. Course work includes class discussion, readings, collaborative projects and a formal term paper on selected topics.

Credit Hours: 3:0:3

Prerequisites Courses: EN106

Core Learning Outcomes

At the conclusion of the course, learners will be able to:

Examine the major historical implications of information technology.

Identify some of the basic concepts of a global society influenced by Information Technology.

Evaluate possible effects of emerging technologies upon the social, cultural, economic, and ethical values of the United States and world.

Analyze the ethical considerations of the introduction of new technology globally.

Course Assignments.

Annual Editions Journal (20 points) – Due by 11:59 p.m. Sunday, CT. Read the assigned selections from Annual Editions (Friedman & Mittleman). Download the Annual Editions Journal Summary form and complete for each article. Submit the completed form on the assignment page in each unit.

Current Articles Review (10 points) – Due by 11:59 p.m. Sunday, CT (Central Time). Locate and read a current article about new technology. Determine the main idea and major points presented in the reading. Summarize the article on the form provided. Include your reaction to the article. Post only title of the article and the link to access it on the Article Review page in the Introductory Canvas CS300 module. Submit the complete review on the Article Review assignment page in each module.

Discussions (20 points) – Due by 11:59 p.m. Sunday, CT.  Participate in the discussion in Units 1- 8.  Each question response should be 100-150 words. Initial posts are due by 11:59 p.m. Thursday, CT (Central Time).  After posting your ideas, read and respond to the post of at least two other classmates for full credit. Writing just “I agree” is not sufficient. Share your thoughts on your points of agreement or disagreement in detail.

Quizzes (20 points) – Due by 11:59 p.m. Sunday, CT. Read the assigned chapters in Ethics for the Information Age (Quinn). Complete the quiz covering that information. You may take the quiz a second time to improve your score. Canvas will record the highest grade.

Individual Research Paper (100 points) – Due by 11:59 p.m. Sunday, (Central Time), Unit 4. The individual term paper is an in-depth research opportunity designed to allow a more complete understanding of how a specific new ideas or new technologies have come to change our world. Each student will elect to research an influential person, or  technology idea that has had an impact on the global society. The paper will follow the standard APA format and will be submitted in Unit 4.

Final Exam (200 Points) – Due by 11:59 p.m. Sunday, CT, Unit 8.  The comprehensive two hour final exam will cover the readings in Ethics for the Information Age (Quinn) and will consist of multiple choice and essay questions.  Unlike the quizzes, the final exam may be taken only one time.
