When reading through the chapter I was curious about what the level 0 Stratum clocks were, and how they work with NTP, e.g., atomic and GPS. I wasn’t sure if they were a device, connection, or software that kept track of the time. I understood the concept of how a NTP server works and needs to synchronize the times of different system. This especially becomes true when we apply Moore’s Law and the speed of which a processor runs have substantially increased, as well as data being stored over multiple servers (clusters), which is called “causality” (Chandler, 2022).

An atomic clock works by essentially measuring the vibration of atoms. What is really happening is the atoms are sent through a resonator, where they are hit with radiation which changes the state of the atom, then the number of atoms that are pulled out with a magnet and counted by a detector (Bikos, 2024). These are measured at 9,192,631,770 oscillations a second.
