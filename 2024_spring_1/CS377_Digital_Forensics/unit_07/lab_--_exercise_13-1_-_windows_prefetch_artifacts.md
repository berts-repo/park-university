Excellent job on the lab this week. Prefetch files are some of the most valuable artifacts for a forensic examiner. Prefetch files show evidence of a specific program’s execution. They can prove that a suspect ran a program. This may be very important especially if you think the suspect tried destroying evidence. For example, you may see prefetch files that show the suspect used a program such as CCleaner to cover their history. Prefetch files are all named in a common format where the name of the application is listed, then an eight-character hash of the location where the application was run, followed by the .PF extension.
