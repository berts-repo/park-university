Class,

When working through this assignment, I recognize the recurring loop of challenges that a digital forensics investigation might keep encountering. The legal and technical challenges faced in a cloud investigation are intertwined: for example when collecting data in an investigation it might encounter jurisdictional challenges of accessing the evidence, or they might face architectural challenges when they are trying to get data from a non-centralized location, or maybe the data it is protected from an SLA. 

 

 Legal Challenges

Service Level Agreements

Traditionally services are available if the hardware is operational, but with a cloud-based SLA there could be a “service availability” clause that only promises access in a 12-hour window.

Jurisdiction Issues

This one is straight forward; the cloud platform could be in a different state or country, with different laws, where traditionally an investigation would most likely take place in once location.

Accessing Evidence

Traditionally a court-issued warrant allows access and collection of evidence from devices owned by the company. In a cloud environment providers may have terms-of-service agreements with third parties that affect how they respond to warrants and subpoenas.

 Technical Challenges

Architecture

In a traditional investigation the architecture is normally centralized, while in a cloud environment the artifacts could be distributed across multiple datacenters which are most likely virtualized (no true MAC addresses).

Data Collection

Data stored in the cloud may be encrypted. Investigators may need to work with their CSP to obtain encryption keys or decrypt data for forensic analysis, which leads back to jurisdictional and legal accessing of data.

Analysis of data

Data in the cloud may be fragmented across multiple geographical locations, or the log files and metadata that is archived might be deleted after a certain amount of time.

Anti-forensics

The cloud challenges faced include data fragmentation, embedded malware, and changing timestamps. Dealing with SLA, Jursidictions and collection of data makes it difficult in a cloud environment.

Incident first responders

In a traditional investigation the investigator would have immediate access to the network infrastructure, while in a cloud investigation the investigator would have to work with the CSPs personnel staff, who might not know how or care to help the investigation.

Role management

In a cloud environment access-control might involve third parties, who manage the cloud infrastructure. In a traditional investigation a company usually has very strict access-control policies on who is allowed to access sensitive data.

Standards and Training

In a cloud-centric environment investigation faces different challenges in standards and training. Corporations must follow cloud standards and provide specialized training for the cloud infostructure.
