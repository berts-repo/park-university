Course Details

Title:  CS377 Digital Forensics 

Course Description: This course will focus on principles, procedures and techniques of digital forensics. The topics to be examined include data acquisition, incident scene processing, data and file recovery, digital forensics analysis and validation, virtual machine, networks, mobile device and cloud forensics, etc.  Several of these concepts may be put into practice using laboratory exercises.

Credit Hours: 3:0:3

Prerequisite Courses: CS335, CS351 and CS366

Core Learning Outcomes

At the conclusion of the course, learners will be able to:

Explain how to prepare a digital forensics investigation by taking a systematic approach.

Describe standard procedures for conducting forensic analysis.

Apply different computer forensic tools to a given cybercrime scene.

Apply current practices to data recovery and acquisition.

 Late Assignment Policy

Discussions may be made past the initial Wednesday deadline with a deduction of 20% per day for lateness. No discussion posting will be allowed after the final Sunday deadline since no one else will be discussing the topic.

Other assignments from units 1 to 7 may be turned in late with a 20% penalty per day. Note that items turned in more than 4 days late will receive a 0.

All unit 8 assignments must be completed on time.

Student Opinion of Teaching

Starting in Unit 7, you will have access to the online Student Opinion of Teaching Survey for your courses. When you open the Survey page, you will see “Load Student Opinion of Teaching Survey in a new window” – please click this button. In the new window that opens you will see a list of courses for which you have surveys to complete. Each course evaluation should take no longer than 15 minutes to complete. You will be able to access your surveys multiple times to edit answers or to complete saved surveys. Surveys must be completed by the last day of the course, 11:59 p.m. CT.

Please be assured that your responses are presented anonymously to your instructors, and will be compiled for the university via a third party provider, Campus Labs. All student responses will be summarized and reported to your instructor(s) after the term is over and grades have been posted.
