Excellent job on the hands-on project. In this project, you had the opportunity of using FTK imager to hash. A hash value is a result of a one-way mathematical formula that provides a fixed output. The output is essentially a digital fingerprint which is unique to a file, drive, or other type of digital media. If a single bit changes, the entire hash will change. From a forensic standpoint, we can use a hash as a check to ensure that we have received a bit-for-bit copy of an item or to ensure that nothing as changed (i.e. integrity of digital evidence). For most forensics, a MD5 or SHA1 hash will suffice. Keep up the good work. Don’t hesitate reaching out if you have any questions on any of the material covered this week.
