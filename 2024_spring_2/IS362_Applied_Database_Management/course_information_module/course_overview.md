Course Details

Title:  

Course Description: This course builds on the Data Management Concepts course and focuses on the creation, administration, and use of databases. This course assumes a knowledge of database system concepts. The student will be introduced to application program development in the database environment with emphasis on setting up, modifying, and querying a database.

Credit Hours: 3:0:3

Prerequisite Courses: IS361

Core Learning Outcomes

At the conclusion of the course, learners will be able to:

Create an actual database using a relational database management system.

Formulate SQL queries to retrieve information from a relational database management system.

Create high-level language programs to create, manipulate and retrieve information from a relational database model.

Manage a relational database management system.

Course Assignments.

Discussion Questions (10 points) - There will be discussion questions in Units 1- 8.  Each initial response should be at least 150 words. Each response to classmates should be at least 100 words. 

First post due 11:59 p.m., Thursday, CT. 

Respond to one or more classmates by 11:59 p.m., Sunday, CT.

The discussions are worth 20% of the final grade.

Unit Lab Assignments (100 points) - In Units 1-7, there are unit assignments due by 11:59 p.m. Sunday, CT (Central Time). The unit lab assignments are worth 35% of the final grade.

Course Project (300 points) - The Course Project requires a selection of subjects by Sunday at 11:59 pm (CST) of the first unit (worth 10 points in the delivery of the first milestone). There will also be a delivery of results of 3 project milestones by Sunday at 11:59 pm (CST) on Units 3, 5, and 7 (each worth 100 points). The Core Project is worth 24% of the final grade.

Final Exam (100 points) - In Unit 8, there is a final exam due by 11:59 p.m. Sunday CT (Central Time). The final exam is worth 20% of the final grade. This exam is to be proctored, closed books, and limited notes. Multiples sheets of scratch paper are allowed. Bathroom breaks are not allowed. Students should take care of their needs before starting the exam.
