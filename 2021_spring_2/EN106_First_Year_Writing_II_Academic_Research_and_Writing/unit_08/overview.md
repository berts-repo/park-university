# Unit 8: Overview 

Looking Ahead: Writing In Your Major

Your  primary  task  this  unit is  to  complete the  Exploratory Assignment.  You  will  also  have  the opportunity to reflect on your experiences in EN106 and set goals for future work.

The Exploratory Assignment asks you to use your research and writing skills to learn more about the types of writing and research required in your college major or intended career field. Ultimately, you will produce a brief essay that outlines what you have learned about how writing works in your academic discipline and/or professional field.

Task List

Monday - Wednesday, read all assignments for the unit.

By Thursday noon CST or earlier, make your initial post to the Discussion area.

Thursday – Sunday, interact with peers by posting in the Discussion.

Monday-Sunday, complete the Exploratory Assignment.

By Sunday, you will have completed the final component of the course. Congratulations!

Unit Learning Outcomes

Maintain a controlling idea/thesis for a variety of academic genres (CLO #2).

Apply strategies for developing academic arguments across the disciplines, including conducting research and incorporating culturally diverse perspectives (CLO #3).

Consider the rhetorical situations faced by academic writers to respond appropriately in both writing and research (CLO #4).

Use common formats and conventions (e.g., research, structure, documentation, tone, mechanics) for various genres of academic discourse (CLO #5).
