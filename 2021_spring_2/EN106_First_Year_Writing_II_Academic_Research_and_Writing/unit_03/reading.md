# Unit 3: Reading

Required

Greene & Lidinsky, “From Formulating to Developing a Thesis” (Ch. 6).

Chapter 6 addresses how writers move from actively listening at the sidelines of an academic conversation to engaging in that dialogue as participants. Indeed, being able to identify the central issues at play in an academic conversation and to develop a compelling response (or thesis) are key abilities of successful academic writers.

Greene & Lidinsky, re-read articles from Ch. 14.

To complete Essay #2, you will need to read or re-read the assigned articles from Ch. 14 of From Inquiry to Academic Writing. As the Essay #2 assignment explains, your job this week is to respond to at least one of the articles you have read from this chapter.

Lunsford, "Integrating Sources and Avoiding Plagiarism" (Ch. 13), "MLA Style" (Ch. 15), and  "APA  Style" (Ch. 16).

These chapters offer helpful tips and examples for effectively incorporating quotations and paraphrases from sources into your writing. Read these chapters carefully! Scan the chapters on MLA and APA styles to make sure you understand the basics of citation formatting for both MLA and APA guidelines. Plan to come back to the chapters on MLA and APA style later this term!
