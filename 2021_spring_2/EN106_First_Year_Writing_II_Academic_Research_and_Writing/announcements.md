## Week Eight - The Final One!
*Posted: 2021-05-03T10:00:25Z*

This week you'll turn your attention to the writing done in your own field of study. If you're a business major, you'll examine the guidelines and expectations for business - both in the academy and in the work world. If you're in social sciences, or humanities, or natural sciences, you'll be exploring the relevant rules for those fields. 

The module pages give you some specific resources to review, mostly within Park's web site. In addition, please read Chapter 10 in Easy Writer.

There are several essays in From Inquiry to Academic Writing that serve as models for writing in specific disciplines. Here are a few you might choose to review:

Paul, Nancy. "A Greener Approach to Groceries: Community-Based Agriculture in LaSalle Square." (pgs. 235-245) - this is a student paper in MLA style. It's nicely annotated to show various elements of the essay, including the emphasis on synthesis. 

Pasco, C.J. "'Dude, You're a Fag': Adolescent Masculinity and the Fag Discourse." (pgs. 464-481) - an example of the IMRAD style of writing in the social sciences (see the headnote, as well as the essay's construction). This is also a fine example of the correct use of APA style. 

hooks, bell. "Seeing and Making Culture: Representing the Poor." (pgs. 486-493) - an example of humanities writing, with deliberate omission of the usual academic apparatus. 

Hoffman, Andres J. "The Full Scope." (pgs. 693-701) - an example of footnote documentation

You don't need to read these for content, but you might be able to compare them to the guidelines found in the assigned web pages and comment on them as models in your exploratory paper. 

I'll see you in discussion!

## Week Four - Research, Revision and Peer Review
*Posted: 2021-04-05T11:08:50Z*

This week you'll get the opportunity to construct the expanded argument many of you were anticipating last week, so I hope you took my advice and left room for new research and revision. You'll work to expand and revise your essay from last week by adding outside sources you locate through research in the McAfee Library databases (yes, the online databases, not the open Web). I recommend reading all the assignment pages for this Unit before you begin work. The discussion and essay assignment pages are situated within the whole of the week's unit and all the pages in the unit inform the work you're asked to do.

In addition to the reading assignments you'll find within Module 4, read Chapter 8 in FIAW, particularly the section on synthesis. I'll be looking for you to synthesize your sources, rather than presenting them one by one, in your essay this week. Also, the essay you submit this week should be a complete revision of your earlier essay and, as the assignment itself says, not simply the previous essay with new paragraphs added in. 

You'll find links to a Library Material course in both the Overview page and the Reading page, but the Overview page gives you a better description of how it fits in this week's work. In addition, Park University Student Success Librarian Allison Rand has put together a resource page just for EN 106 students.It can help you develop research questions and begin your search. Check it out at   [link: https://library.park.edu/EN106] https://library.park.edu/EN106  

Your revised draft is due for a Peer Review workshop on Wednesday - a day earlier than the normal discussion deadline. Peer Review workshop due dates are part of the scoring rubric, so be sure to get your submissions in on time for potential full credit. Also, in order to ensure everyone gets a review and no one has to do extra work, please claim the essay you want to review with an initial reply before you post the review. If you see an essay has been claimed, move on to one that has not. If you do not claim the essay you review, you may be asked to do another one. Your review should by placed directly into the dialog box even if you also return a marked-up draft to the author.  

In our discussion this week, we'll be building a research community. Each of you will post results from your research, and then you'll work to help others enhance their results. Between the research and the peer review, you should be able to produce a thoroughly revised and enhanced essay for submission at the end of the week. 

Again, please review all the pages for the Unit 4 Module before you begin work, and get started on your research right away to ensure the best possible revision draft for the workshop. If you have any questions, don't hesitate to ask. I'll see you in discussion.
