# Student Responsibilities

This page identifies the responsibilities of and expectations for all online learners in EN106. If any of these items are unclear, or confusing, or you have concerns about your abilities to meet these expectations, please contact me using the information provided in the Instructor's Office under Course Information.

General

Read all items in the Course Information Module. Make sure you understand the syllabus policies, assignments and schedule, and how your work will be graded and assessed.

Background Knowledge

To be successful in EN106, you should have the following background knowledge:

Reading comprehension in English

Although this class will help you to develop your reading and writing, you will need to have basic proficiency in reading English before entering this class.

Basic writing proficiency in English

You will learn a lot about academic writing this term, but to succeed, you will need to have basic proficiency in writing sentences and paragraphs in English before entering this class.

 

Technical Skills & Technology Requirements:

To complete this class successfully, you will need to know have the following technical skills and access to the following tools:

Consistent, reliable internet access 

To succeed in this class, you will need internet access. Class discussions, correspondence with your instructor, and many assignments will require the use of Web/internet resources.

E-mail 

Use your  [link: http://www.park.edu/e-mail] Park University e-mail account for all class-related correspondence. Do not use Canvas messages to contact your instructor unless your instructor states otherwise.

Access to a word processor

All of your major writing assignments must be uploaded in .doc, .docx, or .pdf formats. Most word processors, including  [link: http://docs.google.com] Google Docs,  [link: https://office.live.com/start/Word.aspx] Word Online, and  [link: https://www.openoffice.org/] OpenOffice allow you to save your work in these formats.

Ability to post to Canvas discussions

Desktop, mobile, and tablet versions of Canvas allow you to participate in Canvas discussions.

Ability to upload files to Canvas

If you are using a web-based file storage system like [link: http://drive.google.com]  Google Drive or  [link: http://www.dropbox.com/] Dropbox, learn how to transfer files from your storage system to Canvas.

While you will be able to complete many assignments using the mobile or tablet version of Canvas, access to a laptop or desktop computer will make this class much easier, especially when it comes to completing your major writing assignments.

Attendance, Communication, & Conduct

Log in to the course regularly to participate in discussions, submit assignments, and check for new Announcements.

Review the attendance and late work policies specified in the syllabus for this course.

Check your Park University email regularly; you and your instructor should use this email address for all course-related correspondence. Including "EN106" in the subject line of all emails is helpful.

In the unit Discussions, Office posts, and other formal “public” areas of the course, please take care with your language, tone, and style so that all of your communications are clear and professional. 

However, regardless of where you are posting in EN106, or to whom you are sending email, do not include inappropriate content, anything that could be offensive to the recipient, or could be perceived as harassment. 

Participation & Assignment Submission

Participation differs from attendance. While attendance is ungraded, participation is earned as you engage activity in unit course discussions and submitting all course assignments on time.

Read the overview for each unit, which will specify the assignments and due date for each unit. Typically, you will be responsible for submitting initial posts to all discussion threads no later than Thursday noon, Central Standard Time (CST), so that you can engage in class discussions and peer response Thursday - Sunday. Papers are typically due on Sunday midnight (CST). Note that in certain units of the course, you will post rough drafts (sometimes called “discovery” drafts) earlier than Thursday. See the unit overviews for all due dates.

Unless you are instructed otherwise, essays are not accepted late unless warranted by extenuating circumstances (i.e., documented illness, funerals, or military deployment). Late discussion posts cannot earn maximum points. It is wise to review your work and personal schedule early on to let me know of any conflicts you anticipate. Please review the syllabus for specific policies on late work.

 

Research & Academic Honesty

Because this class focuses on academic research, you will be required to locate and evaluate academic sources through online databases available through the Park University McAfee Library. You can access these via Pirate Search at  [link: http://www.park.edu/library/] http://www.park.edu/library.

Make sure you understand the Park University standards for academic honesty expected in this—and indeed every—Park University course. This information is  in  the   [link: http://www.park.edu/catalog] University Catalog, on  the  syllabus for  this  course, and  in  the   [link: https://www.park.edu/about-park/student-conduct-code/] Park University Student Conduct Code. In EN106, we will be especially concerned with the academic honesty guidelines for citing all words and ideas that you have incorporated into your own writing from an outside source (i.e., library research, interviews, discussions with other students, etc.).

You are responsible for ensuring proper acknowledgement of the words and ideas you incorporate into your papers from your research using standard academic documentation guidelines. Most students will follow the Modern Language Association (MLA) documentation format. This documentation format is the designated professional standard for the humanities. Other disciplines use similar systems (APA for social sciences, Chicago/Turabian for business and management, CSE for the natural sciences, and so on). Although the particulars of the citation styles may differ, the underlying philosophy is the same: Any time a writer uses the words or ideas of others, that writer must include both an in-text and end-text acknowledgement. If you choose a format other than MLA to cite your sources this term, please inform your instructor which documentation guidelines you will follow this term, and why.

You  will  find information about  academic citation in  the  Easy Writer handbook required for this course, and online at the   [link: https://owl.english.purdue.edu/owl/] Purdue Online Writing Lab.
