# Unit 5: Reading

Required

Greene & Lidinsky, articles from Ch. 14-19, as assigned by your instructor.

Your instructor will assign three articles from Ch. 14-19 to read, discuss, and analyze. Check the announcements for the specific readings, which will form the basis of your Essay #4 assignment.

Lunsford, "Writing in a Variety of Disciplines and Genres" (Ch. 12) and "Language That Builds Common Ground" (Ch. 20).

This unit's reading from Easy Writer addresses strategies for writing in various genres and for using "language that builds common ground." This is particularly important when writing about controversial, complex subjects, as you will do this unit.

Recommended

Greene & Lidinsky, “From Identifying Issues to Forming Questions” (Ch. 5).

At this point in the course, through reading and writing assignments, peer review and class discussion, you have learned much about constructing an academic essay. Chapter 5 builds on this knowledge by looking closely at how academic writers identify issues and formulate questions. You will practice these skills in Essay #4 this week.
