# Unit 5: Overview

Entering Academic Conversations

Our focus this unit will be on craft a new, thesis-driven essay that responds to a conversation begun by authors from our textbook. Your instructor will assign three articles from Ch. 15-19 that will give you the basis to enter an academic conversation. You will consider the issues raised by the articles in discussion, and use strategies and examples from Chapter 5 of Greene & Lidinsky as inspiration to draft a working thesis and first draft of a new essay. 

Task List

Monday - Wednesday, read all assignments and Lecture material for the unit, including the Essay assignment.

By Thursday noon CST, make your initial post to the unit Discussion area.

Thursday – Sunday, interact with peers in each of the discussion.

By Sunday midnight CST, submit your final draft essay in the required file format (.doc, .docx, or .pdf). See the Essay assignment for instructions on formatting and submitting your work.

Unit Learning Outcomes

Maintain a controlling idea/thesis for a variety of academic genres (CLO #2).

Consider the rhetorical situations faced by academic writers to respond appropriately in both writing and research (CLO #4).

Explore and practice strategies for writing effective introductions.

Explore and practice strategies for developing coherent paragraphs.

Explore and practice strategies for writing effective conclusions.
