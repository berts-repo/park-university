Discussion Topic

This unit, you will once again enter a conversation. Your job in this discussion is to outline and define the conversation begun by the three assigned readings:

"How I Discovered the Truth About Poverty" by Barbara Ehrenreich (pgs. 482-485)

"from Mindset: The New Psychology of Success" by Carol Dweck (pgs. 594-605)

"The Rise of the Working Poor" by Robert B. Reich (pgs. 749-759).

In this discussion, let's read rhetorically to understand the central claims, supporting arguments, and persuasive strategies of the authors. 

In your initial post in this thread this unit, choose 1 of the assigned articles to analyze, using all of the questions below. Answer each questions in 2-4 sentences or so.

Read the brief introduction to the article. What is the context of this piece? Who is the author? When, where, why, and how was it initially published? Who was the original audience for this article? 

What seems to have motivated the writer to write this piece? Is there some current event, a problem, a question, or another article that seems to have inspired the article? How do you know?

What seems to be the overall message or point of this article? Where does the author state this point or main claim?

What evidence does the writer include to support their claims? What pieces of evidence do you find most persuasive?

How is the article structured? What strategies has the writer used for introduction and conclusions? Why might the writer have chosen these particular strategies? 

How does this article "speak" to the other two assigned articles? What do they have in common? What major differences can you identify? In other words, what is the "conversation" among the articles?

Read the brief introduction to the article. What is the context of this piece? Who is the author? When, where, why, and how was it initially published? Who was the original audience for this article? 

This article, written by Carol Dweck, and is intended for the general audience. The context is about how the people either have a fixed or growth mindset, and the ability to develop your intelligence, through a mindset. Dweck is a professor of psychology at Stanford and is a leading expert in motivation. Dweck writing is from her bestselling book "Mindset: The New Psychology of Success" (2006).

What seems to have motivated the writer to write this piece? Is there some current event, a problem, a question, or another article that seems to have inspired the article? How do you know?

In her opening paragraph, Dweck talks about when she was researching, early in her career. She studied children who looked at at complex problems with excitement, and devoted herself to figure out why these children viewed the challenge with such a positive attitude (595).

What seems to be the overall message or point of this article? Where does the author state this point or main claim?

Dweck overall message, which is spread-out through the entire article, is how one can learn anything if they have the right outlook. She makes example from the inventor of the IQ test, how intelligence is not something you're born with, but something you learn, a skill (596).

What evidence does the writer include to support their claims? What pieces of evidence do you find most persuasive?

The author writing as a whole is more persuasive then one particular paragraph or sentence. You have to look at all the limitations of thinking in a particular way.
