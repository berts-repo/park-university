# Unit 6: Overview

Making Evidence-Based Arguments

This unit, you will revise last unit's essay, to ensure that it is focused around evidence-based arguments. Our Reading assignment—Chapters 8 and 12 in Greene and Lidinsky—will jump-start this process by examining ideas for expanding your arguments and responding to the work of other writers. Finally, in Peer Review, you will read and develop suggestions for how your fellow writers can incorporate these appeals into their revised essays.

It is imperative to start the process of peer review as early in the unit as possible, so that you can receive (and give) feedback before the revised essay is due Sunday. For this reason, please note that your initial posts to the Peer Review are due on Wednesday midnight CST, and you must respond to one peer’s draft no later than Friday midnight CST.

Remember, I expect that in peer response you will offer thoughtful, careful and deliberate feedback to your classmates, and that at the same time you will learn what your classmates think of your work before you engage in deep revision. Although in Unit Seven we will explore the value of helping each other proofread our work, in this unit’s Peer Review we are working on what compositionists term “higher-order concerns”—focus, development, organization, and rhetorical strategies. 

While your instructor will not  respond to each post in  the peer review thread, your instructor will observe the discussion, noting repeated themes and issues that emerge from your review of each other’s work and offering general input and guidance. As usual, your instructor will spend the bulk of their time this unit assessing your essays from last unit, offering you constructive suggestions for how you might revise those essays for the Writing Portfolio. Look for these suggestions in the Gradebook later this unit.

Task List

Monday - Wednesday, read all assignments and lecture material for the unit, including the essay assignment. Note the requirement to locate scholarly, peer- reviewed research via the Park University Library databases.

By Wednesday, post your draft of this unit’s essay in the Peer Review area.

By Thursday noon CST, make your post to the unit Discussion area. Note: There is no requirement for additional posts in this unit’s Discussion area due to the expectation for dialogue in the Peer Review area.

By Friday, post a response to one peer’s essay in the Peer Review area. This will give you and your peers the weekend to incorporate each other’s readerly feedback.

By Sunday midnight CST, submit your final draft essay in the required file format (.doc, .docx, or .pdf). See the Essay content item for instructions on formatting and submitting your work.

Unit Learning Outcomes

Read and respond meaningfully to the writing of another student in the class.

Pose provocative questions that will stir issues the writer might consider for deep revision.

Transform your own essay through a process of deep revision supported by peer and instructor comments.

Apply strategies for developing academic arguments across the disciplines, including  conducting  research  and  incorporating  culturally  diverse perspectives (CLO #3).

Consider  the  rhetorical  situations  faced  by  academic  writers  to  respond appropriately in both writing and research (CLO #4).
