# Unit 2: Reading

Required

Greene & Lidinsky, “From Reading as a Writer to Writing as a Reader” (Ch. 2) and "From Writing Summaries and Paraphrases to Writing Yourself into Academic Conversations" (Ch. 3).

Chapters 2 and 3 are essential resources for completing your first essay assignment, due this unit. This essay assignment asks you to compose a summary and analysis of an article on education from Ch. 14 in our textbook. Ch. 2 will acquaint you with the processes of close reading—that is, active engagement in “conversation” with a text. Ch. 2 also includes a sample rhetorical analysis you can use to inspire your own essay assignment this unit. Ch. 3 reviews in more detail the “tools” of using close reading to write—direct quotation, paraphrase, and summary. You will practice these skills in the Discussion this unit.

Greene & Lidinsky, articles from Ch. 14, as assigned by your instructor in Announcements

Your instructor will assign at least two articles from Ch. 14 for you to read, discuss, and analyze. As you write your essay this unit, you will analyze one of the assigned articles. Check the Announcements for your assigned readings.

Lunsford, "Reading and Listening Analytically, Critically and Respectfully" (Ch. 7).

This chapter from Easy Writer explores simple tips for reading critically and summarizing what you read. The chapter also includes a sample rhetorical analysis.

Recommended

Lunsford, "A Writer's Choices" and "Exploring, Planning, and Drafting."

Lunsford's Easy Writer is a crucial handbook to helping you write. You should use this book as a reference tool throughout the term. This unit, Lunsford's short chapters on getting started reading and writing will be useful for getting started with the essay assignment. If you are stuck, start here!
