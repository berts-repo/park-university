# Introductions

Directions

Before you start the course, take a few moments to become acquainted with the Discussion tool by posting a short introduction. Please abide by the Netiquette guide below.

Netiquette

Netiquette is a set of guidelines for how individuals communicate over the Internet. The important concept here is that students respect one another.

Review   [link: https://www.park.edu/life-park/clubs-organizations/online-student-help-resources/netiquette/] Park's Netiquette resource.

Suggested Questions

Here are some questions that might help you determine what you want to share in your discussion.

What goals do you want to accomplish in this course?

Why are you taking this course?

What are your educational and career goals?

What do you expect to learn in this course about programming?

Do you have any work or educational experiences in programming?

Feel free to only share what you feel comfortable with. Some students don't feel comfortable sharing information about their personal life, such as their family. One way to get to know your classmates is by sharing something that you like to do, such as an activity, sport, hobby, interest, favorite web site, book, movie, vacation spot, author, music, or television show. You can share information about your pets or a talent that you might have. Please show courtesy and respect as you read and reply to each other's posts.

Please post your introductory information below and then return later, or tomorrow, to read and respond to other participants' introductions.
