Prompt 1: How a parent and child can use a teeter-totter even though the parent weighs much more than the child.

Torque is a measure of the force that causes the teeter-totter to move up and down on it's pivot point. When a child and mother sit on opposite ends of the pivot point they are applying toque to the teeter-totter which is proportional to weight, so twice as much weight, half the distance from the pivot-point would cause the sum of forces to balance, and the teeter-totter will balance.

Prompt 2: How a pipe (cheater bar) can be used with wrench to loosen a tight nut.

Torque is proportional to the force applied to it, and the distance from the axis to the rotation force, so extending the length of the wrench allows the user to apply more fort. The longer the wrench, the more leverage, which translates to more torque applied to the nut.
