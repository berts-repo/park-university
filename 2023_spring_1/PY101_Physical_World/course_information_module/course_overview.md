Course Details

Title:  Physical World

Course Description: An overview of physics concepts in our world accessible to student with little or no physics background. The class will focus primarily on classical physics and selected topics on electricity and magnetism.

Credit Hours: 3:0:3

Prerequisite Courses: None

Core Learning Outcomes

At the conclusion of the course, learners will be able to:

Examine and explain physics concepts in writing and through calculation.

Multiple strategies for examining and describing physical phenomena will be used.

Demonstrate use of Excel to graph data and then explain the behavior demonstrated through discussion and writing.

Examination of alternative systems like Metric versus Imperial units and differences in power grid voltages.

Recognize experiments from scientists throughout history and around the world and see how those explorations challenged and pushed our understanding further.

Evaluate and solve class assignments involving everyday objects (balls, cars, etc.) and extraordinary systems (satellites, rockets, etc.)

Exams (Midterm 150, Final 200) - In Module 4 and 8 you will have a comprehensive exam. The exam is due by 11:59 p.m. Sunday CT (Central Time).
