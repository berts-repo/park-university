Hello Class,

To get a plane to start moving and take off, the first force I think of is the thrust from the engines. The plane will not start moving until there is a positive sum-of-forces, relative to the axis, for example the jet thrusters are pushing one direction and friction from the ground is pushing the opposite, until there is more thrust than friction (inertia) then the plane will not start moving.

The next forces are in regards to the plane taking off and aerodynamics, which is its own physics field, and probably contains more "forces" than I assume. So, the next force that I do assume would be the weight of the plane, which is the product of the constant gravity and the mass. Then I think about forces like air resistance, which is pushing in a x-negative direction, and also lift, pushing up, in a y-positive direction. The force of lift would also be affecting the weight of the plane, in regards to the sum-of-forces, which makes sense. I am not sure but air resistance might be considered friction; I haven't read chapter 5 yet.

When the plane is at a constant flight, the forces would be the same, but the sum-of-forces would balance out in regards to the y-axis, which ties back to the force of lift and weight.
