yes even if two objects have different masses they can still have the same momentum and kinetic energy. The formula for momentum (p) is mass times velocity, and the formula for kinetic energy is 1/2 * mass * velocity squared: 

momentum (p) = m * v

kinetic energy (k) = 1/2 * m * v^2
