 Late Assignment Policy

Quizzes are due when specified. Late work is not accepted.

Discussion posts may be made past the initial Wednesday deadline with a deduction for lateness. No discussion posting will be allowed after the final Sunday deadline since no one else will be discussing the topic.

Homework and labs may be turned in late with a 20% penalty per day. Note that items turned in more than 4 days late will receive a 0.

Discussions

There is one discussion question in each unit. You must make an initial post by Wednesday 11:59pm Central Time in each unit for each question. Unless otherwise indicated, each post must adhere to the following guidelines:

Use your own words, no plagiarizing. You may copy and paste a sentence or two from another site (with quotes and appropriate attribution) to support your work, but in general these posts must be your own writing.

Write at least 100-150 words.

Provide all specified references (source and URL). URLs should be added with the link button in the editor so that fellow students can easily click on your references.

Be substantial so that your post can generate additional thoughts, discussions, concepts, etc.

Use correct spelling and grammar. The Canvas environment checks your spelling for you and it is courtesy to your fellow students to correct the mistakes it finds. Grammar doesn’t have to be perfect, but your posts should look like you have put some thought and care into them and not just typed a stream of consciousness as fast as you could.

Feel free to bring in your own personal experience. As long as it is relevant, a concrete example from your own experience, on which you can write with confidence, is interesting and provocative to the rest of the students in the class.

Review the responses to the course discussion topics by your classmates and provide feedback, using your own words, to at least 2 different postings in each discussion topic. Each response must be substantial (50 words or more) meaning that your responses must generate additional thoughts, discussion, concepts, etc. Post your responses by Sunday at 11:59pm Central Time.

Final Exam - Core Assessment

This exam is closed book and closed Internet. However, you may bring one small sheet of notes, (both sides) on a 5x7" index card or half sheet (5.5 x 8.5") of standard letter sized paper. 

The exam is entirely short answer. Questions will be displayed one at a time, but you will be able to move back and forth and may answer them in any order.

You have 2 hours and may only take the exam once.
