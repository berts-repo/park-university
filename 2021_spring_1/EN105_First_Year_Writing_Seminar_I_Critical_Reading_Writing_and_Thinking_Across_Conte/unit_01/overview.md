# Unit 1: Overview

Reading Rhetorically

Introduction

Welcome to the first unit of EN105! In this unit, you will begin to become acquainted with the course design, get to know your classmates, and begin exploring the basic concepts of rhetoric, or what we will call the art of using language to persuade. 

In this unit, you will consider how writer, audience, and purpose govern what you read and write. You will use processes of brainstorming and looping to invent ideas for your first major writing assignment. You will also read and discuss learning narratives from several different writers. By the end of the unit, you will compose your own learning narrative, which will describe an experience from your own life during which you learned something important.

This unit, like all units in this class, will ask you to do quite a bit of reading and writing. Your hard work and close attention this unit will pay off later in the course.

Unit Learning Outcomes

At the conclusion of this unit, you will be able to:

 Describe the rhetorical triangle and its implications for college readers and writers (CLO 3, 4)

 Use brainstorming and looping to invent ideas for writing tasks (CLO 1, 2)

 Define the genre of the learning narrative (CLO 4)

 Develop a controlling idea/thesis for a personal narrative essay (CLO 2)
