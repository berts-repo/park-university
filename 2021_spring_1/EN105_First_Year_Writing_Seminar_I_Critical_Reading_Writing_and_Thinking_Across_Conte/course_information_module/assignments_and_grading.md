Assignment Descriptions

Each assignment for this class will have detailed instructions, including a grading rubric that your instructor will use to evaluate your work. These descriptions will provide you a general overview of the assignments you will complete.

Quizzes

Most units, you will take a brief, self-graded quiz to gauge your understanding of the reading assignments and mini-lectures for that unit. You should take each quiz before participating in discussion or completing the major writing assignment for that unit. Quizzes are self-graded and contribute to your final grade in the class. 

Each reading quiz will contain five multiple choice questions. Each question has just one correct answer. These questions will gauge your understanding of class readings and mini-lectures. Your instructor will consider your performance on quizzes when leading discussions and making other instructions. 

All quizzes have a time limit of 5 minutes. You may take each quiz twice. Only your highest score will be recorded. If you receive a low grade on your first quiz attempt, you should try to re-read the class readings and consult your notes and annotations before taking the quiz a second time.

Discussions

Most units, you will take part in class discussion. Why discussions? Because pedagogical research shows that discussions are one of the best ways to learn. Discussions allow you to try out new concepts, test new ideas, and debate important questions. Your work in discussions will lead to some of your most important learning this term. All discussions will take place in an asynchronous, threaded discussion format. You will be asked to make an initial post that responds to a question or prompt, and then engage in follow-up discussion. Specific details for each discussion's requirements are contained within the discussion prompt. Initial posts to each discussion are due each Wednesday by 11:59 pm Central time. Follow-up discussion is due each Sunday by 11:59 pm Central time -- although most discussions should take place earlier in the unit.

Most discussions will be graded with the following rubric.

Peer Reviews

In units 4, 6, and 7, you will engage in peer review of rough drafts of your major writing assignments. What that means is that you will trade drafts with one or two classmates and provide each other directed constructive feedback. Each peer review will be related to that unit's major concepts, which means that the instructions for each peer review will vary. In general, peer reviews will ask you to post a draft early in the unit (usually by Tuesday) and provide a written response to assigned classmates, using a question guide. Specific instructions are provided in each peer review assignment.

Your peer reviews will be graded with the following rubric.

 Writing Portfolio

Your major goal this term is to create a portfolio of your best writing from the class. This Writing Portfolio will be turned in at the end of Unit 7, and represents the largest percentage of your grade in the course.

To complete the Writing Portfolio, you will choose your two best major writing assignments from this class and revise them further, using feedback from your instructor and your classmates to guide your work. (Most likely, these pieces will be the same pieces that you revised in your Revision Projects.) You will also compile artifacts of your writing process, to help illustrate how you went about creating your writing assignments. Finally, you will compose a detailed reflection that describes your writing process and identifies specific lessons about writing you learned this term.

Your Writing Portfolio serves as the Core Assessment of your learning for English 105. That means two things: first, that the Writing Portfolio should show off everything you've learned this semester, demonstrating how you have achieved the Core Learning Outcomes for EN105. Second, your performance on the Writing Portfolio may be analyzed by the faculty in Department of English to make decisions about curriculum, learning outcomes, student success, and adjustments to our teaching strategies.
