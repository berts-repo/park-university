# Unit 3: Overview

Writing To Explain

Introduction

Welcome to the third unit of EN 105! In this unit, you will work with your classmates and instructor to learn about one of the most common types of writing you will do in your academic and personal life: writing to explain a concept, idea, or process.

To do so, you will first analyze several sample concept explanations from the web. You will work on your own and with classmates to understand effective rhetorical strategies for how to explain complex concepts. (As you read, pay close attention to how authors use examples, details, statistics, and analogies in order to explain abstract ideas.) You will also spend time brainstorming concepts that you can explain to your classmates in this assignment.

Ultimately, you will come away from Unit 3 with a better sense of how to organize a piece of informational writing -- which is a very common type of writing you will encounter at school and at work.

 

Unit Learning Outcomes

At the conclusion of this unit, you will be able to:

Define the writing genre of an explanation (CLO 5)

Identify common methods of using sources in writing (CLO 4, 5)

Invent a focus for an expository piece of writing (CLO 2)

Use a they say / I say process to develop a piece of writing (CLO 1, 2, 3)

Use claims and evidence as a rhetorical strategy for academic writing (CLO 3)
