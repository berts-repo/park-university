Most Americans take the humble hamburger for granted.  It has a long and interesting history.  The burger originated in Russia when a group of Mongolian Tartars shredded some raw meat scraps and ate them.  They gave this new delicacy to some German sailors who had sailed into the Baltic ports.  After leaving  the sailors introduced the raw meat mixture to the people of Hamburg, Germany.  Because these people did not like raw meat they broiled the meat patties.

In 1900, Louis Lassen served the recipe to his lunch wagon customers. By 1904 he was inserting the meat patty between two buns. The crowds at the St. Louis fair started adding condiments such as ketchup, mustard and relish. The hamburger quickly became a familiar American dish.
