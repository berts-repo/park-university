## Instructions

In this unit's Writer's Journal, practice using either the freewriting and looping technique from the Unit 1 Writer's Journal, or the self-interview technique from the Unit 2 Writer's Journal, in order to develop ideas for this unit's major writing assignment. You may re-use any element of this journal in your writing assignment this unit.

Option 1: Freewriting & Looping

First, write (typed or handwritten) for 5-8 minutes on the following prompt: A commonly misunderstood concept is _______________.  Keep your pen moving or your fingers typing -- try not to stop for the entire time. We will call this your freewrite.

Then, read back through your freewrite. Circle, underline, or highlight any important words, ideas, or phrasesyou might be able to explore further. 

Finally, do another 5-8 minute freewrite, in which you use your circled/underlined/highlighted words as your prompt. Try to dig more deeply into these ideas in your first loop.

Now, create another loop. Read through your first loop and circle, underline, or highlight any important words, ideas, or phrases. Write another 5-8 minute freewrite, in which you use these phrases as your prompt.

Option 2: Self-Interview

On a sheet of paper or in a typed document, list as many concepts that you know a great deal about.  Define "concept" as broadly and as narrowly as you want. Answer all of the following questions that apply:

What concepts have I studied in school?

What concepts do I use at work?

What concepts have I learned from my family?

What concepts have I learn from friends?

What concepts have I developed in my personal, for instance, through hobbies or extracurricular activities?

What concepts do I know about from the world of:

philosophy, literature, art?

business management?

psychology, sociology?

government and law?

criminal justice, public safety, security?

natural sciences, biology, chemistry, physics, environmental science?

nutrition, public health, social work?

physical sciences, math, engineering?

After you have listed all the concepts you know a great deal about, select one. Then, dig deeper into this concept by answering all of the following questions:

How would I define this concept?

What elements of this concept are my classmates likely to know about?

What elements of this concept are my classmates probably less likely to know about?

What background information will my classmates need in order to understand this concept?

What are some common misconceptions about this concept?

What examples provide a good illustration of this concept?

What questions do I have about this concept?

What research will I need to conduct to fully explain this concept to classmates?

What type of sources will my readers find credible?

After you have written your reflection, upload them to this assignment. You may upload a photograph of handwritten notes if you would like, or upload a typed document.either as a typed document file. Please upload either a .doc, .docx, .jpg, .gif, or .pdf file. Use your journal as a starting place for this unit's major writing assignment.
