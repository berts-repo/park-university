# Unit 3: Critical Reading Strategies

Understanding Explanations

Introduction

Welcome to this unit's mini-lecture on critical reading strategies. This mini-lecture is designed to help you read explanatory writing.

What Does It Mean To Explain?

You might find the title of this lecture a little ironic. After all, isn't an explanation designed to help you understand something? Why would you need to read an explanation actively and critically in order to understand?

As you will see in your web readings this unit, explanations can take many forms, and can address many types of subjects. Some of the concepts you will read about this unit are extremely complex and technical; others are more straightforward. However, you will notice that each explanation uses a clear, logical structure; effective and credible evidence; and, usually, plenty of examples to help you understand. 

Reading Strategies for Concept Explanations

Here are several questions you can ask when reading a concept explanation:

What is the concept being explained in this piece of writing?

What do I already know about this concept? How does my prior knowledge compare to how the author describes the concept?

What is new to me about this concept?

What examples are provided to help me understand this concept? Do the examples truly illustrate the concept?

What specialized vocabulary is used in this explanation? Do I know what each of these terms means? (If not, look them up!)

What sources are cited to further explain this concept?

What questions do I have about this concept? Have these questions been answered already, or do I need more clarification?

Use this questions as guidance when you read the example concept explanations this unit.
