# Unit 5: Overview

Making Claims & Using Evidence

Introduction

Congratulations on completing the first half of EN105! You have read, discussed, written, and revised; now, in the second half of the course, you will apply your critical reading strategies, writing processes, and burgeoning rhetorical knowledge to new writing tasks.

In this unit, you will work with your classmates and instructor to enter a controversial conversation about how technology affects learning and understanding, and you will deepen your understanding of how to use evidence to make effective arguments. Ultimately, you will answer the question: Is Google making us stupid?

Begin by reading Nicholas Carr's recent article "Is Google Making Us Stupid?" Although the article was published as recently as 2008, it has already become something of a classic, galvanizing a conversation about the effects of the Internet on memory, concentration, knowledge, and even individuality. Carr's article is dense with allusions and somewhat tricky in its rhetorical strategies. So, you will spend much of your time in this unit's discussion working with your classmates to analyze Carr's argument and to develop your own thoughts about the effects of the Internet on you. This discussion will lead into the final "major writing assignment" of the course.

Unit Learning Outcomes

At the conclusion of this unit, you will be able to:

Use the genre of argument to enter into critical conversations (CLO 4, 5)

Use source quotations effectively in academic writing (CLO 1, 4, 5)

Use diverse rhetorical strategies for developing a focus in argumentative writing (CLO 2)

Establish a sense of credibility in academic writing (CLO 3, 4)
