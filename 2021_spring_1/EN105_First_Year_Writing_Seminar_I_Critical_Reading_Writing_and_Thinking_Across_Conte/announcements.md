## Reminders
*Posted: 2021-01-13T21:01:39Z*

Hello, All,

I have enjoyed learning more about you. Thank you for filling out the information sheet.

In going over the other work you have submitted, I have noticed a couple of trends. I am letting you know now so that you can be aware of the following going forward:

1) Not everyone is reading the assignment directions thoroughly and following them accordingly. Read the directions for each assignment. Then reread the directions. If you have any questions, please ask.

2) In academic writing, the use of first and second person is to be avoided unless the assignment calls for you to write about yourself.

First person = I, we, me, us, my, our, mine, ours

Second person = you, yours

Third person: he, she, it, someone, anyone, nobody, etc.

In academic writing, it is not appropriate to address the reader directly as "you." Keep in mind that there are different genres of writing. When you are composing a brochure or directions for using a product correctly, then using "you" is perfectly fine. In academic writing, you are presenting ideas. State the ideas and stick to third person.

EXAMPLE: Instead of "You should avoid college debt," the better way to state this idea would be "Students should avoid student debt." This keeps the idea in third person and is written in an academic style.

Finally, everyone has some aspect of writing that needs improvement. EVERYONE. Even Stephen King. Stephen King pays editors a lot of money to fix his work. Luckily, you can take advantage of a free resource by working with the Writing Center. You are entitled to unlimited assistance to help make your work the best it can be before you submit it.

Please don't wait to take advantage of this free resource. Make an appointment with the Writing Center. Start here:  [link: https://www.park.edu/academics/academic-success-center/writing-resources/] https://www.park.edu/academics/academic-success-center/writing-resources/
