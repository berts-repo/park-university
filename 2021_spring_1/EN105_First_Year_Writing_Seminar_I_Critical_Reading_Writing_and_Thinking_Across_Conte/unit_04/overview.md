# Unit 4: Overview

Understanding Rhetorical Appeals

Introduction

Welcome to the fourth unit of EN105! In this unit, you will return to one of your first three major writing assignments and perform a substantial revision, using the responses of your classmates and the rhetorical concepts of ethos, pathos, and logos as guides to revision.

This unit will work slightly differently than our first three units. For one thing, so that you can give and receive good peer review responses, you will need to trade your drafts with an assigned peer review group. For this activity to work, it is imperative that you post your draft for your peer review group as soon as possible. Specific instructions can be found in this unit's Peer Review assignment. After giving and receiving peer reviews, you will revise your work, taking your classmates' responses as guides.

You will also use the three appeals as guides to revision. You will learn about these concepts in mini-lectures and in several web readings.

Your work in this unit will prepare you to do an excellent job on the course portfolio, which is the ultimate goal of the course. Good luck, and happy revising!

Unit Learning Outcomes

At the conclusion of this unit, you will be able to:

Define and identify the three rhetorical appeals: ethos, pathos, and logos (CLO 3, 4)

Revise your writing to incorporate the rhetorical strategies of the appeals (CLO 1, 3, 4)

Use peer review as a writing process (CLO 1)
