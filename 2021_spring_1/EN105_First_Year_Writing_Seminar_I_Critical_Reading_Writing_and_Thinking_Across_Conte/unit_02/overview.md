# Unit 2: Overview

Language & Community

Introduction

Welcome to the second unit of EN105! In the first unit, you got acquainted with your classmates, became familiar with the course design, learned about the basic concepts of rhetoric (including audience, writer, and purpose), and composed a personal narrative essay that explored an important learning experience.

In this unit, you will add to your understanding of rhetoric by learning about the concept of genre. You will use this concept, along with your previous rhetorical knowledge, to write about the language used by a community you belong to. Working toward a goal of composing a descriptive essay aimed at your classmates, you will read mini-lectures and web articles, participate in class discussion, and complete a writer's journal activity. 

This unit, like all units in this class, will ask you to do quite a bit of reading and writing. Your hard work and close attention this unit will pay off later in the course.

 

Unit Learning Outcomes

At the conclusion of this unit, you will be able to:

Define the rhetorical concept of genre and its implications for writers and readers (CLO 4)

Identify common genres of writing that you will encounter in college and on the internet (CLO 3, 4)

Use a self-interview process to invent and develop subjects for writing assignments (CLO 1, 2, 3)

Invent a focus for an essay (CLO 2)

Use description and analysis as a rhetorical strategy for academic writing (CLO 4)
