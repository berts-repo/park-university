# Unit 2: Writing Processes

Self-Interview

Introduction

Welcome to this unit's mini-lecture on writing processes. This mini-lecture is designed to introduce you to writing processes that will help you invent subjects and develop ideas for your major writing assignments. Each unit, you will read about a different writing process, and then practice that process in your unit Writer's Journal assignment. This unit, you will focus on using a self-interview to approach a writing assignment.

Interviewing Your Self

Many college students report that the most difficult aspect of writing is finding a way to develop an initial idea. Selecting a topic comes naturally to many student writers, but developing that topic beyond the general or the obvious can be tough.

One process you can use to develop your ideas is to interview yourself about your subject. Once you've selected a topic, use the following questions to find your "way in" to writing about your subject:

What do I know about this subject?

Where did I first learn about this subject?

What relevant experiences have I had with this subject?

What is important for other people to know about this subject?

What have I read, viewed, or listened to that is relevant to this subject?

What are the various points of view on this subject?

What don't I know yet about this subject?

Where can I learn more about this subject?

Your goal in this unit's major writing assignment is to introduce your classmates to a "language community" that you belong to. To do so, you can use the "self-interview" technique to not only select a language community, but also to come up with precise aspects of your community to discuss. You will do so in this unit's Writer's Journal assignment.
