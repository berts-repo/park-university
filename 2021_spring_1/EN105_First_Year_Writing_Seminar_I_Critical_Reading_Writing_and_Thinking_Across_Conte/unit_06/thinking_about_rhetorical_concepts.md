# Unit 6: Thinking About Rhetorical Concepts

More With Kairos

Welcome to this unit's mini-lecture on rhetorical concepts. This unit, you will dig deeper into the rhetorical concept of kairos.

"The Right Thing at the Right Time"

As you probably concluded from your assigned readings and the mini-lecture on critical reading strategies, kairos is a tricky concept. As the reading from Writing Commons notes, it can be tough to find good examples of kairos, because kairos depends so heavily on time, place, and context.

One way to learn about kairos, however, is to examine newspaper articles. Newspapers are highly kairotic, because their entire purpose is to report and comment on current events. Most newspaper articles, web and print, begin with a dateline that describes the date the article was written and the location from which the article was reported. Datelines often look something like this:

PARKVILLE, Mo., May 28 -- 

According to The Associated Press Stylebook, which provides guidelines for many journalistic organizations, a dateline implies that the newspaper "obtained the basic information for the story in the datelined city" (68). So, a dateline not only provides the date of the article, but also suggests where the article was composed, or at least reported from. Why does this matter? Because careful readers know that a story that was reported firsthand is probably more reliable than a story that was written secondhand.

What's more, usually the first few paragraphs of a newspaper article describes the who, what, where, when, why, and how of the subject. Answering these "Five Ws and One H" shows readers why the subject matters now.

Likewise, newspaper editorials, which are presented as the "voice of the newspaper," and columns, which are opinion pieces credited to one writer alone, usually refer to current events in their openings. Once again, a sense of kairos is apparent: because the subject of the editorial emerges out of current events, the editorial is automatically timely.

While the example of a newspaper is a little simplistic for such a complex concept, it can be a helpful way of understanding the basics of kairos.
