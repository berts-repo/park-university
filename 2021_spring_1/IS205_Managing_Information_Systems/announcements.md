## Unit 5/6/7 - Sample Case Study
*Posted: 2021-02-08T06:00:01Z*

In the past, I've had questions from students about the case study, and what the final product will look like.
For your review, below is an example of a final submission.  Your submission will of course be different (since the case is different),
but the format should be like the one shown below.  

Sample Solved Case Study

"The Baking Business: Bits, Bytes and Chips"

Issue:

How can a very large, very flat organization utilize both new technology and a personal touch to keep the original traditions of a small cookie company alive?

Discussion:

Debbi Fields and her husband Randy are the owners of Mrs. Fields Cookies, Inc. They have owned this enterprise since the day Randy bet Debbi she couldn’t sell $50 dollars of cookies in one day. Now the company has approximately 600 stores in 5 different countries. In 1987, the Fields expanded their operation by purchasing the La Petite Boulangerie bakery chain from Pepsico. This organization is no a very large, and geographically dispersed corporation.

From the beginning, the Fields had two conflicting goals. They wanted very tight controls on each store to ensure that they would remain profitable. This has been Randy Fields primary focus of attention for the corporation. Equally important, they wanted to keep a small store atmosphere at each location and make available to each of these stores Debbi Fields marketing knowledge and expertise.

Each of the Mrs. Fields Cookie locations has a personal computer, which is linked to the centralized mini-computer at the home office in Park City, Utah. Information from each of these stores is condensed at the central site daily, and distributed to the four reporting levels of the management team. The sales results are shown with a straight ranking, letting each team member know where they stand in relationship to the others. Along with the usual financial and administrative systems on the mini-computer, the company has very good reporting and control systems.

The big problem for this company was how to push Debbi Fields experience to the field level. The company developed an expert system that is available in each store’s personal computer. This system contains Debbi’s expert recommendations. This allows a store to continually make adjustments to market conditions. The store managers are still able to try out their own ideas. New managers find the system extremely beneficial, because it mentors them in successful marketing practices.

Finally, the personality of Debbi Fields is very important to this company. Besides, the expert system, Debbi wanted meaningful contact with the store managers. She wants to be their coach. She sees her duty as training them – so they can do a proper job, monitoring their successes and failures, and fostering a positive atmosphere for success. She travels widely, personally visiting as many of her stores as possible. She talks with each of the managers to obtain their views and explains her own. She tests their assumptions and allows them to test hers.

Findings:

What is the business growth goal? Keep the organization flat and responsible. Also, keep the original Debbi Fields touch in place at each of the local stores.

What was the business strategy? Tight control to track operations. Embed procedures and standardize operations. Make marketing changes as soon as the market dictates. Fact based decisions are not only made with sales figures.

What leadership strategies were employed? A presence leadership style by Debbi Fields. Making site visits to the stores to keep a personal touch. Performance goals and measurement tracking.

What were the information system functions? Provide a expert system at each location, pull daily sales figures, furnish management with effective reports. Manage complexity and provide facts about performance

What tools or techniques were used? Flat organization, expert system for each store, phone mail, electronic mail. There were two types of expert systems that were used: process and content.

Conclusion:

This case demonstrates how to use technology to complement leadership skills and to implement business strategies. Computer Automation were tools, or the means to a specific goal.

Randy Fields operates much like an administrative vice-president. He is in the background, always making sure that the I’s are dotted and the T’s are crossed. He is important to the company because he supplies the foundation upon which the company operates.

Debbi Fields provides the spark that is needed by a company to become great. She knows she must coach her employees – they are too widely spread to do otherwise. Her high energy level is contagious and provides the employees the incentive needed to continually strive for excellence. The Mrs. Fields – in – a –box gives the local stores a quick and easy way to "contact" Debbi and use her methods in the marketplace.

In short, this company demonstrates a remarkable combination of tight controls, positivism through a coaching leadership style, and constant refinement of details. All three make a winning combination.

Ideas for further concern:

What will happen when Debbi Fields gets busy and can’t travel as much?

Could the Mrs. Fields Cookie Company expand any further? Do they rely on Debbi’s leadership style too much?

Why is it important for an organization to have clear cut goals and methods in a geographically widespread, large organization?

References:

Omitted by the instructor - but a minimum of three references are required to be documented for your submission !

Case Study Template (100 Points)

Format (10 points): Elements of case format include:

At least one and one-half pages in length (single spaced), not to exceed 3 pages in length

Proper English, grammar, and syntax must be used

One inch margins

Issue (10 points): One or two paragraphs that summarizes the key issue of the case study.

Discussion (20 points): Three to six paragraphs that give a more detailed summary of the case study, including your analysis and insight.

Findings (6 points each for a total of 30 points): The elements of your submission must include -- the Letter of the finding you are addressing (A, B, C, etc.), one or two paragraphs documenting your findings, and analysis or insight to validate your position. Each of the following items must be addressed:

A: What was the business goal?

B: What was the business strategy?

C: What leadership strategies were employed?

D: What were the Information Systems functions?

E: What tools or techniques were used?

Conclusion (10 points): Two to four paragraphs that summarize your insight into this particular case study, and what conclusions you can draw from it -- parallels, textbook insights, personal insights, etc.

Ideas for Further Concern (10 points): Ask three to six questions about the case that should be addressed to develop a better understanding of this situation. You only have to formulate the questions, not answer them.

References- at least 3 references from the periodicals, textbooks (our textbook is not allowed as a reference for this purpose), speeches, Internet searches. These server the same purpose as in a term paper -- to provide independent validation of the position you have taken in the paper.
