11-15 What ethical and social issues are raised by self-driving car technology?

Self driving cars could predominantly take over the road. With the increase and capabilities of self driving artificial intelligence, the progress will only progress. I see some of the large issues accruing around pointing the finger for self driving vehicles. It will be easy for people to blame an entire company for one accident even though there are wrecks every day. But the technology does still have a long way to go. The case study listed some of the reasons, being bad weather, bad climates, road constructions and large objects like semi trucks. 

In 2015 a couple of white hat hackers developed a piece of software that could take control of a 2014 Grand Cherokee. The software could remotely control the transmission, breaks, and steering along with most he features of the car. They were doing things like turning off the car, slowing it down and taking control of the steering while the operator could not stop these actions. They also claim that there are many more cars that they would be able to do this to.  [link: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MK0SrxBC1xs&ab_channel=WIRED] (Greenberg) With the rise in completely self automated cars this will only raise concern as the cars would be able to take them to the destinations of the hackers choice.

The rise with self driving cars would also put a lot of jobs at risk, while only create a few highly educated positions in return. If you were to get on a career finding site you would realize that 30% of the jobs listed would be driving jobs. On top of taxi services stated in the case study, you would have delivery and transportation jobs at risk as well.  And if these cars did reach a safer point for drivers, which is a good thing, there would also be all the auto body shops, emergency response teams and many other careers at a decrease as well.
