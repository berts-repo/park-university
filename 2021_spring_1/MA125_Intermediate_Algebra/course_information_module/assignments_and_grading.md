 Late Submission Policy

It is unfair to other students to allow some individuals to submit assignments after the scheduled due date. Therefore, all assignments are expected to be completed by set deadlines. An exception to this rule is a 24 hour extension provided only for discussion postings; using it will mean you will be assessed with a 50% penalty on earned points for the assignment. 

The only other considerations for extending assignment deadlines are limited to the following valid list of emergency reasons. Please note even these reasons are only acceptable at the discretion of your instructor and any deadline extensions will be given for the minimal time required.

A medical emergency or a serious acute illness for yourself or an immediate family member: all medical emergencies and illnesses must be verified by a note on letterhead by an M.D., D.O., P.A., or R.N. Notes from other health professionals (e.g., Ph.D., MSW, D.C., Physical Therapist) will not be accepted because their professional functions rarely involve medical emergencies or acute illnesses.

An accident or police emergency: an accident report or note on letterhead from an appropriate law enforcement officer will be required.

Unforeseen jury or witness duty: a note on letterhead from a judge or attorney stating you had no advance notice of the duty will be required.

Unforeseen military deployment or activation: a note on official letterhead from your commanding officer stating you had no advance notice of deployment or activation will be required.

Funerals for immediate family member (e.g., parents, siblings, grandparents, aunts/uncles, first cousins): a copy of the obituary or a note from a minister or funeral director will be required.

Assignments

Discussions

Read through the answer postings and responses already posted by classmates. Choose a discussion prompt not previously answered. (Once a discussion prompt has been addressed it is no longer eligible for you to answer, and will not earn you any points should you post an answer to it.)

When you choose a discussion prompt, you must answer it at that time. Discussion prompts cannot be reserved for answering at a later time. Should two students work simultaneously on the same discussion prompt, and post answers within minutes of each other, the second answer posted can be considered as that student's response posting. Another discussion prompt will need to be chosen and answered by that student in order to earn answer points.

Put the discussion prompt number at the very beginning of the FIRST LINE of your answer post. Type (or copy and paste) the discussion prompt as the first line(s) of your answer post. Begin typing your answer after the discussion prompt.

Example:

1. What is the name of this course? (Not a real discussion prompt...just an example!) 

The name of this course is Intermediate Algebra.

You may copy and paste your discussion prompt from the list. If you do, make sure you make adjustments for any symbols that do not paste as seen in the discussion prompt. (x2 – 1 from the list will paste as x2 -1, so it must be reentered use the "Insert Math Equation" tool in the Canvas thread toolbar.)

Not posting the discussion prompt number in the FIRST LINE, or not typing the discussion prompt as the first line of your answer posting, will cost you a point off of your answer score.

You will not earn points for postings that contain quotes without proper punctuation and source citing.  Repeated plagiarism will result in course failure, and a plagiarism incident report on your academic record.  Remember this is a serious offense with possible administrative expulsion as the ultimate consequence.  If you are in doubt, ask for assistance.

Response Posting Guidelines

All respectful responses are welcome to the Discussion thread, but in order to earn the response posting points you must respond to one of your classmate's answers with a thoughtful, math concept focused posting meeting the criteria described below. (Only one response posting is eligible for earning points per unit, but you may respond to as many postings as you like.)

In order to earn the points your response must have at least two complete concept focused sentences. Show that you understand the concept by expanding the thoughts given in the answer posting by adding more information. Rewording the thoughts given, or give an example are also ways to show your understanding or enhance the discussion of the concept. If you do not understand the concept, ask discussion prompts specifically about the concept discussed in the discussion prompt and answer. Make sure your discussion prompts are related only to the discussion prompt and answer you are responding to.

Please respond to incorrect or incomplete answers as well as the correct ones, tactfully completing the answer or presenting what you believe to be the correct answer.

Before you praise and agree with another's answer, you need to verify that it is correct. If the answer is incorrect and you are agreeing, your points will decrease. "I agree" or "Good answer" type sentences are not included as part of the two sentence requirement. Discussing your personal understanding of the concept is not part of the sentence count either. Also not included are sentences of appreciation for a page or website quoted in the answer. You may state that you agree or disagree, but you must go on to state why. If you merely write that the answer is good because it is correct, or because it helped you to understand the concept, it will not be considered for earning points.

Remember all thread postings should be proofread and the spellchecker tool used before posting. All college level writing, even thread postings, should be grammatically correct.

Your answer post must be posted by 11:59 p.m. Thursday (CT).

Your response posting must be posted by 11:59 p.m. Sunday (CT).
