## Please Register AND Schedule With ProctorU
*Posted: 2021-02-08T06:00:01Z*

Class,

This is Mr. [INSTRUCTOR], your math instructor.

This particular course requires remote virtual proctoring of the final exam for online students only (not all Park courses require proctoring - check with your instructor in your other courses for requirements). Final exams are taken online under the virtual supervision of ProctorU during a window of time set in the course modules. 

IMPORTANT: You need BOTH to register with ProctorU AND to schedule each exam with ProctorU at least 72 hours ahead of time. 

To register and schedule an exam with ProctorU, click on the link from the left menu in our classroom and follow the instructions.

NOTE:  Please check the "name" of the exam carefully when you schedule with ProctorU. Please be sure the “name” of your exam indicates:

 (1) the correct course, 

(2) the correct term, and 

(3) the correct instructor ([INSTRUCTOR]).

To take exams under ProctorU’s virtual supervision, you must have access to a computer with a video camera, a microphone, a good internet connection, and a reflective device e.g., a mirror, and be located in a quiet room where you can take the exam(s) in privacy. Interruptions in the internet connection or entry of other persons into the room will be reported by ProctorU and investigated to ensure the academic integrity of the exam.

It is up to each student to sign-up online in advance to have their final exam scheduled with ProctorU. Exceptions to remote proctoring will be granted in only rare cases such as when a student's circumstance prevents him/her from participating in remote proctoring. In these instances, the instructor, in collaboration with the student, will make alternative arrangements for the student to take the test with a face-to-face proctor.

Students needing testing accommodation for a qualifying disability must follow the instructions in our Park University Online Proctoring Policy Page (see URL below).

Students who are unable to take a remotely proctored exam at home may be able to take a remotely proctored test through ProctorU at a Park University Campus Center or the Park University Testing Center, or alternative site if approved in advance by your instructor.

Park, however, cannot guarantee that all Campus Center locations or the Testing Center will be able to administer remotely proctored exams due to the requirement for a controlled, interruption-free environment. Students who wish to take a proctored exam at a Park Campus Center or the Testing Center are responsible for notifying their instructor and making the arrangements on their own.

Students should contact the Center during the first week of class to determine if the Campus Center or Testing Center can accommodate remote proctoring.

There will be no charge to the student for using ProctorU services for the required remote proctoring for this course. However, failing to schedule your exam 72 hours prior to the exam date will result in the following late-fee charges for which the student will be responsible for paying:

- Students may be charged a $12.00 late exam registration fee if they schedule the exam within 24 hours of the exam date. 
- Students may be charged an $8.00 late exam registration fee if they schedule the exam between 72 and 24 hours of the exam date.

For further details on signing-up online in advance, how to test your equipment, how the ProctorU process works, or for information regarding accommodations for test-takers with disabilities - see Park Distance Learning - Academic Policies - Park University Online Proctoring Policy Page. 

URL:  [link: http://www.park.edu/park-distance-learning/academic-policies.html] http://www.park.edu/park-distance-learning/academic-policies.html

## Help With Interval Notation
*Posted: 2021-01-11T06:00:01Z*

Class,

When solving an inequality or writing domain, range, and other intervals of a function, we often use "interval notation."

When writing interval notation, first please be sure to write your intervals from least to greatest from left to right, just like on the real number line.

We then use either a parenthesis,  ( or ) ,  or a bracket, [ or ],  for the end of each interval, as follows...

We always us a parenthesis with either -oo  or +oo (we don't type the "+").

We use a [  or ]  around a number if the inequality is < or > . (non-strict inequality)

We use a (  or  )  around a number if the inequality is < or > .  (strict inequality)

EXAMPLES:

x > 5     interval notation:  (5, oo)

x > 5    interval notation:  [5, oo)

x < 5     interval notation:  (-oo , 5)

x < 5     interval notation:  (-oo , 5]

 

I hope this helps!

Mr. [INSTRUCTOR]

## Organizing Your Time For Your Math Course
Class,

Hello!  This is Mr. [INSTRUCTOR], your math instructor.

As you begin preparing for this course, I wanted to give you some tips on how to be successful in this course.

1.) The key is to work on the material every day (not just the weekends).

Each day you should work through at least one Section.

Begin by (1) reading the textbook, (2) watching the video, and (3) viewing the PowerPoint for each section.

To access the textbook, videos, and PowerPoint in MyMathLab:

Click on “MyLab and Mastering” from the menu on the left of the screen in Canvas

Click on “MyLab and Mastering Course Home”

Click on “Multimedia Library” from the menu on the left

Select the appropriate Chapter and Section from the Drop Box menus

Click on the appropriate media type and then click on "Find Now"

You should then work the problems in the Study Plan for that section in MyMathLab (MML).  

To access the Study Plan:

Click on “MyLab and Mastering” from the menu on the left of the screen in Canvas

Click on “MyLab and Mastering Course Home”

Click on “Study Plan” from the menu on the left

Click on "View All Chapters" (you may have to scroll down the page to find the link)

Click on the appropriate Chapter and then Section

Be warned.... there are LOTS of problems in each Assignment, so it is important that you are working on the material every day.

Also, make sure you are keeping neat and organized notes... you will want them as you work through the Quiz and prepare for the Final Exam.

2.)  To access the Assignment, click on "MyLab and Mastering" from the menu in the classroom and follow the links. 

You can (and should) work on the Assignment repeatedly until you get 100%.  This will help your grade immensely!

3.)  After you have completed the Assignment (probably by Saturday), you should then complete the Quiz.

To access the Quiz, click on "MyLab and Mastering" from the menu in the classroom and follow the links. 

You only get one attempt at the Quiz.  Once begun, you must complete it in that sitting (you cannot save and return to it later).

You will have 100 minutes to complete each Quiz.

So, be sure to allow yourself enough time to complete the Quiz in one shot with minimal distractions.

Make sure your notes are neat and organized so that you can refer to them while working on the Quiz.

4.) Each week, be sure to answer answer a Discussion Question (one not already answered) and respond to another student in the Discussion.   Answering the Discussion Question is worth 10 points and responding to another student is worth 5 points (for a total of 15 points each week), so it is definitely worth your time!  I encourage you to take care of this early in the week so that you don't forget!

5.)  For additional practice on any section, you can use the "Study Plan" in MyMathLab (MML).

To access the Study Plan:

Click on “MyLab and Mastering” from the menu on the left of the screen in Canvas

Click on “MyLab and Mastering Course Home”

Click on “Study Plan” from the menu on the left

Click on "View All Chapters" (along the top menu that appears)

Click on the appropriate Chapter and then Section

Be warned.... there are LOTS of problems in each Section of the Study Plan.  I suggest work every second or third problem (or more if you want).  You should work enough of the problems so that you are familiar with the material.

 

OTHER THOUGHTS:

I would estimate you will need 2 -3 hours a day to be successful in this course.  I suggest you break that up into two or three segments of around an hour each.

You will be taking LOTS of notes in this course, so be sure they are neat and organized.  You may use your notes while working on the Assignments and Quiz.

The tools in MyMathLab (MML) are very helpful, but only if you work on the material every day.

I hope this helps you structure your time in this course.

Please let me know if you have questions.

Thank you,

Mr. [INSTRUCTOR]

## Want To Earn All Possible MyMathLab (MML) Points? 
Students,

Make sure you are taking advantage of the ability to repeat the Assignment in each Unit.

You can re-enter the Assignment in each Unit as many times as you would like during the current week.  For every problem you want to redo, just click on "similar exercise" beneath the problem.  The problem must be incorrect initially before the "similar exercise" button will appear.  Once a similar problem is loaded, if you get the problem right, the similar exercise replaces the original problem.  

After completing the Assignment and when you feel confident with the concepts covered in the assigned reading (making sure you have practice some exercises of each type of problem given in your textbook), take the Quiz.  Unlike the Assignment, which is repeatable, you can only take the Quiz in each Unit ONE TIME.  Once you submit your Quiz answers you cannot reenter.  Further, the Quiz is timed (75 minutes).  

I want you to learn the concepts and earn as many points as possible.  Repeating  the Assignment each week is a way to do both.  Also, remember the assignments are only accessible during the week assigned.  So make sure you complete them before the end of day deadline on Sunday of each week.
