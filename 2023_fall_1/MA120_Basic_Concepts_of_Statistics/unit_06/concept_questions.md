Why would someone use a 90 percent level of confidence instead of a 99 percent level if the 99 percent level has a greater chance of including the population mean?

Class,

Using a 99 percent level of confidence instead of a 90 percent, would have a greater chance of including the population mean. A 99 percent confidence interval will be wider than a 90 percent confidence interval. A wider interval means that it is more likely to contain the population parameter. A more narrow confidence level of 90 provides a more accurate level of confidence.
