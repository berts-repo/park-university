I noticed that 19 was answered but labeled 18 in the disucussion post, so I labeled this one 19 but it is really question 18. The z-score is the number of standard deviations away from the mean, so a z-score of 0 is equal to the mean. The z-score allows the user to compare values from different data sets. The z-score can also be used to to determine siginificant values, if the values fall within -2 or +2 standard deviations from the mean, so values that have a z-score of +2 are siginificantly high and values that have a z-score of -2 are sigificantly low. In the section 3.2 video it was addressed that 68% of the data in a bell-curve has a z-score in the range of -1 to 1, and 95% of all values fall within a z-score of -2 to 2.
