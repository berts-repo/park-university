Class,

In order for the fallowing to be a valid probability distrobution the problem needs to meet three conditions according to the textbook:

1. There is a numerical (not categorical) random variable x, and its numerical values 
are associated with corresponding probabilities.

2. Σ P(x) = 1 where x assumes all possible values. (The sum of all probabilities 
must be 1, but sums such as 0.999 or 1.001 are acceptable because they result 
from rounding errors.)

3. 0 <= P(x) <= 1 for every individual value of the random variable x. (That is, 
each probability value must be between 0 and 1 inclusive.)

The first two conditions are met, there is numerical numbers that represent x, and the sum of all the probabilities is equal to 1. The third conditions is not met because '-16' is negative and falls outside the range of 0 to 1. The fallowing is not a valid probability distrobution.
