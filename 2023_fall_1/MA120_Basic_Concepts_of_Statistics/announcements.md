##  Class Discussion and Interaction
*Posted: 2023-08-23T05:00:01Z*

Everyone,

Here are some tips for doing well in the class discussion. These are the things I will be looking at when determining your discussion grade:

Participate throughout the Discussion Week. These means posting on multiple days throughout the week and being active in the discussion by responding to your peers. Waiting until Saturday or Sunday to complete the discussion question and then posting a single participation message that same day is not considered active participation throughout the week.

Respond to follow up questions in the class discussion that are asked by myself or your classmates. If I ask a follow up question or clarification on your discussion posting, you are expected to reply and answer it during the week. Not doing so, will result in a point deductions for your class participation.

Cite your sources when using additional information to answer questions.

Discussion questions should be your own thoughts and words. Using a website, textbook, etc to gather information if fine as long as you cite your source. Copying information directly from a website without answering the discussion question with your own words and thoughts is not acceptable.

Jay

What is MyMathLab?

MyMathLab is a tool to help you work on math homework and practice exercises and where you will take your quizzes. (Courses that use either MyMathLab/MyStatLab follow the same directions.) There are many types of learning activities and assessments each week in MyMathLab such as chapter opener, chapter study plan, lectures, video presentation, multimedia textbook, chapter review material, homework and quizzes.

My Math Lab must and the eBook must be purchased using the links on the left side of the page where it says MyLab and Mastering.  Once you have an access code, just use the links under the modules area of class to access all assignments in the lab. You will never access it from outside the course, so no access codes are needed.
