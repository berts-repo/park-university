Grade Policies

Extra credit may be offered at the instructor’s discretion.

If an extra credit assignment there will be an announcement in the Course Announcements.

No incompletes will generally be given. Exceptions may be made are for military deployment or similar circumstances as determined by the instructor and only in accordance with the University policies. Again, the instructor will require you to submit documentation to support your request.

Late Submission Policy

Due dates are posted on the Course Schedule.Discussions will never be accepted late.No late homework will generally be accepted. Technical difficulties, problems with computer software, web server, work schedule or other problems are not excuses for late homework. If your assignment is late, you will receive a 0 for the assignment. Please plan accordingly. 

We support our active and deployed military and our disabled students! Exceptions may be made are for military deployment or similar circumstances (as determined at the sole discretion of the instructor). The instructor may require verification and has discretion to approve the late submission.

However, the instructor does grade the code and gives credit even if the assignment is incomplete or does not function correctly. You are BETTER off turning in your assignment, than skipping it!

Failure to follow directions in submitting the assignment may results in losing up to a full letter grade on the assignment. 

FINAL EXAM

Please note the final exam is required to be proctored through the university approved proctor system.  Please follow the directions in the syllabus. Please review the course announcements about your final exam and proctoring requirements. This is a department requirement. 

This is a 2-hour final that can only be taken once.

The final exam is CLOSED BOOK.

The final exam is open notes. 

You may have 1 piece of paper with handwritten notes (both sides). 

You are not permitted to write on this paper during the exam!

No scratch paper is allowed.

Additional Rules:

You will not be permitted to view the source code of any web pages or use the Internet during the exam. 

You will not be able to use an editor during the exam. The programs you write during the exam must be typed directly into the exam in Canvas. 

These are the same requirements for all sections of the course. If you have any questions about the course, please feel free to contact the instructor!

The proctored final exam is a timed exam and will require programming. Make sure you have time to complete the exam before starting. You will need a reliable internet connection for two hours to complete the proctored final exam.  The final exam will not be reopened if you lose a connection.

Homework Assignments

Grading Rubric

All four learning outcomes are assess on every homework assignment. The student is expected to write, test and debug their web program before submitting the assignment. 

Please remember that exceeds, really means above and beyond the homework requirements. Additional points may be deducted for not following directions.  You will see this rubric used for each homework assignment. 

In order to meet the "creativity and problem-solving skills" objective, the student must be required to produce programming project solutions from given problem descriptions. Note that this objective is not met if a student is able to produce solutions only by pasting together given code fragments. Although pasting code fragments together is sometimes appropriate, it does not enforce the development of creativity and problem-solving skills.
