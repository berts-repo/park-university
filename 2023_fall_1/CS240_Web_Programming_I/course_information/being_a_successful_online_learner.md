# Being A Successful Online Learner

Course Participation Time Requirements

All of Park University courses require the online students to complete the same learning outcomes and have curricular consistency with the face to face courses. This course is as rigorous and comparable in learning outcomes and requirements to a 16-unit course. In other words, it’s the same course you would take in a traditional classroom, but taken online and in a shorter time frame. Most of the programming homework is geared to helping you learn the concepts. In the discussion assignments you are applying the concepts. Please consider your time available in this course. If you cannot participate adequately, or complete the assignments within the time allotted, you should consider taking this course in a traditional mode.

Why is there so much readings and activities in this course?

You are taking the same course that you would take in a traditional classroom. Some students read faster, and use their time and resources effectively. This is an accelerated course. But you should expect to spend at least 10-15 hours per unit on this course, readings, discussions and homework activities. If you follow the directions, you’ll have a much easier time completing the activities in a reasonable timeframe.

The U.S. Department of Education states that we are responsible to make sure that "there is an amount of student work for a credit hour that reasonably approximates not less than one hour of class and two hours of out-of-class student work per week over a semester for a semester hour..." 

Reference:  [link: https://www2.ed.gov/policy/highered/reg/hearulemaking/2009/credit.html] U.S. Department of Education

Planning Your Study Time Wisely

1. Read your book! Do not skip reading the book! You cannot simply skim readings, complete a single web page and expect to do well in the course and earn an “A”. Lack of your ability to prepare and study will affect your ability to learn the material and therefore will likely influence your final grade.  

2. Review the lessons. Use the lessons to help clarify the readings. Lessons in this course provide guidance to the textbook readings, highlighting important concepts and clarifying difficult concepts. Both readings are required. Use the lesson as your guide to the readings and discussions.

3. Practice Programming. You can review the samples in the textbook. The best way to learn how to program web sites is by actually programming  a web site. Learning how to program, and program web applications is complex and requires substantial reading and practice. One suggestion is to make your own "practice" pages as you complete your readings. The homework programming problems take about 2-3 hours per unit depending on the unit, and another hour for the discussion. If you are taking longer, then, you need to make sure you review the lessons and readings while you are completing the discussions. Remember on the  [link: https://canvas.park.edu/courses/75721/pages/textbooks-and-materials] Textbooks and Materials page you can locate a link to the publisher's web site which contains the source code for the book!

4. Complete all of the homework and discussion activities. The homework in this class consist of web programming assignments. You will be adding pages each unit. The faculty will review your homework and the code each unit. You are expected to participate online in the discussions, and submit your assignments online.

5. Start your homework early. You cannot expect to start your homework on Saturday and have it finished on Sunday. Learning web development is challenging even in a 16-unit course.  Therefore, you should review the homework assignments and begin them as early as possible during the unit. That way, you can receive help during the unit. The instructor and fellow students can help answer questions and provide guidance well before the assignment due date.

6. Follow directions. Of course, each student's time will vary. Some units you will spend more or less time. First, make sure to follow directions. However, failure to follow directions can result in delays. The [link: https://canvas.park.edu/courses/75721/pages/general-homework-requirements]  Homework Requirements page contains general instructions, which you should follow each week. 

7. Instructor-specific directions. Each instructor may have additional requirements for the assignments which they will clearly outline for you in the  [link: https://canvas.park.edu/courses/75721/announcements] Announcements. Please contact your instructor if you have questions about the assignments. 

8. Ask for help. If you are “stuck” try asking for help on the Instructor Office page. No they can't give you the answer, but they can direct you to the textbook of what you might want to review to solve your problem. Cheating on homework will not be tolerated. 

9. Complete activities off-line. If needed, you can complete your reading offline, compose your discussion responses offline, and practice creating your pages offline. Many students use Word to check spelling and grammar for the discussion posts, and then paste the discussion posts online. When you are including sample code in the discussion, please make sure the code is formatted as "preformatted" so that it is easily differentiated from the response.  [link: https://www2.ed.gov/policy/highered/reg/hearulemaking/2009/credit.html] 

Schedule Changes

Occasionally  there are weather problems on campuses, technical outages or other problems that may interfere with a student having access to the online courses. Instructors may modify these dates if needed. Students will be notified of changes in the schedule in the  [link: https://canvas.park.edu/courses/75721/announcements] Announcements.

Students who are deployed or TDY that may cause problems with due dates, please contact your instructor.  Students who need additional time for the final exam or other assistance who are disabled, please contact the instructor.

Your instructor may add content to the course, to help clarify concepts or provide additional tutorials or content, which may also be required. No book contains all of the information that we will cover in this course, or cover the variety of learning styles and various programming  levels, which students possess. So, please remember that the book is a reference too. You may also be given additional required readings and video tutorials in the online classroom. Your instructor will post in the  [link: https://canvas.park.edu/courses/75721/announcements] Announcements when additional content is provided and state where the content item is located. 

VARK Learning Styles for New Online Learners (Optional)

This information is optional. The purpose is to help students identify their learning style preference in order to understand learning style methods that would best suit their needs. The VARK Learning Style Questionnaire can help you identify your learning style preference in order to understand learning style methods that would best suit your needs. You may already know what your learning style preferences are, and some of you may disagree with the questionnaire results or usefulness. However, many students over the years do find this useful. Therefore, it is recommended that you complete this questionnaire but this is not required. 

This is a self-reviewed, non-graded activity. There are about 16 questions. This is a very popular assessment tool. After completing the tool, you will immediately be shown your results. The results will show you what your score was and what learning preference you prefer and learn how to improve your success in online courses.
