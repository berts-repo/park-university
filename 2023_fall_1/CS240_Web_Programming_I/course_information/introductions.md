# Introductions

Directions

Park University Campus 
in Parkville, Missouri. 

Working with new technology is such a part of what we do in this class. Most of you are taking the course because it is required. But instructors online try to help students meet their personal goals as much as possible. It would be helpful to the instructor to know what experience you have with web programming and what are your personal goals. Before you start the course, take a few moments to become acquainted with the discussion tool by posting a short introduction. 

However, we would like to get to know you better. One way to get to know your classmates is by sharing something about yourself. Some students don't feel comfortable sharing information about their personal life, such as their family. Please answer what you feel comfortable sharing. You do not have to answer all of the questions! 

Grading Requirement 

This assignment is required to be completed before you can proceed to Unit 1.

There are no points awarded for this assignment.

Please maintain professional decorum and netiquette. Refer to the Netiquette and communication guidelines in the Course Policies page. 

Please introduce yourself to the class . . .

Describe one of your educational and career goals you want to achieve

Describe one thing that you would like to accomplish in this course 

Describe your educational and work experiences with computer programming, mobile applications or web development.

Describe one thing about you as a person outside of academia such as a favorite pet, favorite activity, sport, hobby, interest, talents, favorite web site, book, movie, vacation spot, author, music, or television show.  (only what you are comfortable sharing). 

Mobile and portable devices and social networking applications are such a major part of our world today. Share with your classmates is your favorite app or social networking program. 

Post a photo or make a video or audio introduction (optional)

Post a response post . . .

Read and respond to at least one other participants' introductions!
