# Course Policies

Copyright, Content and Graphics

You are not required to create any graphics for your site or in this course. However, graphics can enhance the appearance of your web site.

You may use third party graphics provided you have the legal rights to use them and you cite the resource in your web page code, and they are in the public domain! If you would like to create your own graphic artwork you may do so. However, you will need to cite yourself as the resource owner in your web page code.   

You may not use code from other web sites except for syntax assistance from w3schools.com or other code snippets provided by your instructor. 

Course Communication and Participation

What is Participation?

Participation means forming supportive and inquiring relationships with other students in the class through postings to one another, sharing points of view, and providing encouragement and constructive critique, reading the assigned readings before beginning your assignments and online development, and applying readings to the virtual discussions and course development.

Netiquette

Each student brings different life experiences, points of view, and ways of knowing to our classroom and our learning environment. Everyone should plan to become an active member of the learning community. Netiquette describes the behaviors accepted for online communication. Do not use the discussion threads for posting comments or complaints about the course, instructor or other students. If you do, the discussion posting will be removed by the instructor! If you have a concern, contact your instructor.

Communications modes

The Course Announcements tool in the Online Classroom is an important way we communicate with you during the course. You are responsible for reading each of the announcements, including the regular unit discussions. If there are any changes required in assignments or due dates, they will be posted in the Course Announcements. 

Introductions

You are required to complete the Introductions discussion before you can start Unit 1.
