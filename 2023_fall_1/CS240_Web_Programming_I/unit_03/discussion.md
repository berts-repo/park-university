Chapter 6: Review Quistion 18

When a vector graphics image gets resized, why does it avoid the degradation experienced by bitmap images?

The vector does not experience the same drop in quality as a bitmap (or raster) image format, GIF, JPEG, and PNG. When bitmap remaps (changes sizes) it causes pixels to be added or dropped.  WIth vector images use mathmatical formulas that are independant of mapping colors to pixels. So, vector images would be best for images that use less colors, such as logos for a website that constantly need resized. Bitmap, on the other hand, would be best for images with a wide range of colors such as photographs. (Tech ease, 2023)
