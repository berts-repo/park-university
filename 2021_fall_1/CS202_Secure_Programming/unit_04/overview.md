# Unit 4: Overview

Introduction

In this unit we will review Arrays in C/C++ Programming: their declaration, initialization, use, and security.  We follow with the study of the characters as a data type. Finally, we combined the previous two topics and describe strings in C as arrays of characters ending in the null character (‘\0’). Various functions useful in the handling of C strings are also reviewed in detail, since they are the source of a group of programming vulnerabilities. Finally, the topic of bi-dimensional arrays is introduced as a gateway to begin the description of arrays of strings (to be completed in future units).

Unit Learning Outcomes

At the conclusion of the unit, the learner will be able to:

ULO#1: Incorporate the use of Arrays, Characters and Strings data types in C/C++ programs to solve problems (CLO2).

ULO#2: Identify vulnerabilities in the use of Arrays, Characters and Strings data types and develop strategies to prevent those (CLO3 & CLO4).
