# Unit 3: Overview

Introduction

In this unit we will review the use of integer and floating point numbers in C/C++ and the possible pitfalls that a program may contain by their use. Like any other programming language element, numbers operate within the constraints imposed by the hardware and the operating system of the computer in which they reside. Not every number can be created inside a computer, and there are limits on how small, or how large a number can be. Dealing with numbers without paying attention to these constraints are the source of vulnerabilities that can be prevented with more care. Students will be exposed to these vulnerabilities and the way to prevent them. Topics included in this unit are:

Representation, Wrapping and Conversion of Unsigned Integers

Representation, Wrapping and Conversion of Signed Integers

Operations with Signed and Unsigned Integers

Representation, Operations and Conversion of Floating Point Numbers

C Mathematical Libraries

Unit Learning Outcomes

ULO#1: Identify vulnerabilities in the use of Integer and Floating Point data types and develop strategies to prevent those (CLO3 & CLO4).
