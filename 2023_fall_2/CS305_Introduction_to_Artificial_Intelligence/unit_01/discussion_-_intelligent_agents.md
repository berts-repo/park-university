# Unit 1: Discussion - Intelligent Agents

Introduction

The purpose of this assignment is to better understand the agent perspective in artificial intelligence. You will work together with your classmates to develop several specifications of intelligent agents following the guidance of the text. 

Directions

Before beginning this assignment, complete the reading assignment at least through Ch. 1 of AIFCA and the Intelligent Agents lecture notes. 

Consider the agents mentioned in the text. Create a post naming and very briefly describing an intelligent agent of your choosing, separate from these examples. Your agent should meet AIFCA’s definition of acting intelligently outlined in  [link: http://artint.info/2e/html/ArtInt2e.Ch1.S1.html] Section 1.1.  Consider an agent that will be recognizable to your classmates and provide only a brief description (1-2 sentences at most).

Once there are a number of initial posts available in the discussion, choose one that has not yet been responded to. If you don’t feel the example meets the definition of an intelligent agent well, briefly outline some changes or assumptions you would consider for the example. Next consider the inputs to agents outlined in  [link: http://artint.info/2e/html/ArtInt2e.Ch1.S3.html] Figure 1.3 of the AIFCA text. Use the template on slide 6 of  [link: http://www.artint.info/2e/slides/ch01/lect1.pdf] AIFCA Lecture 1.1 (a blank template is also available on slide 11 and several examples are in-between). Post a response to the initial post you chose, filling out the details of the template for the agent your classmate chose and including any other comments you deem relevant. 

Before completing this step you should complete the reading assignment. Once most of the initial posts have been responded to, choose a response to an initial post containing the agent inputs that another student described in their submission for step 3 of this assignment. Develop a response to this response in which you describe a suitable environment for this agent. Consider  [link: http://artint.info/2e/html/ArtInt2e.Ch2.S2.html] Figure 2.1 of the AIFCA text (or slide 3 of [link: http://artint.info/2e/slides/ch02/lect1.pdf]  AIFCA Lecture 2.1). In addition to a brief description of the agent’s environment, also describe at least one important stimulus the agent could receive from the environment, as well as at least one action the agent might take.
