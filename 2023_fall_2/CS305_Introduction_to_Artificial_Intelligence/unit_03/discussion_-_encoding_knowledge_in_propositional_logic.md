# Unit 3: Discussion - Encoding Knowledge in Propositional Logic

Introduction

The purpose of this assignment is to practice modeling real-world knowledge with propositional logic and using proof methods to reason with that knowledge. You will explore, by hand, how an automated system can answer user questions using these knowledge representation and reasoning techniques. 

Directions

Before beginning this assignment, complete the reading assignment at least through Ch. 5.3.2 of AIFCA and the Intelligent Agents lecture notes. 

You will be split in to small groups for this assignment. Each group will be assigned a knowledge base along with several English descriptions of propositions that will need to be translated in to propositional logic, and one question for which an answer, along with a proof, will need to be provided. Your group should complete work on this prompt and post your final results by Thursday night. The instructor will post group assignments and prompts for each group in the discussion forum. Use the discussion forum to collaborate towards answers for your prompt. 

After Thursday, take a look at some of the work performed by other groups. Comment on some of the work where you think you can help them improve their responses, or if you only agree with their work and comments from others, explain your support and elaborate beyond your classmates’ replies if possible. 

Your classmates and possibly also your instructor should provide you with feedback by Saturday afternoon. If there are important issues with your work that need to be corrected, work with your group to produce an improved response to the original prompt.
