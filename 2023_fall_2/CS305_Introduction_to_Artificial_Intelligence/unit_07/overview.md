# Unit 7: Overview

Introduction

Unit 2 examined some basic techniques for representing and reasoning with knowledge. This unit will extend the topic with practical knowledge representation formats and reasoning systems using more powerful forms of logic. In this unit, you’ll work with your peers to utilize a commercial or open-source tool incorporating W3C standards for knowledge representation in the semantic web. In your homework assignment, you’ll work with Datalog for representing and reasoning with knowledge. 

Unit Learning Outcomes

At the conclusion of the unit, the learner will be able to:

Compare and contrast models for representing knowledge (CLO 3)

Model narrow domains of real-world knowledge using sophisticated logical reasoning systems (CLO 3, 4)

Import formal knowledge in to a popular sharable format (CLO 3, 4, 5)

Explain the importance of temporal and spatial aspects of reasoning (CLO 1, 3)
