# Unit 5: Discussion - Probabilistic Reasoning with Naïve Bayes

Introduction

In this assignment you’ll work through the model building process of a simple probabilistic learning algorithm, Naïve Bayes. Working with a group, you’ll develop a model for classifying data just as a machine would in Python and apply that model to a couple test cases.

Directions

Before beginning this assignment, complete the AIFCA reading assignment at least through section 10.1.2. 

You will be split in to small groups for this assignment. Each group will be assigned a dataset of training data with class labels and two test instances without class labels. Your group should work together to manually determine the probabilities needed for a naïve bayes model for the training data and determine the class for the two test instances by Thursday night. The instructor will post group assignments and prompts for each group in the discussion forum. Use the discussion forum to collaborate towards answers for your prompt.

After Thursday, take a look at some of the work performed by other groups. Comment on some of the work where you think you can help them improve their responses, or if you only agree with their work and comments from others, explain your support and elaborate beyond your classmates’ replies if possible.

Your classmates and possibly also your instructor should provide you with feedback by Saturday afternoon. If there are important issues with your work that need to be corrected, work with your group to produce an improved response to the original prompt.
