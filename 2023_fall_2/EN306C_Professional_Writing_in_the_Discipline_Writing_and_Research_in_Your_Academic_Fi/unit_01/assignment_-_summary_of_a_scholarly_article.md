Introduction

Directions

As we learned from Thonney, one of the most important foundational skills academic writers in all disciplines learn is the ability to take apart, understand, and paraphrase or summarize complicated academic writing.  For your first assignment, you will write a write a 300-word summary of Coyne et al.’s “ [link: https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/pdf/10.1080/08838151003737931?needAccess=true] Does Reality Backbite” using the reading and summarizing strategies presented in this unit. 

Your summary should pay attention to the overall conclusions, key terms, key examples, and main points of the authors while being specific and concise.   Rather than providing an unconnected list of points, your summary should demonstrate how these ideas fit together and the author’s purpose in writing (Why is this important? Relevant? Interesting? And to whom?).  Although you will be putting the author’s argument into your own words, you need to avoid oversimplifying, distorting, or judging the author's arguments. 

Criteria for Evaluation

When evaluating your summary, your instructor will examine how well your summary

Demonstrates accuracy, specificity, and concision

Displays comprehensiveness and balance: reasonable choice of main ideas, major points, and examples

Explains how the main ideas fit together and the author’s purpose(s) in writing

Avoids intrusion or judgment

Effectively organizes the information

Uses effective sentence structure, word choice, grammar, spelling, and punctuation that enables rather than hinders clear and effective communication.
