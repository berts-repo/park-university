## Instructions

Introduction

In this assignment, you will submit a 4-5-page report on your interview findings with your disciplinary expert.

Directions

This assignment is designed for you to explore an additional perspective on disciplinary research and writing practices, and to get feedback on your thinking for your final project in the course. 

Using email, phone or videoconference, interview a faculty member in your discipline about the field’s research and writing practices and compose a report that presents your findings to other undergraduates in the discipline. Your 4-5 pg. report, which can be organized in whatever format you deem effective, should include the following information:

A summary of your interviewee’s academic background, teaching and research focus, why you chose them to interview

A section in which you discuss the most important lessons about disciplinary research and writing you learned from your interview subject. What did you learn from this interview about writing conventions in your field? To develop this section, you should also include some direct quotes from your interviewee, which is where good note taking becomes especially important.

A section in which you discuss how your interviewee’s perspectives seem to affirm, complicate, or expand your perspectives on disciplinary discourse you gained in prior assignments.

A discussion of how the information you gleaned in the interview may have affected your thinking about your final project. Did it raise new perspectives for you to explore? Confirm your thinking? Suggest alternative perspectives? 

Your interview subject must be someone who has a terminal degree in the field, and who teaches and writes regularly in their academic discipline. 

This project will have several checkpoints you must complete before the final write-up. 

In Unit 2, you will be introduced to this assignment and begin identifying interviewees.

By the end of Unit 3, you must have an interview date scheduled with your interviewee and you will be recording this information for quiz credit.

By the end of Unit 4, you must submit your interview questions for quiz credit.

By the end of Unit 6, your interview should be complete.

At the end of Unit 7 your final write-up is due.

You should not wait or delay in identifying and scheduling your interview: Remember, professors are busy people, so try to get on their schedule early and give yourself plenty of time to switch interviewees if you are having trouble scheduling or completing an interview.  Waiting until the last minute and failing to secure an interview will result in a 0 grade for this project.  However, if you need help getting an interviewee to commit or respond, please talk to your instructor early and often for some help. 

Criteria for Evaluation          

When I grade your interview write-up, I will be looking for:              

Effective summary of your interviewee’s academic background, teaching and research focus, and why you chose them to interview

Effective discussion the most important lessons about disciplinary research and writing you learned from your interview subject

Effective discussion of how your interviewee’s perspectives seem to affirm, complicate, or expand your perspectives on disciplinary discourse you gained in prior assignments.

Effective discussion of how the information you gleaned in the interview may have affected your thinking about your final project.

Effective use of sentence structure, word choice, grammar, spelling, and punctuation

Effective organization and transitions

Unit Learning Outcomes Reflected in Assignment

Effectively respond to peers using peer response guidelines (CLO 2)

Use interview information from a disciplinary expert to reflect on writing conventions in the student’s field of study (CLO 1)
