# Unit 7: Peer Response

Introduction

In this unit, you will be placed in a small group to share drafts with one another for feedback.

Directions

For your post, write a list of questions you would like your peers to address in their responses to you, and attach a copy of your draft of the final essay to your post. Please note that your first post in the peer response is due earlier than your discussion in this unit.  This is so your peers have plenty of time to work on their responses.    

For your replies, read your peers’ drafts and post a well-developed response giving them feedback to help them revise their essay.  Make sure to use the tips for peer responding from the lecture to produce specific and helpful feedback. 

In your response, you need to write about at least 3 things that you think are effective (and explain why) and discuss the 3 most important things needing revision or development (along with some advice for how the writer could revise).  Simply listing these is not sufficient to get full credit for peer response.  As the guidelines explain, you need to explain your comments, provide specifics and examples, and provide helpful advice.   Use the questions your classmates posted with their draft to help you respond, but also think critically about areas that might need attention even if the writer does not ask for it specifically. 

In your response, you may not respond to grammar or mechanics; please focus on larger content issues. 

Because this task is worth 50 points, you should plan to write longer and more developed replies to your peers for this assignment. 

If you are placed in a group with 4 people, make sure to coordinate with your group to ensure that everyone gives and gets 2 responses.
