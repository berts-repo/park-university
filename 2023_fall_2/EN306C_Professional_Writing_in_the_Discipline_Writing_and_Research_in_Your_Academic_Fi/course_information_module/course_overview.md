Course Details

Title: EN306 (C) Professional Writing in the Disciplines- Advanced Expository and Research Writing

Course Description: This course is the third course in the required writing sequence at Park University. It emphasizes professional writing skills and expectations in various disciplines while developing further basic writing skills. Specific departmental courses may be deemed equivalent and will be used to satisfy this course. This course will have three sections- C. Advanced Expository and Research Writing: This course develops further skills in advanced expository writing: long essays or articles for publication in journals or trade magazines, arts or literary publications, the teaching of writing, and general critical or argumentative pieces.

Credit Hours: 3:0:3

Prerequisite Courses: EN105, EN106 and 60 credit hours.

Core Learning Outcomes

At the conclusion of the course, learners will be able to:

Use, analyze, and explain general academic and discipline-specific writing conventions appropriate to the student’s field of study.

Demonstrate effective academic writing techniques including planning, developing, researching, organizing, drafting, editing and proofreading written work.

Incorporate research findings into academic writing using effective techniques for evaluating, integrating, and documenting sources.

Student Opinion of Teaching

Starting in Unit 7, you will have access to the online Student Opinion of Teaching Survey for your courses. When you open the Survey page, you will see “Load Student Opinion of Teaching Survey in a new window” – please click this button. In the new window that opens you will see a list of courses for which you have surveys to complete. Each course evaluation should take no longer than 15 minutes to complete. You will be able to access your surveys multiple times to edit answers or to complete saved surveys. Surveys must be completed by the last day of the course, 11:59 p.m. CT.

Please be assured that your responses are presented anonymously to your instructors, and will be compiled for the university via a third party provider, Campus Labs. All student responses will be summarized and reported to your instructor(s) after the term is over and grades have been posted.
