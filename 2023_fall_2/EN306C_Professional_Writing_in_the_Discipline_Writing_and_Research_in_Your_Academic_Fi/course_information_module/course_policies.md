# Course Policies

Plagiarism

Plagiarism is the use of source material without proper citation and is a very serious offense.  Plagiarism includes copy-pasting material from other sources without citations, the use of quotations from a source without quotation marks, the use of quotations or paraphrases without in-text and end-text citations, the use of another's idea without citing the source, the submission of a paper, project, or class assignment (or any portion of such) prepared by another person or prepared by you but for a different course. 

Plagiarism can result in failure of the assignment or the course, probation, suspension, or expulsion depending on the seriousness of the violation. 

If you have questions about proper use of source material or citation, please review the course materials on citation and plagiarism and ask your instructor for clarification.

Discussion Guide

Discussion allows you to interact with your classmates and instructor, apply information you learned in that unit’s material, and/or practice concepts that will help you develop the formal assignments. 

Each discussion topic requires a post and 2 replies to your peers’ posts. 

Discussion posts are due by Thursday, 11:59 pm CT of each unit and replies are due Sunday at 11:59 pm CT. There are other assignments that will use the discussion modules, such as peer response, that have other due dates and requirements.  Please read these assignments carefully. 

Post Guidelines:

Unless otherwise specified by the discussion assignment, posts need to be a minimum of 300 words long and should be written in complete sentences.  While discussion posts are somewhat more informal than the assignments, you should still use a writing style appropriate for a classroom. Please edit your posts for grammar and spelling and general readability. 

Reply Guidelines:

When replying to your peers, please be respectful of one another.  While it is okay, even desirable, to question one another or disagree, please do so in a way that is constructive and polite.   Replies to others’ posts should be substantive, i.e. they should add something to the conversation.  You can certainly agree with one another, but you should still add something to the conversation (why else might you agree? What other reasons are there to agree?) 

Late Assignments:

Instructors set their own guidelines for accepting (or not accepting) late papers.  Please refer to the syllabus for their policies.
