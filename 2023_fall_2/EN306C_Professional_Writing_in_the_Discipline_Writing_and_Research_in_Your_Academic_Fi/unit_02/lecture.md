Unit 7 Assignment: Final Interview write-up

Introduction

In this assignment, you will submit a 4-5-page report on your interview findings with your disciplinary expert.

Directions

This assignment is designed for you to explore an additional perspective on disciplinary research and writing practices, and to get feedback on your thinking for your final project in the course. 

Using email, phone or videoconference, interview a faculty member in your discipline about the field’s research and writing practices and compose a report that presents your findings to other undergraduates in the discipline. Your 4-5 pg. report, which can be organized in whatever format you deem effective, should include the following information:

A summary of your interviewee’s academic background, teaching and research focus, why you chose them to interview

A section in which you discuss the most important lessons about disciplinary research and writing you learned from your interview subject. What did you learn from this interview about writing conventions in your field? To develop this section, you should also include some direct quotes from your interviewee, which is where good note taking becomes especially important.

A section in which you discuss how your interviewee’s perspectives seem to affirm, complicate, or expand your perspectives on disciplinary discourse you gained in prior assignments.

A discussion of how the information you gleaned in the interview may have affected your thinking about your final project. Did it raise new perspectives for you to explore? Confirm your thinking? Suggest alternative perspectives? 

Your interview subject must be someone who has a terminal degree in the field, and who teaches and writes regularly in their academic discipline. 

This project will have several checkpoints you must complete before the final write-up. 

In Unit 2, you will be introduced to this assignment and begin identifying interviewees.

By the end of Unit 3, you must have an interview date scheduled with your interviewee and you will be recording this information for quiz credit.

By the end of Unit 4, you must submit your interview questions for quiz credit.

By the end of Unit 6, your interview should be complete.

At the end of Unit 7 your final write-up is due.

You should not wait or delay in identifying and scheduling your interview: Remember, professors are busy people, so try to get on their schedule early and give yourself plenty of time to switch interviewees if you are having trouble scheduling or completing an interview.  Waiting until the last minute and failing to secure an interview will result in a 0 grade for this project.  However, if you need help getting an interviewee to commit or respond, please talk to your instructor early and often for some help.

How to Find Journals in Your Discipline

This unit’s assignment asks you to survey journals in your field and choose 2 to review for your assignment.  You might be wondering how to find journals in your field and how to access them to review a recent issue. The assignment sheet gives you some ideas for how to locate journals in your field, but you might also be wondering how to access and read those journals.

First, you may find it helpful to know that many journals are moving to a fully open access format online.   [link: https://doaj.org/] The Directory of Open Access Journals is a good place to start when looking for journals that have made all their research available free to anyone online.  You can also search for a journal’s website online, as many are making articles fully available on their websites. 

 [link: https://library.park.edu/home] Park University’s Library also has numerous subscriptions to databases that can give you access to non-open access journals.  There are a couple of ways you can use Park’s resources to locate journals.  If you already know the name of a journal you want to access, you can use the journal finder from the library’s home page.  If you are off campus, it will ask you to sign in using your Park ID and password.   If one of the databases has access to the journal you want, it will tell you which one.  In many databases, you can browse issues of journals.  If the journal finder does not locate your journal, it means we do not have access to it through one of our subscriptions.  However, the journal may still be available through the Open Access Journals or on the journal’s own website, so try googling the journal to see if you can get access.

Perhaps you don’t know yet which journal you would like access to.  The databases can help you with that too.  You can access  [link: https://library.park.edu/az.php] a list of databases from the library’s home page and browse for a database that indexes journals related to your field, then access the list of journals indexed by that database.  You can also search for a topic in your field that interests you then examine the search results to see which journals publish articles on that topic regularly. 

You can also access  [link: https://library.park.edu/guides?b=g&d=a&group_id=16767] research guides categorized according to majors and disciplines from the Library homepage.  The guides might help you decide which databases or resources could be most relevant to your field of study. 

Plagiarism and Citation

You will be learning about integrating sources in more detail in later units, but for this unit, please review the College Writing Handbook chapters on  [link: https://courses.lumenlearning.com/suny-jeffersoncc-styleguide/chapter/defining-plagiarism/] Defining Plagiarism and  [link: https://courses.lumenlearning.com/suny-jeffersoncc-styleguide/chapter/avoiding-plagiarism/] Avoiding Plagiarism.  You should also pay careful attention to the plagiarism statement in your syllabus.   If you have questions at any time about what constitutes proper use of sources or citation, please ask your instructor for clarification. 

You may also want to become familiar with the  [link: https://owl.purdue.edu/owl/purdue_owl.html] Purdue OWL guides to citation styles.  Purdue’s OWL has the most accurate free guides to the most common citation styles used by students in different disciplines (APA, MLA, Chicago, and AMA).  You will need to know which style is used by your discipline, and you will be expected to use a style guide to produce both in-text and end-of-text citations accurately.  You might note that citation generators like Easybib, while sometimes useful, only get citations correct about 50% of the time. There is no substitute for knowing the style used by your discipline and being able to use a citation guide effectively.  If you do not already know what citation style is preferred by experts in your field, you can ask your professors in the field or look at journal websites (who will tell authors in their submission instructions which citation style they should use). 

Disciplinary Differences in Writing

In the last unit, we talked about writing conventions that are common to all disciplines, but how are disciplines different? In this unit, you will begin to articulate how writing in your field works differently from other fields. 

First, read Nahid Golafshani’s article “ [link: https://nsuworks.nova.edu/tqr/vol8/iss4/6/] Understanding Reliability and Validity in Qualitative Research” to begin to get a sense of how disciplines with quantitative methods might differ from those that use qualitative methods.  You might also remember that there are fields that use mixed methods, or that have both qualitative and quantitative strands in their field.  For example, many of the social sciences such as sociology or political science have both qualitative and quantitative traditions and you may find scholarship in those fields that does one or the other or a mixture of both. One of the questions you might be able to answer for yourself this unit: What methods are used by your discipline?  Think about this as you review journals this unit. 

Also look for your discipline on Montclair State University’s  [link: https://www.montclair.edu/center-for-writing-excellence/digitaldashboard/resources-for-writers/writing-in-the-disciplines-across-the-curriculum/] list of writing resources across the disciplines and review the materials they have linked: What can you learn about writing in your discipline by looking at resources like these?
