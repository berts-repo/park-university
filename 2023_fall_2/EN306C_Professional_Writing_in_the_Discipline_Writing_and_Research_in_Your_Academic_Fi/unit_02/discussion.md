Class,

When looking and reading through prior journals and other academic writing in my field of Cybersecurity, I have already observed that most of the professional writing uses qualitative structure. Cybersecurity is constantly using validity in almost all their journals; my entire degree is using different software that can monitor and measure vulnerabilities and exploits to determine the magnitude of the threat, often new or upcoming binaries (software) and algorithms (programing) created by the authors of the journal. The experts in the field need to be able to determine what they are measuring and recreate the solutions and detections accurately, to determine if their actions are replicable (reliable).

In my journal article, Smart Approach for Botnet Detection Based on Network Traffic Analysis, the author is referring to botnets sometimes called zombie networks, which is essentially a large group of infected client computers that the threat actor can activate from a base of operation (command and control) to deploy different payloads (types of attacks) through all the infected computers. The most common attack vector performed by botnets are denial of services attacks (DDOS), but there are many other attack vectors. The authors work implements artificial intelligence to monitor network traffic via timestamps to identify and potentially prevent an attack, that is determined by different “rules” (Obeidat & Yaqbeh, 2022).

The author must prove the credibility of the trustworthiness of his work, by giving the proposed methodologies that uses an IDS (Intrusion Detection System) tool, to extract the most important network packets. (Obeidat & Yaqbeh, 2022) The study also uses different variables including the number of “in packets” and “out packets” as well as “packet/time” and “packets retrieved from the same IP” (the computers address) (Obeidat & Yaqbeh, 2022). The different variables are implemented into the rules to reliably determine the threat of a botnet attack.

The only clear examples of quantitative writing I found in my journal is when the author refers to how cyber crime is on the rise, in the introduction. (Obeidat & Yaqbeh, 2022) The author also states, “Information security flaws, identity theft, and other threats might threaten the confidence and reliability of the information.” (Obeidat & Yaqbeh, 2022) so the triangulation of the different tools (validity) and the accuracy to recreate these solutions (reliability) becomes a necessary to show that identified threats can be accurately recreated to prove the confidence of the study.  

 

Work Cited:

Obeidat, A., & Yaqbeh, R. (2022). A novel approach for detecting and classifying cyberattacks using deeplearning. Journal of Electrical & Computer Engineering, 2022, 1-10
