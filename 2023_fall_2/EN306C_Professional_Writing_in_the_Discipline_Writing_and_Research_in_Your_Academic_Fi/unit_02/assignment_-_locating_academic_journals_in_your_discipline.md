Tips for Picking Journals

Consult your department’s website, look up information about faculty, and note the journals in which they’ve published research

Google the name of your discipline +journal (for example, google American History Journals, and skim the results)

Ask faculty which journals they read in their field or which journals are the top journals in the field

Consult the library’s research guide for your major or databases relevant to your discipline to see which journals they index

Check for professional organizations that publish academic journals

Google professional forums that discuss or rank journals in your area

Class, the authors of Journal of Information Science Theory and Practice, begin by describing how botnets, or zombie computers, have become the most common threat regarding cybercriminals (Obeidat & Yaqbeh, 2022). These botnets lay dormant on a computer and can be awakened from a command-and-control computer to perform different malicious activities: including, trojans, worms, viruses, DDOS attacks, and rootkits.

The topic is a subject that is commonly and highly reviewed in academic journals, “the technique and scale of botnet detection are the key features that distinguish this work from existing” (Obeidat & Yaqbeh, 2022). In the study the authors propose a new solution using machine learning (ML) to monitor network traffic and accurately catch malicious network packets.

The authors propose a model that includes these stages: capturing network packets, extracting flagged packets, training AI to classify packets, and inspecting packets for malicious activity (Obeidat & Yaqbeh, 2022). The study uses different variables including the number of incoming and outgoing packets, as well as the timestamp, for the extracted data. By using the proposed new detection algorithm, the protection systems can accurately compare different packets to a set of rules, that is known for malicious activity.

The algorithm works by analyzing the network packets: by checking the IP address of the sending computers, how often the sending computer sends a packet, if the packet contains malicious key words, as well as the packet size. The algorithm then sends the information to an AI to determine if the packet contains malicious activity through ML.

To determine the validity of the study the authors have implemented a rigorous and extensive testing system to determine what is normal. The results can then be replicated by different performance measurements: including a test for (1) accuracy, which uses a formula to evaluate events that were successfully predicted compared to the total of all predictions, (2) precision, which takes all the true positives divided by all positive predictions, (3) recall, which uses the true positives divided by positive results (Obeidat & Yaqbeh, 2022).

The authors of this study present a reliable and accurate approach to detect and prevent botnet attacks using machine learning and network packet analysis. The paper contributes to the field of information security by proposing a new solution that leverages the power of artificial intelligence to prevent one of the most common and dangerous cyber threats.

Class, with the growing threat of artificial intelligence (AI) and the number of devices connected to networks, the threat of malicious activity is at an all-time high, and the research team at the Korean Institute of Science and Technology Information, explain the importance of this. There are many prevention systems in place: SIEM, ESM, UPS, but the authors at KISTI are focused on the Intrusion Detection Systems (IDS), and Intrusion Prevention Systems (IPS), but the threat is still growing due to AI. Because AI is now able to automate tasks and enumerate attacks the threat is becoming more prevalent.

The study takes place by monitoring network packets, which is the format for communicating all the information from devices connected to the network and internet. IDS is used to monitor network packets, but they do not interrupt services, so if there is malicious activity it is recorded but not stopped. IPS is used to monitor packets as well, but it will stop the flow of traffic if malicious activity is detected. The authors propose a solution that more efficiently trains AI models to properly respond to “real-word” sultions. (Taewoong et al., 2022). The authors also describe that is addressed in the academic conversations, because to the AI training methods using datasets, which tends to be “outdated” data (Taewoong et al., 2022). The solution is taken in three steps: (1) Preserving significant special characters, (2) Generating a stopword list, and (3) Class label refinement.

Malicious activity often requires special delimiters, the character ‘:’ is used in system to often set a parameter or property for software. The ‘@’ symbol is commonly used associated with emails. The authors have provided quantitative data that is highly successful at training AI to recognize malicious activity through special characters that are used for malicious activity, add them to a stopword list, and then properly label these variables for review and training of the AI systems. They do this by using a Term Weight Calculation, that takes a weight of commonly used character combinations, that is commonly used with malicious activity. With all the different defensive systems in place, it becomes hard to keep everything labeled uniformly, so the authors use a proposed calculation, TF-IDF, to properly label the malicious activity.

The paper by the team at KISTI addresses the challenge of detecting malicious activity on a network packet using AI. The study contributes to the field of information security by proposing new techniques that leverage to improve, detect, and prevent malicious attacks, which helps cope with the evolving threat posed by AI-based attacks.
