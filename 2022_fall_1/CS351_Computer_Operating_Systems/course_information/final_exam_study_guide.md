# Final Exam Study Guide

When

2 hours, between Monday and Sunday of Unit 8. May be taken only once.

It may be a good idea to take the exam by Saturday (or even Friday) to give you some buffer time, just in case something happens at the last minute. 

It’s also good to set aside a 2.5-hour window for the actual test, as the sign-in may take a few minutes. 

Online Proctoring

Be in a private, well-lit room free from other people and noises.

Clear your workspace. Please show your workspace during the room scan of your exam session.

No break is allowed during the test. Refresh yourself before starting the exam.  Please follow  [link: https://www.park.edu/terms-and-regulations/disability-guidelines/] Park's Disability Guideline if you require accommodation.

A practice quiz may be available for you to get acclimated to the online proctoring system. Find it under Honorlock in the Canvas menu, otherwise check with your instructor. The practice quiz is optional and may be taken repeatedly. Be aware that the question types used by the practice quiz may not match those used in the final exam. It's just a standard practice quiz from the proctoring system.

The Exam

Allowed

One sheet of handwritten notes, US letter size (8.5 x 11''). You may use both sides. You must show your notes to the camera, two seconds per side at the beginning of the test as part of the room scan.

An on-screen basic calculator will be provided through the proctoring system (see the Basic calculator at  [link: https://honorlock.kb.help/students-during-exam/honorlock-calculator/] Honorlock calculator). 

No other resources are allowed. You will only be allowed to open the final exam (a longer quiz) and type up your answer on the quiz page.  You will not be allowed to use another application/additional browser tab/window/device. 

Final Exam “Show one question at a time” Setup

The final exam is set up to display one question at a time.  Unit 6 and 7 homework/quizzes are set up in this style to help you prepare for it. 

You may use the Next/Previous buttons at the bottom of the page or the sidebar on the top right corner of the page to navigate through questions.  Questions are not locked so you may go back to an answered question and revise as often as you need. See the Canvas Student Help page “ [link: https://community.canvaslms.com/docs/DOC-10535-421291043] How do I take a quiz where I can only view one question at a time?”  

Types of questions:

Concept questions: explain, compare/contrast, and/or illustrate with examples.

Problem-solving

Topics:  comprehensive (i.e. units 1 – 7).  Review all quizzes, assignments, and discussion questions.   

Here are the overall learning outcomes for the course, which provide a big picture guide for final exam questions:

Solve problems involving processes and threads, memory management, processor management, input/output, and deadlock.

Explain and analyze operating system concepts in practical applications.

Explain and analyze operating system security.

Concept questions will come from Unit 1 ~ Unit 7, similar to the ones in quizzes/assignments. 

Problem-solving questions will be on those topics:

Ch2 Process and Threads

How long processes take to run in sequential and parallel processing.

Ch3 Memory Management

Impact of page faults

Calculate average processing time

Ch4 File Systems

Calculate data transfer time

Ch5 Input/Output

Calculate data transfer time

Ch6 Deadlocks

Identify deadlock and starvation
