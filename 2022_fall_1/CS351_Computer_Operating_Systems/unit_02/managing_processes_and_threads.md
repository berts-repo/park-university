# Unit 2: Managing Processes and Threads

Overview

In this unit, you will study in detail the central concept of Operating Systems: Process and Thread. This covers the definition, the purpose, various states of a process/thread. various process/thread scheduling methods, how processes/threads communicate with one another, and the difference in handling processes and scheduling between Windows and Unix.

Learning Objectives

Upon completing this unit, students will:

Appraise the characteristics of a process

Categorize basic states of a process

Examine the concept of queuing

Examine how processes are controlled

Examine the concepts of threads in the creation of processes

Identify the types of scheduling and scheduling algorithms

Examine the concepts real-time scheduling

Define a kernel and appraise how it is used

Apply frequently-used Linux commands

Write a simple program in Linux

Compare and contrast processes and scheduling in Windows to Linux
