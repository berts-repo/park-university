I am finding it difficult to specifically specifying a role for each career level. The reason this is difficult and interesting to me is the redundant job titles in the cybersecurity field. An example, there is a common job title of Security Engineer in the entry, mid, and advanced career levels. Also, if you look in the NICE framework in the Protect and Defend category, there is title of Cyber Defense Analyst which is also in all three tiers of the career path. The different career levels seem to be more a reflection of mastery rather then title.

I wanted to fallow the career path in the Protect and Defend category of the NICE framework. This led my path to be:

Cybersecurity Specialist -> Penetration and Vulnerability Tester -> Cybersecurity Engineer

As a Cybersecurity Specialist some of the rolls include, Cyber Defense Analysis, Cyber Defense Infrastructure Support Specialist, Cyber Defense Incident Responder, and Vulnerability Assessment Analyst. This role partially requires knowledge that I have already studied, networking, architecture, threat vectors, and cross-network traffic, and some things I have yet to study in my degree like cryptography. Going this route would expose me to a variety of skills and practices, that would get me ready for a mid-level career, like threat hunting and threat intelligence and response.

Penetration and Vulnerability Tester sounds rewarding and is currently my mid-level goal. So far, I have really enjoyed my programming classes and the feeling you get when you HAVE to get a program running, or it's just a fail. The same reward could be applied to pen and vulnerability testing, you either fail or succeed. Some of the rolls include, Cyber Defense Analyst, Cyber Defense Infrastructure Support Specialist, and Vulnerability Assessment Analyst, and some of the certifications often associated with this position include: CEH, Security+, and CISA.

This position would require an underlining knowledge of how everything works, before you could figure out how to troubleshoot issues in virtualization and cloud computing.
