Course Details

Title: CS375 Secure Operations

Course Description: This course will focus on the principles, knowledge, skills, and tools about secure operations of computers, networks, and information technology systems. Topics include security concepts, cryptography, attack methods, security monitoring, incident response, handling and analysis. The course will contain various hands-on security labs. This course includes the concepts in a course recommended by Cisco for Cisco Certified CyberOps Associate (CBROPS) certification.

Credit Hours: 3:0:3

Prerequisite Courses: CS335 and CS366a

Core Learning Outcomes

At the conclusion of the course, learners will be able to:

Describe the role of the Cyber security Operations Analyst in the enterprise.

Analyze the operation of network infrastructures, protocols and services.

Demonstrate various types of network attacks.

Practice various methods to identify, monitor, prevent, respond and analyze attacks to computer networks, hosts, and data.

Student Opinion of Teaching

Starting in Unit 7, you will have access to the online Student Opinion of Teaching Survey for your courses. When you open the Survey page, you will see “Load Student Opinion of Teaching Survey in a new window” – please click this button. In the new window that opens you will see a list of courses for which you have surveys to complete. Each course evaluation should take no longer than 15 minutes to complete. You will be able to access your surveys multiple times to edit answers or to complete saved surveys. Surveys must be completed by the last day of the course, 11:59 p.m. CT.

Please be assured that your responses are presented anonymously to your instructors, and will be compiled for the university via a third party provider, Campus Labs. All student responses will be summarized and reported to your instructor(s) after the term is over and grades have been posted.
