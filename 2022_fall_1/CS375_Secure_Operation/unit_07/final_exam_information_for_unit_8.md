Introduction

You have been hired as a junior security analyst. As part of your training, you were tasked to determine any malicious activity associated with the Pushdo trojan.

You will have access to the internet to learn more about the events. You can use websites, such as VirusTotal, to upload and verify threat existence.

The tasks below are designed to provide some guidance through the analysis process.

You will practice and be assessed on the following skills:

Evaluate event alerts using Squil and Kibana.

Use Google search as a tool to obtain intelligence on a potential exploit.

Use VirusTotal to upload and verify threat existence.

Directions

The skills exam assesses your hands-on security operation skills using Netlab.

The exam is available during Unit 8. It is recommended to complete other labs in Unit 8 before starting the skill exam.

You can access the exam multiple times. However, each time you will start from the initial system default configuration.

You are required to do web search during the lab. Make sure your internet is connected and can visit major search engines, such as google, etc.

To get access to the exam lab, schedule the lab the same as you do for the course. You should see the below list while you are scheduling. Select "Cisco CyberOps Associate v1 SA" to get to the lab system.

   

Follow the lab instruction to complete the lab. While performing the lab, write a report to answer all the questions listed in the rubric table. Notice that the rubric table may require additional screenshots to prove your work, which might not be specified in the lab instruction document. To avoid losing points, please attach screenshots if requested in the rubric table.

This Lab takes longer time. When you find your time slot is almost used up, hit the button on the top right corner of NetLab to extend the time.

Due Date

By 11:59p.m., Sunday, CT

Submission

A lab result addressing all the questions listed in the Rubric.

Notice that the rubric table may require additional screenshots to prove your work, which might not be specified in the lab instruction document. Please make sure to attach screenshot as requested on rubric table.
