I want to talk about how New Media is changing the media model, which is not just affecting journalism but it also affects how democracy is depicted. The newspaper model of media is about obsolete, and the media companies have to make substantial shifts in their business model while making cutbacks on expenses. ABC laid off 400 employees while CBS had to lay of 80, which was averaged to be 25% of a media outlets staff. (Page One, 2011) Along with the large staff cuts companies like The New York Times had to shift with the technology and start implementing journalism through blogging, and Brian Stelter references how he does not understand when a journalist does not use a platform like Twitter, as it is front line to breaking news. (Page One, 2011)

The shift to New Media is causing issues in journalism related to politics and democracy as well. These media companies can no longer afford to send journalist to political events, and in response anyone with a phone can be recording an event. Maxwell McCombs and Donald Shaw, scholars of mass communication, talk about how when the media covers an event it becomes a more relevant subject to the public, but when the media stops talking about the topic it becomes a lot less relevant to the people. (Barnett & Reynolds, 2009, p 50-51) With the ability for agenda setting by the media outlets, it is putting a lot more media pressure on the politicians.
