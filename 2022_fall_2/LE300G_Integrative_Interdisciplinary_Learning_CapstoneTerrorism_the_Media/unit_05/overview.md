# Unit 5: Overview

Introduction

This unit’s lecture covers cable TV news and terrorism and the blurred lines between reporting and commentary.

By the end of this unit you will have read five lectures and the first five chapters in the textbook and completed three reading quizzes; all of which were designed to inform your understanding of the relationship between media and terrorism and prepare you to do well on the Midterm, due Sunday by 11:59 p.m.

This unit also marks the beginning of your research project for this course. You will submit a research paper by the end of Unit 8. This unit you will learn the assignment requirements and begin the first phase: brainstorming and doing preliminary research for the proposal for your project. The proposal is due the end of Unit 6. All Unit assignment details as well as tips on how to do research and verify the legitimacy of online resources are included in Unit 5.

Unit Learning Outcomes

At the conclusion of the unit, the learner will be able to:

Define terrorism (CLO 1, 2)

Compare distinguishing characteristics of different forms of media including social media related to the coverage of terrorism (CLO 3)

Synthesize how political science, communication, and other fields of study may be used to better understand the relationship between terrorism and the media (CLO 4)

Explain the roles media play in terrorist acts (CLO 5)
