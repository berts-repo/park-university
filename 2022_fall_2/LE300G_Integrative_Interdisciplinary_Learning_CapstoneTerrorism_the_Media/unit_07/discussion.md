In the article Behind TV Analysts, Pentagon's Hidden Hand, we can see David Barstow take an adversarial approach to his journalism. Barstow is trying to show how there is mis-information, and, or mis-direction of information, between what is going on with "The War on Terrorism" and media, and also how there is interest groups that are profiting from war and utilizing this mis-information. We see signs of Barstow implementing investigative reporting from the amount of sources and the variety of references, he uses throughout the article. The entire text is filled with dates and statistics from many different entities, and this would require extensive investigating and note taking. The big difference between this article and a "normal" article would be the framing that the writer takes. What I'm noticing is when an author is doing investigative reporting he is usually taking the adversarial approach, and when an article is covering "breaking-news" or "normal" articles, then the rolls are usually interpretive or dissemination. We can see this in the framing of Barstow's article, and the amount of times he is trying to discredit the apposing voice, and a good example is when he reports on how The White House takes a  strong interest in who their analyst are and what they talk about.
