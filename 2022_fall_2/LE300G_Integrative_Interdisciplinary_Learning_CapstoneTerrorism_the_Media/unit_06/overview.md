# Unit 6: Overview

Introduction

By now, we hope your understanding of the problematic nature of the relationship between news media and terrorism has deepened and that you are ready to apply what you have learned by creating a research paper proposal, which is due the end of this unit. The Discussion this unit asks you to discuss your research topic choices with fellow students and your instructor.

The focus this unit is on helping you think through your chosen topic and how to research it – and to collaborate with your peers on how to figure out the most efficient and efficacious way to complete this assignment. There is no lecture, no reading assignments or quizzes so that you can allocate time this unit to think, research, discuss, and write.

Your instructor will act as facilitator and guide of the Discussion, helping by answering questions about topic choices and how to find legitimate media sources and articles. Your professor can help you define the scope of your research, so that you do not become overwhelmed by taking on a topic that can’t be done well in a couple of units.

If you want to bounce ideas off of your instructor without other students able to “listen in,” remember to send him or her an email via Canvas or use their Park.edu address.

To get started, review the assignment details and requirements, tips and suggestions by clicking this link:  [link: https://canvas.park.edu/courses/71314/pages/unit-5-research-paper-proposal-guidelines] Research Paper Proposal Guidelines 

Unit Learning Outcomes

At the conclusion of the unit, the learner will be able to:

Define terrorism (CLO 1, 2)

Compare distinguishing characteristics of different forms of media including social media related to coverage of terrorism (CLO 3)

Explain the roles media play in terrorist attacks (CLO 5)

Give and receive feedback on research topic (CLO 6)
