# Course Policies

Quizzes

Quizzes are timed, and you will run out of time if you have to look up all your answers in the book. To do well, make sure you have learned the material in advance.

Proctored Final Exam

This course requires a proctored, 120-minute comprehensive final exam. Online students will be proctored via an online proctoring service. Face-to-face students will be proctored in the classroom. See  [link: https://canvas.park.edu/courses/71018/pages/study-guide-for-cs252-final-exam-online-sessions] Final Exam Study Guide for details about the final exam.  See the Online Proctoring Module for details about the online proctoring requirement.     

Documented Excuse

The following is a list of valid documented excuses. Those may be applied for late assignment submission as specified in Late Assignment Policy on  [link: https://canvas.park.edu/courses/71018/pages/grading-and-schedule] Grading and Schedule.

A medical emergency or a serious acute illness. All medical emergencies and illnesses must be verified by a note on letterhead by an M.D., D.O., P.A., or R.N. The course instructor will not normally accept a note from other health professionals (e.g., Ph.D., MSW, D.C., Physical Therapist) because their professional functions rarely involve medical emergencies or acute illnesses. The course instructor may accept late work for students who can provide evidence of a verified medical emergency (but not acute illness) involving a child, spouse, parent, sibling, or grandparent.

An Accident or Police Emergency. An accident report or note on letterhead from an appropriate law enforcement officer is required to accept late work due to accidents or police emergencies.

Unforeseen Jury or Witness Duty. A note on letterhead from a judge or attorney is required to accept late work due to jury or witness duty.

Unforeseen Military Deployment or Activation. A note on official letterhead from your commanding officer is required.

Funerals for Immediate Family Member (e.g., parents, siblings, grandparents, aunts/uncles, first cousins). A copy of the obituary or a note from a minister or funeral director is required.

Attendance

Refer to the Tuition, Fees, Grants, Scholarship and Financial Aid section of the catalog for the  [link: https://catalog.park.edu/content.php?catoid=5&navoid=490#student-attendance-participation-policy-for-financial-aid] Student Attendance Policy for Financial Aid.

You will receive a grade “WF” if you fail to participate in the course activities through the end of the term but do not officially withdraw from the course. A grade of “WF” receives the same grade points as an “F” but may affect your financial aid for students receiving financial aid or visa status for international students (same as a "W").
