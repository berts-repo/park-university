Answers

Q1: How do I download and install OpenJDK/JavaFX?

Installing OpenJDK is super easy and quick, much easier than setting up Oracle's JDK. Installing JavaFX is just downloading a zip file, unzip, and copy a folder. See instructions and screenshots in  [link: https://canvas.park.edu/courses/71018/files/9524373?verifier=J4x3RefTS4wQvBzZIzSMzgmmP71ZANISiUtlEupU&wrap=1] Install_OpenJDK_JavaFX.docx.

Q2: How do I know if I have OpenJDK/JDK installed on my computer and which version?

On Windows, Eclipse Adoptium OpenJDK is installed under C:\Program Files\Eclipse Foundation\jdk-version.num.num-jvmversion\.  Oracle's JDK is normally installed under C:\Program Files\Java\jdk-version.num.num . Java compiler javac.exe is under the bin subfolder for both.

Mac users may check out online posts like this one  [link: https://stackoverflow.com/questions/22539779/how-to-tell-if-jre-or-jdk-is-installed] https://stackoverflow.com/questions/22539779/how-to-tell-if-jre-or-jdk-is-installed 

Q3: How do I configure JavaFX?

We need to configure JavaFX with our Java IDE before building GUI programs. Please see  [link: https://canvas.park.edu/courses/71018/files/9524130?verifier=LyilrCxQiLKtyLnfvclgoeKdEiLDf5IpRWj8rBlz&wrap=1] ConfigureJavaFX.docx for configuring JavaFX with Eclipse on your own machine. Unit 6 has two videos showing how to configure JavaFX with Eclipse and with VS Code.

This guide should work too if you use Park's Virtual Lab. Make sure you get the location of JavaFX correct. It may be at C:\Program Files\javafx-sdk-version.num.num in the virtual desktop.

Q7: How to use Park's Virtual Lab?

Access the Virtual Lab from your computer/device:  [link: https://parkuniversity.freshdesk.com/support/solutions/articles/6000177801-how-do-i-access-the-computer-lab-applications-from-a-personal-computer-device-] https://parkuniversity.freshdesk.com/support/solutions/articles/6000177801-how-do-i-access-the-computer-lab-applications-from-a-personal-computer-device- (Note: you should use "CS_Lab" under Desktop tab, not "Student Lab". Q8: How do I get my files in and out of the virtual lab?

Because it's a virtual lab, you should always save a copy of your work as it may not be saved between sessions.

To upload files to the virtual machine or download them to your local device, click open a black half dot at the top middle of the virtual machine screen to see a menu and use the Upload or Download buttons:
