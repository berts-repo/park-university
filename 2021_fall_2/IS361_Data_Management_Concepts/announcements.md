## Practice Exam Reminder and Cautionary Advice for Assignment 6
*Posted: 2021-11-23T19:42:02Z*

Dear Students,

Let me remind you that this course will have the Final Exams proctored by Honorlock. To familiarize yourself with the procedure there is a Practice Exam under the Honorlock item in the left menu of Canvas. This exam will be available for students until the end of Week 7 (next week). Take a time to get used to the new Proctor System before this deadline. The Practice Exam will not be available on the last Week of Finals.

This message is also a cautionary advice regarding Assignment 6. All questions that require the creation of a table using a SQL command should use the version of the CREATE TABLE command that uses a SELECT command afterward. This command is explained in the document in Unit 6 named "Lecture - Data Warehousing" and the recording for Live! Session 6.

Under no circumstances you should use the CREATE TABLE command that defines table fields one by one. The assignment deals with the creation of tables of a data warehouse from a transactional database, so the fields for tables in the data warehouse do not need to be constructed from scratch but taken from the other tables. That is why we use the CREATE TABLE command that uses the SELECT afterward.

One more thing, some of you may try to use an old database from previous homework to try the CREATE TABLE with the SELECT command. Unfortunately, the command does not work in Access. It works in other SQL variants. That is why I did not provide a database or request one. All that is needed is the command. If you want to test if the SELECT works, just test the SELECT, and then when you are happy that you have the information, just write at the top CREATE TABLE name with the name of the table to be created and submit that command as the answer in the assignment.

## Avoid the use of JOIN clauses in Discussion Thread 5 
*Posted: 2021-11-15T18:29:00Z*

Dear Students,

For the discussion thread this week, please avoid the use of inner join or any other JOIN clause in your queries. While this is a valid command, I want to see that all students know how to use the WHERE clause to establish the links between tables, and add all involved tables in the FROM clause. This is explained in the document with examples under Week 5. Use the WHERE clause to establish the link between tables and do not use any JOIN clause.

## On Discussion Threads …
*Posted: 2021-10-18T20:22:33Z*

Dear Students,

The main purpose of the discussion threads is to help students to understand the course material. As the Instructor, I will challenge you on your answers and I will try to elicit from you a critical response. I will also give you suggestions on what to correct or improve. All other students are also encouraged to offer their critical advice. If the comments to your work appear too harsh, please do not take them personally. Think of them the same way you would think of a physician treating your wounds. The treatment is not done with the aim to hurt you but to heal you. And, like good physicians, we aim to heal all our patients. On more than one occasion we will also tell you when your work is fine and that will be equivalent to the physician giving you a clean bill of health.

Discussion threads are used as a mechanism to assess your learning, to find the difficulties you may have, and to try to solve them. By answering the questions and replying to the instructor’s and students’ comments you will show your degree of understanding of the material and your interest in the course. You should consider the requirement of one posting per discussion thread by Thursday and a response to other comments per discussion thread by Sunday only as a minimum requirement and it should not be considered the standard to which one would measure participation. Beyond this minimum quantitative requirement, the most important measure of participation in the course is quality. Quality on postings counts.

Responses that are succinct, general, vague, out of point or mere agreeable (or disagreeable) to some point are not helpful in your learning process and therefore will not be graded favorably. Equivalently, not answering to questions from Instructors and other students will be considered as a lack of interest or care in the educational process.

Please take all these remarks just as cautionary and continue with the same enthusiasm and drive I have seen the first week. Let us try to take full advantage of our discussion threads and every other resource we have at our disposal in the online environment. Give it a good try!

Finally, three procedural notes:

Please, when answering to a discussion thread for the first time use the Response link at the top of the discussion thread. Use this link only once per discussion thread. If you want to make further responses to your own topic, to add, respond to, or correct something previously said, please use the Response link on the previous posting. Do not create a new thread. There should be only one discussion thread per student.
 

As part of the rules of the discussion thread, you must comment on at least another student’s posting. The responses you made in a thread initiated by you do not count. These are just natural replies in a discussion. We encourage you to make these replies because they add to the discussion, but we also want that you comment on other threads, initiated by somebody else, at least one other somebody.

Make a habit on late Mondays of checking the discussion thread of the previous week. I will be adding some comments when I grade it in the morning.
