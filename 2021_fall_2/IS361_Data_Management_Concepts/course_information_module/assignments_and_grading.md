Discussions

(Eight discussions equal 27% of final grade)

In Unit 1-8, you will participate in discussions by responding to the discussion questions provided in each unit. Discussion posts are designed to provide you with the opportunity respond to unit materials while demonstrating your ability to communicate effectively with a group.

Initial Posts - Your initial discussion posts should directly address all parts of the discussion requirement.  Initial discussion posts should include a minimum of 150 words. Post your response to the topics by Thursday, 11:59 PM CT.

Response Posts - A minimum of one additional response post to your classmates or instructor is required by Sunday, 11:59 PM CT. This additional response should not be part of your own discussion thread.  Responses to your classmates or instructor should provide thoughtful insight to the topic, include information that directly relates to the course content and moves the group discussion forward in an effective manner.  Each response post should include a minimum of 100 words.

Each post must demonstrate proper grammar, spelling, word usage and effective communication practices.

Additional Instructions for Discussion Threads

The purpose of discussion threads is to provide a venue in which students and instructors can talk about the issues that are important on topics being covered during the week. Most discussion thread questions require the student to present an example of a topic, so students have the responsibility of getting familiar with the material, and post answers to the discussion threads based on that knowledge. Their understanding may not be correct all the time, but the fact that is posted makes easier for instructor and other students to point out its deficiencies and the way to correct them. This is pretty much the same environment as we have in a physical classroom, where students respond to questions for all to hear and further discussion leads everybody to a better understanding.

Students should read carefully the instructions for each discussion thread. The kind of questions and expected responses for discussion threads may be different from week to week to suit the topic being discussed.

You are expected to read all postings by the instructor and students.

At the end of the unit, the discussion should be closed, so that you can concentrate on participating on the new discussion for the current unit.

If you want to continue discussions throughout the course, a Virtual Cafe will be provided in the Course Information module as a class lounge.

You are expected to communicate professionally using netiquette at all times. Netiquette is a common set of standards that are expected when individuals communicate online. If you don't know what netiquette is, click on Park Policies in the left-hand navigation menu.

Final Exam

(The Final Exam equals 35% of final grade.)

In Unit 8, a Proctored Final Exam will be administered and due by Sunday at 11:59pm (CST). Failure to complete the final exam will result in an automatic WF in the course.

The final exam for this course has the following characteristics:

It is proctored.

It is a 2-hour final that can only be taken once. (This is school and department policy)

It is closed books, closed notes

No use of calculators is allowed.

No use of websites allowed, other than the exam itself.

Multiples sheets of scratch paper are allowed.

No use of additional software is allowed

Bathroom breaks are not allowed. Students should take care of their needs before starting the exam.

Only the Chrome Browser is allowed.

Each question in the exam will be presented to the student individually and students will not be able to go back to a question after it was answered and submitted.

Additional Examinations Notes:
Students may be flagged and warned about infractions while taking the exam, but they should be allowed to complete it within its allocated time period.
