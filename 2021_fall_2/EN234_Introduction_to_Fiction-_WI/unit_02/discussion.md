Hello class,

I am working with prompt 1: what were early writers of fiction experimenting with?

While reading the first chapter of "Miguel de Cervante’s Don Quixote," (1612) I originally thought of the gentleman, later to adopt the name Don Quixote, as a poor idealist. With the author referring to him as someone who often neglected his property work, to read heroic books, and he who spent 75% perfect of his income on food. After I re-examined this weeks readings, I tried to looked at literature as someone from the 16th century would have. I looked at it as the someone who was able to afford fine clothing, who had a housekeeper, and who could afford a horse.

In Kate Chopin “Desiree’s Baby,” (1893) The author leaves a the reading viewing Desiree's husband as romantic, "fell in love, as if struck by a pistol shot," and kind, as he hasn't punished anyone even for skipping out on work. We go through most of text with how happy Desiree is with her life. Around the time Desiree's baby was 3 months old, the author switches the mood of the husband, and the reader starts to interpret that Desiree might have been partly black. We see the shift in character from her husband as this realization sinks in, burning all her possessions. 

The authors are writing with a point of view from the cultural standpoint of a particular period in time. We see in both the protagonist from my previously described readings, come from comfortable financial background, both having servants. We see personality painted for both of these characters. It is just important to keep in mind that culture and perspective changes over time.
