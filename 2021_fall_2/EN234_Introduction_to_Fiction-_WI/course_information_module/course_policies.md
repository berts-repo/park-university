# Course Policies

Online Participation

Because this online course is the equivalent of a face-to-face course, you should plan to spend the same amount of time per week (approximately 6 hours). A class week is defined as the period between Monday morning (begins week) and Sunday night (ends week). Students must participate in academically related activity on a weekly basis in order to be marked present in an online class. Examples of academically-related activities include but are not limited to: contributing to an online discussion, completing a quiz or exam, completing an assignment, initiating contact with a faculty member to ask a course related question, or using any of the learning management system tools. If an emergency forces you to miss several class periods, contact me as soon as possible so we can discuss your options.

Positive Learning Environment

This isn't a lecture course. We'll be working together to explore various literary texts and to improve your writing, which means that you need to participate actively in discussions and exercises (See “Attendance and Participation”). While literature can inspire strong opinions and may touch on personal experiences and beliefs, please be civil in expressing your ideas and respect your classmates' contributions. Please review the rules of  [link: http://www.albion.com/netiquette/corerules.html] Netiquette.

Office hours, Email, and Writing

Please feel free to drop in during my virtual office hours or to make an appointment for other times. I check my email regularly, so don’t hesitate to send me a message if you have questions or concerns about the class. Use email for private messages to me or your classmates, and use the conference area for public messages. When sending an email, please use the Email tool within the course itself (the Inbox tab in the left column of the page). In addition, be sure to follow formal letter writing etiquette (i.e. include a salutation and sign your full name and section at the bottom of the email).

This is an English class, so make sure you use complete, grammatically correct sentences in your posts and assignments. I encourage you to show your personality and adopt a more casual tone in your discussion posts, but you should always remember that this is a professional environment.

Accessibility

I am committed to creating a course that is inclusive in its design. If you encounter barriers, please let me know immediately so that we can determine if there is an adjustment that can be made or if an accommodation might be needed to overcome the limitations of the design. I am always happy to consider creative solutions as long as they do not compromise the intent of the assessment or learning activity. You are also welcome to contact the disability resource office to begin this conversation or to establish accommodations for this or other courses. I welcome feedback that will assist me in improving the usability and experience for all students.
