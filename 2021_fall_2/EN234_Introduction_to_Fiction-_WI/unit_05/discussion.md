Hello class,

what I wanted to talk about in A Farewell to Arms is the point of view that Hemmingway took when writing this story. The narration takes the view of the protagonist Frederic Henry. While reading the the text you often get a sense that the narration is taken from Henry's personal journal, using past tense and the first person point of view; "then we turned off and commenced to climb into the
hills again," is a good example when they are driving to the town where they first get hit by the bunker shells and take the first injuries. Then the rest of the time while reading the story, like, we are taking place in the conversation, with the narration taking on the second person, putting the reader in the conversation; "Thank you. You keep it. It's for you." 

I have found it very interesting how Hemmingway has been able to take the reader and put them into the story, but on the other had it feels like a story being told to you like it has already happened. This is unique way of writing, that I would have never noticed, if It wasn't for critically analyzing the text. This in turn has made the story more immersive, it sets a different tone, it allows Hemmingway to express the story with different outlets. I do not know if this is something intentional Hemmingway has put into his writing, but I do know he is one of the most well known authors and I would put money on it was on purpose.
