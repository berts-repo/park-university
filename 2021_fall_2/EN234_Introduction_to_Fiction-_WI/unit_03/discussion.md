I will be responding to prompt 1: Choose one reading from this unit. How does the point of view affect how we understand the story and the characters? How does the narrative structure (chronological, back and forth in time) affect the story?

In "The Adventure of the Speckled Band" Doyle writes the story from the point of view of Dr. Watson. Watson and Sherlock Holmes are not just colleagues but old friends, because of this, the tone is set for Sherlock Holmes in a highly regarded fashion, from the point of view of friend and admiring colleague. With narrations like "working as he did rather for the love of his art than for the acquirement of wealth, he refused to associate himself with any investigation which did not tend towards the unusual, and even the fantastic," we have a esteemed view of Sherlock Holmes and his capabilities. 

With the story being written from the point of view of Holmes the narrative structure is established in a way that the reader doesn't know what Sherlock Holmes is thinking, but rather the unfolding of events as Dr. Watson sees them. If we were to have the narration from the perspective of Holmes, it would effect the timeline and take a lot of the mystery out of the story, because Sherlock Holmes had the crime solved, or at least suspected the conclusion early in the story. In the last paragraphs Holmes states: "My dear Watson, I had come to these conclusions before ever I had entered his room" and  "how dangerous it always is to reason from insufficient data." 

As crime fiction, the reader can really see how the point of view effects the narrative structure. The chronological order plays an important roll in the style of this literature. The genre wouldn't be the same if we had the solution before the end of the story.
