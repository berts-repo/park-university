# Unit 8: Lecture

Genre and Experimental Fiction

We’ve nearly reached the end! Hopefully, over the course of this semester you have come to appreciate the variety and complexities of fiction. In this unit, you will get a chance to read some fictional stories that stray from the norm (to varying degrees). Experimental Fiction is all about innovation and pushing/blurring boundaries. Visit  [link: http://www.mapliterary.org/on-experimental-fiction.html] Map Literary to learn more about experimental fiction.

Writing and Revising

This unit, you will be revising your rough draft based on feedback from your instructor and one of your classmates. But you should also refer back to the prompt and rubric to make sure you have addressed all the necessary elements.

In revising your paper, you should refer back to those tips/questions I asked in Unit 2. You should also consult the Purdue Online Writing Lab (OWL) for tips and strategies about how to use and cite sources effectively and ethically. They provide clear instructions on how to use MLA formatting through their  [link: https://owl.purdue.edu/owl/research_and_citation/mla_style/mla_formatting_and_style_guide/mla_formatting_and_style_guide.html] MLA Formatting and Style Guide.

I also recommend you return to the  [link: https://canvas.park.edu/courses/64138/pages/assignments-and-grading] Assignments and Grading module and review the links about plagiarism. This final paper is worth a lot of points, and you don’t want to lose any because you didn’t paraphrase appropriately or you forgot to cite a source.

This Unit’s Author

It is often very helpful to learn a bit about the author and his or her cultural context when reading a piece of literature. As such, each unit, I will include links to information about the authors and the context of the readings.

Read about Lydia Davis in  [link: https://www.newyorker.com/magazine/2014/03/17/long-story-short] an article from The New Yorker and in an  [link: https://www.theparisreview.org/interviews/6366/lydia-davis-art-of-fiction-no-227-lydia-davis] interview in the Paris Review.

For the other authors you might have read this unit, google their names! It is very likely that you will find biographies and interviews and readings from any or all of them.
