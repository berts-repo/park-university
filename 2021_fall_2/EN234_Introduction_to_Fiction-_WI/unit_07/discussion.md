I will be examining the second page, on page 4 of Fun Home. In the top narration the author, Alison Bechdel’s makes a Greek mythology reference, and we see this becomes a theme throughout the first chapter; "considering the fate of Icarus after he flouted his father's advice and flew so close to the sun his wings melted, perhaps some dark humor is intended." The author previously established how he needs perfect balance when playing airplane, and the Greek mythology reference actually has a double meaning, possible referring to the "dark humor is intended" The caption of the boy, "uh-oh!" is him loosing balance and possibly falling to the floor, although this is not illustrated, or written. The boy however does state "again!" before his father gets distracted by the dirty carpet, which leads into the second possible reference. The tone of the first chapter is set by his father in this next two illustrations, "go get the vacuum cleaner" and "and then get me my tack and hammer, that strip of molding is coming loose." The narration was foreshadowing, how his father is always putting the housework before anything else. The last narration also depicts this as well, "his greatest achievement, arguably, was hi monomaniacal restoration of our old house." The author describes his obsession with the house as 'monomaniacal' and the narration also says arguably, as his father most likely does not see his own fault.

Just from the second page of the book, we see the tone of the father begin to be developed, there is also the theme of the authors writing style being implemented as well. Without referring to more of the chapter, we get a nice window into how the context of the book will develop.
