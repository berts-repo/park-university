# Unit 7: Overview

Introduction

Genre and Graphic Fiction

In this unit, you will do the following: 

Read the Overview and Lecture

Read the assigned texts

Write and submit your reading journal

Participate in the discussion (1 initial post, 2 responses)

Write and submit a Rough Draft of your Core Assessment paper

Write and submit a Peer Review for one of your classmates

Unit Learning Outcomes

At the completion of this unit, the learner will be able to:

Compare literary genres. (CLO 1 and 2)

Argue a claim about a fictional text. (CLO 3)

Find appropriate sources to help analyze fictional text. (CLO 3)

Support the claim with evidence from an outside source. (CLO 3)

Readings

Chapter 1 of Alison Bechdel’s  [link: https://www.google.com/books/edition/Fun_Home/eq0n9Ck79ysC?hl=en&gbpv=0] Fun Home  (This link takes you to the preview of the book in Google Books - just read chapter 1!)

Instructor’s choice or choose one Web Comic from the [link: https://bookriot.com/must-read-webcomics/]  list and read a 3-4 examples of that one comic

Assignments

Reading Journal (15 points) Submit by 11:59 p.m. on Tuesday, CT.

Discussion (20 points) First post due 11:59 p.m., Wednesday, CT.  Respond to two or more classmates by 11:59 p.m., Friday, CT.

Student Opinion of Teaching Survey

During this unit you will have access to the online Student Opinion of Teaching Survey for your courses. When you open the Survey page, you will see “Load Student Opinion of Teaching Survey in a new window” – please click this button. In the new window that opens you will see a list of courses for which you have surveys to complete. Each course evaluation should take no longer than 15 minutes to complete. You are be able to access your surveys multiple times to edit answers or to complete saved surveys. Surveys must be completed by the last day of the course, 11:59 p.m., central.

Please be assured that your responses are presented anonymously to your instructors, and will be compiled for the university via a third party provider, Campus Labs. All student responses will be summarized and reported to your instructor(s) after the term is over and grades have been posted.
