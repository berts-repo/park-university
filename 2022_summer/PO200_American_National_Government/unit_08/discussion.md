Class,

I agree with Justice Scalia’s stance on cameras in the court room, there should be no cameras in the court room. I particularly liked his reasoning, that these are in depth constitutional procedures that a 30 second video clip cant portray. (CSPAN) The court room already has reporters and a public audio transcript in place, there is no need for the cameras during a Supreme Court argument. Where cameras, maybe, should be in place is the when the court decides to rule on a case, granting "cert". There are already checks and balances in place regarding the public, agencies and the media put pressure on congress to put certain bills into act, this same kind of public pressure should possibly put on the supreme court as well.

The federal court system automatically gets first dibs on cases involving the US and foreign affairs, and cases involving multiple states. The court is currently made up of 9 Justices who decide to rule on a case by the Rule of Four. After a case is being revied by the court, each argumenting party gives a brief, usually about an hour. After the brief there is the oral argument where all the inquiry happens. Then then Justices go and decide on the ruling, and this part is not public.
