Explain the factors which influence participation in politics

The textbook points out an important factor in participation that I wanted emphasize: "Forty-nine percent of the voting-age public cast a ballot in the 1924 presidential contest, the same percentage as in 1996. Turnout in presidential elections in the 1960s was over 60 percent. More voters were mobilized during this period of political upheaval in which people focused on issues of race relations, social welfare, and the Vietnam War (Piven & Cloward, 2000)". This is a statistically valid way to increase voter turnout through chaos and malicious intent. I think it's important for people to be aware of this tactic. 

Culture and social are other ways to influence participation in politics. Being in the Information era, we are seeing social media having a huge influence on politics. The actions and words of candidates can easily be taken out of context.
