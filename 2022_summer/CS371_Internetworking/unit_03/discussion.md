When dealing with stateful ipv6 address autoconfiguration (DHCPv6) the network's nodes use a series of protocols, ARP and ICMP to determine the IPv6 address. The IPv6 address's are pulled from a pool of address's that are configured on the cisco router. The pool is just a range of address's in you're own network, each address has a configured "lease," and the address will be recirculated when the lease expires. To keep the autoconfiguration form using address's that are already in use there has to be exclusions configured as well, these include any IP address reserved for special purposes, the DNS server, the default-gateway.
