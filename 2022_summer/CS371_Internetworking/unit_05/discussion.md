wlan interference is a common issue, and there are three main types of wi-fi interference, co-channel, adjacent-channel, and non-wifi interference. (Metageek) Co-channel interference is really only problem when there are too many devices on the same channel. Adjacent-channel interference causes more problems when the wlan channels overlap. Each channel on a 2.4GHz wlan are 22 MHz wide. The 802.11b identifies 11 channels in North America, with a 5MHz gap in-between each channel. This diagram shows only 20Mhz for each channel, but according to the textbook it is 22GHz for the USA.

The other interference issues, non-wifi interference, are caused by things like microwaves, analog cameras and cordless phones.

 

Reference:
