## Instructions

“ Historically, physical and cyber vulnerability assessments have been performed independently. This independent evaluation omits important aspects of the integrated system, where the impacts resulting from malicious or opportunistic attacks are not easily known or understood…While security analysts working in the physical security domain have been performing standardized vulnerability assessments for decades, cyber security vulnerability analyses are still, in most respects, in early stages.” (MacDonald, et al., 2013).

Your Core Assessment for the course will be supported by completing a  [link: https://canvas.park.edu/courses/74841/files/10201121/download?verifier=XrrxtgBB6AvuVVbQF06OEw3zdbvhUCZV9Mg479YT&wrap=1] Cybersecurity Analysis [link: https://canvas.park.edu/courses/74841/files/10201121/download?verifier=XrrxtgBB6AvuVVbQF06OEw3zdbvhUCZV9Mg479YT&wrap=1]  of your business, institution, or organization, which is comprised of 2 parts:

Part 1

You may use your current place of employment, your institution or agency, faith-based organization or business to administer the:

Cybersecurity Controls Checklist (CCC)

Cybersecurity Threat/Vulnerability Assessment (CTVA)

It is essential that the CCC and CTVA are completed fully, before moving on to Part 2, as the information and data collected will be submitted as attachments to the Executive Summary document. The content of these two instruments will provide a clear threat and vulnerability potential of the entity you examined. The results will be used as a supporting resource to establish Risk, based on the impact/likelihood scales.   

Risk = Impact x Likelihood

The last element of the CCC and CTVA is the assessment of Probability, which incorporates 9 areas of interest which include:

Human Threats
General Threats
Identification Authorization Threats
Privacy Threats
Integrity/Accuracy Threats
Access Control Threats
Repudiation Threats
Legal Threats
Reliability of Service Threats 

Part 2

 After completing the CCC and CTVA instruments above, as part of the Executive Summary, you will Recommend Security Risk Remediation Actions based on Cost, Benefit, and Risk. An example is provided that you can use as a guide. Once the you've completed the analytical instruments discussed above, you will prepare an Executive Summary. 

Your Executive Summary is based on the results of the Cybersecurity Controls Checklist, and the Cybersecurity Threat/Vulnerability Assessment. Your summary includes a brief description of the issues and/or areas of concern identified the likely impact on the entity assessed or evaluated if not addressed.

It includes the Cybersecurity Controls Checklist and the Cybersecurity Threat/Vulnerability Assessment as attachments.

The Executive Summary outlines a set of detailed recommendations for addressing issues and/or areas of concern identified during the assessment.

Together, your Recommend Security Risk Remediation Actions and Executive Summary should be 4 - 5 pages using APA Writing Style.
