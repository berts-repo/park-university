## Welcome to Unit 1
*Posted: 2023-05-20T18:24:42Z*

In unit 1 we begin to immerse with the plethora of terminology to help us gain familiarity with the overall course content.  You will note that you must log into Cengage where a great deal of the course content is located.  Initially the unit 1 quiz was setup there and I reverted it to an automated quiz located directly in Canvas to provide everyone the first week to familiarize with this amazing Cengage platform.  In future weeks you will take your quiz there and your score will auto-populate in Canvas.

During this week you should gain awareness of the key characteristics of information security – Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability.  You will discover how these early-on characteristics have evolved to meet the changing information technology landscape as well as the emerging threats directed at technology.  We will discuss management and leadership and while not in our text or not very accessible I typically revert to a differentiation I coined in the mid-90s – We manage things and lead people. 

The primary leader in a cybersecurity world is typically the Information Security Officer or ISO.  These are the front line leaders that protect our information technology structure and are often believed to possess a varied and wide set of skills to mitigate and respond to a vast number of issues, which can often present in a form not familiar to anyone.

It is important to understand the different areas of security – Physical Security, Operations Security, Communications Security, Cybersecurity, and Network Security.  While these are each defined differently they are all necessary and linked to shape and form the enterprise-wide security umbrella for our technology.  We will discuss various models (e.g., CNSS model) so that you have a conceptual understanding that there are strengths and weaknesses associated with all of them.

It is important to understand the various “categories of threats” as depicted in Table 1-1 of our textbook.  By understanding these different categories you can begin to conceptualize and visualize the measures we take to mitigate risk.  Chances are everyone in this course has been a victim to one or more of these threats.

At the end of the unit we dive right into leadership and management.  One of our discussion questions will talk about the unique functions of information security management – Planning, Policy, Programs, Protection, People, and Project Management.

This will be one of those rare IT-like courses where you can come without any former building block knowledge, immerse in the education, and for those that apply themselves you will leave with a vast range of IT cognitive awareness.

## Plagiarism and Academic Dishonesty
*Posted: 2023-05-20T18:22:42Z*

Very important topic.  Each term I seem to have at least one student that attempts to submit a paper of a former student for their core assessment.  By doing this you will most likely fail the entire course because of the value of the entire assignment.  Any act of academic dishonesty will be reported to the University, which can impact your current term grades or lead to expulsion.

I want to ensure everyone is absolutely clear on plagiarism and academic dishonesty.  I take any form of academic dishonesty very seriously and will not accept any form of academic dishonesty.  Plagiarism involves the use of quotations without quotation marks, the use of quotations without indication of the source, the use of another's idea without acknowledging the source, the submission of a paper, laboratory report, project, or class assignment (any portion of such) prepared by another person, or incorrect paraphrasing.

Furthermore Wikipedia or similar source is not an acceptable source for collegiate material.

Citations

If you cite or quote material in your weekly discussion posting ensure you cite the text correctly using APA style.  You cannot copy text directly from an internet source and paste it into your discussion response without properly quoting and citing the source.
