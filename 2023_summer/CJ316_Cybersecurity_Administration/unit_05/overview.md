# Unit 5: Overview

Introduction

This Unit tackles two important aspects of Risk Management; Assessing Risk and Treating Risk. We begin by defining risk management and its role in the organization and demonstrates how to use risk management techniques to identify and prioritize risk factors for information assets. The risk management model presented here assesses risk based on the likelihood of adverse events and the effects on information assets when events occur. This unit also presents essential risk mitigation strategy options and opens the discussion on controlling risk. Students learn how to identify risk control classification categories, use existing conceptual frameworks to evaluate risk controls, and formulate a cost-benefit analysis. They also learn how to maintain and perpetuate risk controls and conclude with a brief discussion of how to document the results of the risk identification process.

Unit Learning Outcomes

At the conclusion of the unit, the learner will be able to:

ULO 1: Define risk management and its role in the organization (CLO5)

ULO 2: Describe risk management techniques to identify and prioritize risk factors for information assets (CLO5)

ULO 3: Explain how risk is assessed based on the likelihood of adverse events and the effects on information assets when events occur (CLO5)

ULO 4: Discuss the strategy options used to treat risk and be prepared to select from them when given background information (CLO5)

ULO 5: Evaluate control alternatives under the defense risk treatment strategy and formulate a cost-benefit analysis (CBA) using existing conceptual frameworks (CLO5)

ULO 6: Explain how to maintain and perpetuate controls (CLO5)

ULO 7: Describe popular approaches used in the industry to manage risk (CLO5)
