Scenario-Based Discussions

Read the scenario provided and respond to the discussion question relevant to the scenario. After responding to the discussion question, read and provide a substantive reply to the responses of at least two (2) other students. See the example below or ask your instructor what is meant by substantive reply. 
Note: The scenario and discussion questions are available in the text.

Sample Substantive Discussion Reply:

Pat, while I agree with your list of elements that would contribute to identifying a perpetrators criminal motivation, I think it’s worth considering the three components of modus operandi, or method of operation briefly described in our text. In addition, when we looked at how each component could be discounted individually, as a class most agreed that when two or more were present, the criminal motivation could be more easily revealed. The way I broke them down was as factors that facilitate the offender’s perpetration of the crime, such as use of a weapon, the use of a power outage that disarmed alarms, or the creating a hoax to evacuate the building or facility burgled; factors that facilitate obscuring the offenders identity, such as wear a disguise or a mask, creating smoke or other efforts to limit daylight visibility, or damaging external lighting or cameras; and factors that facilitate the offender’s escape, such as having a vehicle waiting, or jamming an exit door with a block of wood, creating a distraction by which the offender could potential blend in with others exiting the building or facility. 

If you'll recall how the text differentiated between MO and Signature, you might see how your post was focused more on the pre or post criminal activity.  Did you note how none of the activities facilitated the crime, hid their identity, or aided in thier escape? 

Thanks 
Drew

Practical Assignments

The objective of the Practical Assignments is to give students an opportunity to examine the information security arena from an experiential perspective. In these assignments students must select and complete at least two of the practical assignment options from a list provided. Each assignment is designed to provide students with hands on experience focused on introducing aspects of the management of information security through research, analysis and critical thinking.

Due Date

Practical Assignments must be submitted before Sunday at 11:59 P.M., CT.

Core Assessment

Your Core Assessment for the course will be supported by completing a  [link: https://canvas.park.edu/courses/74841/files/10201121/download?verifier=XrrxtgBB6AvuVVbQF06OEw3zdbvhUCZV9Mg479YT&wrap=1] Cybersecurity Analysis [link: https://canvas.park.edu/courses/74841/files/10201121/download?verifier=XrrxtgBB6AvuVVbQF06OEw3zdbvhUCZV9Mg479YT&wrap=1]  of your business, institution, or organization, which is comprised of 2 parts:

Part 1

You may use your current place of employment, your institution or agency, faith-based organization or business to administer the:

Cybersecurity Controls Checklist (CCC)

Cybersecurity Threat/Vulnerability Assessment (CTVA)

It is essential that the CCC and CTVA are completed fully, before moving on to Part 2, as the information and data collected will be submitted as attachments to the Executive Summary document. The content of these two instruments will provide a clear threat and vulnerability potential of the entity you examined. The results will be used as a supporting resource to establish Risk, based on the impact/likelihood scales.   

Risk = Impact x Likelihood

The last element of the CCC and CTVA is the assessment of Probability, which incorporates 9 areas of interest which include:

Human Threats
General Threats
Identification Authorization Threats
Privacy Threats
Integrity/Accuracy Threats
Access Control Threats
Repudiation Threats
Legal Threats
Reliability of Service Threats 

Part 2

 After completing the CCC and CTVA instruments above, as part of the Executive Summary, you will Recommend Security Risk Remediation Actions based on Cost, Benefit, and Risk. An example is provided that you can use as a guide. Once the you've completed the analytical instruments discussed above, you will prepare an Executive Summary. 

Your Executive Summary is based on the results of the Cybersecurity Controls Checklist, and the Cybersecurity Threat/Vulnerability Assessment. Your summary includes a brief description of the issues and/or areas of concern identified the likely impact on the entity assessed or evaluated if not addressed.

It includes the Cybersecurity Controls Checklist and the Cybersecurity Threat/Vulnerability Assessment as attachments.

The Executive Summary outlines a set of detailed recommendations for addressing issues and/or areas of concern identified during the assessment.

Together, your Recommend Security Risk Remediation Actions and Executive Summary should be 4 - 5 pages using APA Writing Style.
