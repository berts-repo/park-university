Carrying capacity is the maximum population an environment can sustain for a certain population, while the limiting factor is the factor that restricts the population from growing. (Simon 409) An example of this is the carrying capacity of elephants, at 20 elephants, depends on the limiting factor of water, that is enough water in the area to sustain the 20 elephants

 

Simon, Eric; Dickey, Jean; Reece, Jane. "Campbell Essential Biology." 7th ed., Pearson, 2018
