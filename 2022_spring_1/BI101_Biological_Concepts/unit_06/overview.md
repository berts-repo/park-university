# Unit 6: Overview

Genetics

Have you seen photographs of Kirk Douglas and his son, Michael? If you have, you know that they look very similar. Kirk Douglas gave Michael half of his genes; his mother gave him the other half. One of the characteristics that we automatically notice on both father and son is the cleft chin (dimple in the chin). This particular trait was obviously passed down from father to son. Think about how much you look like your mother or father. What if you were an exact copy of one of them?

Genes are units of instruction for heritable traits. Each has its own location (locus) on one type of chromosome. Different forms of the gene are called alleles. 

Diploid cells have two copies of each gene, usually one inherited from each of two parents, on homologous chromosomes. This unit will examine the history of genetics as well as some basic illustrations of genetic inheritance.

Core Learning Outcomes

Compare and contrast the processes of mitosis and meiosis with reference to the movement of chromosomes and their significance in the transmission of genetic information.

Describe the structure of DNA and illustrate its significance in determining the composition, characteristics, reproduction, and behavior of an organism.

Unit Objectives:

Explain the difference between the genotype and phenotype,

Explain the relationship between an allele and a gene

Describe what incomplete dominance, complete dominance, and codominance are and provide an example of each,

Demonstrate your understanding of genetic crosses using a Punnett Square.

Comprehend the genetics of inherited disorders and provide examples of genetic disorders

Describe what it means when a trait is said to be sex-linked.

Apply an understanding of genetics to real world issues
