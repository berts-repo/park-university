1. With respect to recessively inherited disorders, why might it be a bad idea to marry your first cousin (genetically speaking)?

If the two parents share a particular gene, there is a higher chance of defect or disorder. If the parents share a trait then there is a higher chance the offspring will share the bad traits. There is about a 2-3% chance of a disorder in a normal birth, if two cousins merry it raises the chance by about 3%.  (Genetics Awareness Project)

2. Find an inherited disorder whose incidence is influenced by your answer above:

Briefly explain the disorder and the genetics involved.

Is there a population in which this disorder is more prevalent, and if so, why?

In consanguineous marriages there is a small increase in autosomal recessive disorders, like cystic fibrosis, sickle cell anemia, and Tay-Sachs disease. (Hamamy 2012) Sickle cell anemia has a higher rate in people from Africa, this is due to a mutation in the β-hemoglobin gene. The sickle hemoglobin is more prominent in the Middle East and India due to Malaria. (Solovieff et al., 2011)
