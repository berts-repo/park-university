1. Describe two specific examples of a commercially used GMO.

Corn and salmon are two examples of bioengineered food. Corn was modified to be more tolerant to herbicides and insects, while salmon was altered to grow faster. (List of Bioengineered Food)

2. What are the benefits of GM foods, in general?

More nutritious food, tastier food, disease and drought resistant plants, less use of pesticides, increased supply of food with reduced cost and longer shelf life, faster growing plants and animals, food with more desirable traits, such as potatoes that produce less of a cancer-causing substance when fried, medicinal foods that could be used as vaccines or other medicines. (U.S. National Library of Medicine)

3. What are the main issues of concern for human health?

Creation of foods that can cause an allergic or toxic reaction, unexpected or harmful genetic changes, inadvertent transfer of genes from one GM plant or animal to another plant or animal not intended for genetic modification, foods that are less nutritious. (U.S. National Library of Medicine)

4. Where do you stand on the use of GM foods?

I don't have an issue with GM foods. My concern lies with getting the proper nutrition. As farming is becoming more commercialized and the use of corn syrup and soy are the prominent crops. I am worried that we are replacing our variety of nutrition with blank calories as companies monopolize the farm.
