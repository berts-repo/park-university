In the light-dependent reaction of photosynthesis plants take in sunlight through chlorophyll, which is converted to ATP and NADPH. Photosynthesis also requires H2O, which comes from the plants roots, and CO2 which comes from the leaves. The entire process releases O2 back into the air through pores called stomata (Simon 109) 

Plants store the excess energy through the Calvin Cycle. In the chloroplast is a fluid called stoma, this fluid takes CO2, ATP and NADPH to produce a 3-carbon sugar (G3P). (Simon 117) The CO2 takes an electron from the H2O to produce G3P, which is used for energy to make glucose and the other biological macromolecules. (Simon 115)

 

Simon, Eric; Dickey, Jean; Reece, Jane. "Campbell Essential Biology." 7th ed., Pearson, 2018.
