The brief difference between aerobic and anaerobic is the production of ATP with oxygen, for aerobic, and without oxygen for anaerobic. Both of these take place in the first stage of cellular respiration, glycolysis. This stage require a 6-carbon glucose molecule and a phosphate group to produce pyruvic acid from ATP and NAD+. NADH comes from moving an electron from the phosphate group to NAD+. (Simon 96) In anaerobic the NAD+ is recycled NADH after the electrons are moved to pyruvic acid from the NADH, producing lactic acid. (Simon 101)

Our own cells might use the anaerobic respiration, if our bloodstream isn't carrying oxygen fast enough to the muscles. This can be caused by burning ATP faster then it can be produced.

Some meats like salami and chorizo sausage are made from the anaerobic respiration process. This is accomplished by adding bacteria, usually derived from lactic acid to the meat. This process preserves the meat and gives it a much longer shelf life. (Farrer)
