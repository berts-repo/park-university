# Unit 2: Overview

Cellular Energetics

Cells obtain energy from their surroundings and use it for thousands of tasks. 
Energy drives metabolism; the mechanical energy of moving cell parts, body parts, and even the whole organism; as well as the electrochemical work that must take place within the cell. 

Think about what you ate for breakfast this morning. What you eat provides the fuel that your body needs to function during the day. Putting food in your body is like putting gasoline in your car. But the cells in your body cannot use food directly for energy. The food must be converted into ATP, the energy currency of the cell. 

First, the carbohydrates you eat (sugars and starches) are converted to glucose. As glucose is broken down by a series of chemical reactions into carbon dioxide and water, energy is made available for ATP production. Other foods can also be broken down in order to produce ATP. That ATP is used to carry out all of the activities your body does during the day.
