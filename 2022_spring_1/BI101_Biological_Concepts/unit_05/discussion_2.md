Briefly explain what this genetic code is in general and how it works.

Genetic code is a set of three nucleotides in RNA, that binds to amino acids in the ribosomes, to make a specific    protein. This works when RNA polymerase recolonizes a nucleotide sequence called a "promoter," where RNAP codes and splices a gene of DNA to mRNA, until it reaches the "terminator" nucleotides. (Simon, 179)

What is meant by the universality of the genetic code?

All living things are made up of DNA.

Explain briefly what the advantages and disadvantages of this type of genetic code are to humans.

An advantage of this to humans is that it makes us up the genetics we like, big brains, butts and muscles. A disadvantage would include genetic diseases that get coded through the generations. In a different direction to something that has many advantages and disadvantages, that is gene manipulation with the CRISPR and the CAS9 protein. "CRISPR is a powerful tool for editing genomes, meaning it allows researchers to easily alter DNA sequences and modify gene function. It has many potential applications, including correcting genetic defects, treating and preventing the spread of diseases, and improving the growth and resilience of crops." (Vidyasagar, 2021) This same technology raises ethical concerns: including, making super humans, and segregation due to a class of people with hand selected "good" genes.
