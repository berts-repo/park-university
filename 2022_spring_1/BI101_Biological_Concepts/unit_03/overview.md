# Unit 3: Overview

Cells And Their Structure 

Cells are incredibly variable. Bacterial cells look rather simple compared to those found in our nervous system. 
However, the bacterial cell is the entire organism; it senses the environment, obtains food, eliminates waste, and reproduces. 
In comparison, a human body contains approximately 10 trillion cells. Each type of human cell is specialized to perform specific functions, but it still has to do many of the same things that a bacterial cell must do. 
Regardless of the differences in cells, there are certain structures that every cell must have and certain functions it must perform. This unit discusses the commonalities and some of their differences.

Core Learning Outcomes

Describe the structure and function of a cell and its various parts; compare the structures of bacterial, plant, and animal cells.

Describe the scientific method and be able to evaluate experiments using procedures and terminology of the scientific method.

Unit Objectives:

Identify the major eukaryotic cell organelles and explain the functions of each,

Describe the fluid mosaic model of the cell membrane and explain the importance of its characteristics,

Compare and contrast diffusion and osmosis,

Differentiate among bacterial, plant and animal cells based on appearance

Analyze and interpret results from experimental data,
