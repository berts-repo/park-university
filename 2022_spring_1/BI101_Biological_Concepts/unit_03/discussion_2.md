Cell membrane – is the semi soluble membrane that surrounds the cell. It is a barrier that protects the cytoplasm, while allowing nutrients and waste to pass through.

Nucleus – Is located in the center of the cell surrounded by a double membrane (nuclear envelope), which houses the chromosomes (DNA). The nucleus is responsible for regulation of the cell’s activities, including growth and metabolism. (Britannica)

Golgi apparatus – Is located near the nucleus, and contains stacked vesicles surrounded by a single membrane. It is responsible for the transport of the chemical product made by the cell’s cytosol. (Interactive Cell Model)

Cytoskeleton – Is located in the cytoplasm of a cell and is responsible for the cell structure through microtubules, microfilaments and intermediate fibers (Interactive Cell Model)
