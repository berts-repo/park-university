What does it mean for a membrane to be selectively permeable?

The cell membrane (plasma membrane) is an example of a selectively permeable membrane. It means the membrane only allows some molecules to pass in or out.

Select two specific components of the cell membrane and explain how each affects the permeability of the cell membrane. Meaning, what specific materials are regulated by the given component and how.

Receptor are usually transmembrane proteins, which means they have part of their mass on the inside and outside of the cell membrane. The part of the protein receptor exposed to the external side of the membrane, is often a binding site for Ligands. “Ligands can be hormones, neurotransmitters, lipoproteins, transferrins, extracellular matrix, and a wide variety of other molecules.” (Yeagle) The part of the receptor exposed to the cytoplasm is allowed for activation of intracellular proteins such as ion transporters, among other functionalities. (Yeagle)

Enzymes – Enzymes in the membrane can do the same thing they do in the cytoplasm of a cell: The enzymes transform a molecule into another form. (Adams) “Even today very little is known about how enzymes catalyze reactions in membranes.” (Tontonoz) 

Why is it important for a cell to regulate what molecules are able to pass into or out of the cell?

It how the cell maintains homeostasis and provides a way to obtain nutrients and dispose of waste.
