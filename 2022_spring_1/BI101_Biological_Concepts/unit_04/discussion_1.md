At the age of 42, how long has a woman’s remaining eggs been stalled in Prophase I?

     All the remaining eggs will still be in Prophase 1 of mitosis.

During both Anaphase I and Anaphase II of Meiosis, chromosomes are separated by a process called disjunction. What types of errors can occur at this time and what are the consequences?

     The type of error that can occur is called nondisjunction, and gametes with abnormal number of chromosomes are the result. (Simon 138)

How might your answers to the two questions above be related to the increased incidence of Down syndrome among children born to older women?
