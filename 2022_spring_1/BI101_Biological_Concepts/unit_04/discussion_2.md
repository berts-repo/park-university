Briefly describe the cell cycle and explain how it is regulated.

The cell cycle is the "lifespan" of a cell, from the "birth" of the cell until it undergoes cell division. This is controlled through the cell cycle control system that is regulated by proteins. (Simon 128)

How might disruption of the cell cycle lead to cancer?

Cancer begins when a cell mutates and divides uncontrollably. This occurs when a gene is mutated and causes the protein to receive the wrong signal. (Simon 129)

Find and describe the actions of a chemotherapeutic agent that directly targets the cell cycle. Be sure to explain specifically when during the cell cycle it works and what it interferes with.

Chemotherapy is the use of drugs to disrupt cell division and is used to treat malignant tumors. Some chemo drugs prevent cell division by interfering with the mitotic spindle, while other prevent the mitotic spindle from forming. (Simon 129) I predict chemotherapy would occur in the Prophase of mitosis, as this is when the miotic spindle begins to form.
