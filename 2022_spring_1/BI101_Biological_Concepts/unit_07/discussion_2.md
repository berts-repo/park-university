Discussion Topic

Mechanisms of Evolutionary Change

This unit we are learning that there are several mechanisms that lead to evolutionary change within a population or species: mutation, migration, genetic drift, non-random breeding and natural selection.

What is meant by “evolutionary change”; meaning what is it and how would you know when it has occurred?

Evolution change occurs when there is a variation in gene frequency within a population. There are four primary sources for genetic variation: mutations, migration (gene flow), genetic drift, and sex.

Select two of the mechanisms listed above and explain how each can lead to evolutionary change as defined in Q#1. Be specific and use an example of each.

Mutation is a change in the DNA sequence of a cell, this affects the look and behavior of a organism, and is caused when the cell makes a mistake during cell division. Common types of mutations in humans include: cystic fibrosis, color blindness, and down syndrome, but some good mutations include: increased bone density or malaria resistance.

Genetic drift is the change in the gene pool of a population due to chance.  A good example of this would be freckles, if people keep reproducing with the freckle alleles that would lead to a population with more freckles, this goes both ways.
