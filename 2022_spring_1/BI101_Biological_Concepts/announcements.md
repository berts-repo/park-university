## Preparing for the Final Exam
*Posted: 2022-02-22T18:00:00Z*

Class, as we work towards the final week of the course, we have a final to prepare for! The final is an online, open-book, open-note exam in Canvas. You will only get one 120-minute attempt to complete the exam; it is not like the weekly quizzes where you can retake them. It is worth 256 points, which is a good chunk of your total grade. The exam consists of primarily multiple-choice questions, there is one section to do some matching, and there are ten short answer questions. Be sure to complete your Week 8 work prior to taking the exam, as it will cover that material as well.

While the exam is open-book and open-note, you won't have time to look up every answer. A good way to prepare for this exam is to make a study guide covering the following topics:

Week 1 Objectives:

Use the steps of the scientific method

Demonstrate your knowledge of the four biological macromolecules by describing their structure and function,

Explain the three main types of chemical bonds,

Discuss the importance of pH related to biological function,

Explain the biological importance of water's physical properties.

Week 2 Objectives:

Explain the role of enzymes in reactions and how they carry out their role,

Discuss the relationship between photosynthesis and cellular respiration,

Describe the difference between the aerobic breakdown of glucose compared with the anaerobic breakdown,

Interpret results from the enzyme experiment and explain the outcome,

Discuss the effect of changes in temperature and pH on enzyme activity,

Explain what is produced and what the products are used for in both the light dependent and light independent reactions of photosynthesis,

Compare and contrast the net energy generated during the three major steps of aerobic respiration.

Week 3 Objectives:

Identify the major eukaryotic cell organelles and explain the functions of each,

Describe the fluid mosaic model of the cell membrane and explain the importance of its characteristics,

Compare and contrast diffusion and osmosis,

Differentiate among bacterial, plant, and animal cells based on appearance

Week 4 Objectives:

Compare and contrast the functions, processes and outcomes of mitosis and meiosis

Explain how each of the stages of the cell cycle relates to cell growth and cell division

Explain the warning signs of melanoma using the ABCD approach

Differentiate between homologous chromosomes and sister chromatids; how are they different, how are they similar and where are they found

Explain the importance of crossing over and explain the risks

Compare and contrast sexual and asexual reproduction

Describe the three basic processes of sexual selection and their importance

Explain the relationship between an allele and a gene

Week 5 Objectives:

Identify the similarities and differences of DNA and RNA in terms of structure, function and location in the cell,

Describe the relationship between the sequence of nucleotides in DNA and the production of proteins,

Compare the different types of DNA mutations in terms of mechanism and outcome,

Outline the steps to extract DNA from living material

Explain the importance of RNA splicing,

Describe the various mechanisms that regulate gene expression.

Week 6 Objectives:

Explain the difference between the genotype and phenotype,

Describe what incomplete dominance, complete dominance, and codominance are and provide an example of each,

Demonstrate your understanding of genetic crosses using a Punnett Square,

Explain what aneuploidy is and give a specific example of what happens when this occurs,

Describe what it means when a trait is said to be sex-linked.

Week 7 Objectives

Describe the process of natural selection and how it relates to evolution,

Explain the five other mechanisms that contribute to the evolutionary process besides natural selection

Illustrate the different mechanisms that result in reproductive isolation: both pre- and post- zygotic

Be able to construct survivorship graphs using data from a population study

Week 8 Objectives

Describe the relationships between populations, communities and ecosystems,

Compare and contrast r- and K-selected species,

Discuss the factors which influence/control population size,

Explain the three major types of interactions between species and provide an example of each,

Outline the species diversity (flora and fauna) and characteristics of the major terrestrial biomes,

Describe the energy relationships among trophic levels in terms of quantity and quality,

Best of luck to you as you prepare for this exam! You'll do great with a bit of prep work!
