# Unit 3: Overview

Introduction

In this unit, you will study physical security and authentications. We will first explore the design of physical locks, as well as attack tools and methods targeting on the physical locks. After that, we will study various authentication technologies. Finally, we will learn various direct attacks against computer and communication systems. You need to complete the required tasks in the below Readings and Materials section, as well as the Assignments section. You are encouraged to complete the optional and recommended tasks.

Unit Learning Outcomes

At the conclusion of the unit, the learner will be able to:

Explain the design of various physical locks and safes. (CLO 2)

Explain different methods and tools used to attack the physical locks and safes. (CLO 4)

Analyze the difficulties of the brute-force attack on breaking locks. (CLO 1)

Explain authentication mechanisms in various systems and their potential vulnerabilities (CLO 2, 3)

Explain various direct attacks against computers (CLO 4)
