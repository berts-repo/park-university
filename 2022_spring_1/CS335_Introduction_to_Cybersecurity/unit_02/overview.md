# Unit 2: Overview

Introduction

In this unit, you will learn the basics of access control, cryptography, and password. We will also learn the principle of brute force attack and learn how to evaluate the time complexity for the brute force attack. We will practice different encryption algorithms in the Lab. For discussion, we will discuss about the social engineer attacks that you have encountered and how can we defeat those attacks. You need to complete the required tasks in the below Readings and Materials section, as well as the Assignments section. You are encouraged to complete the optional and recommended tasks.

Unit Learning Outcomes

At the conclusion of the unit, the learner will be able to:

Explain and operate the access of the Linux file (CLO 2)

Explain different access control models (CLO 2)

Explain and use symmetric, asymmetric and hash cryptographic algorithms (CLO 1)

Explain and use digital signature and digital certificate (CLO 1)

Explain brute force attack and dictionary attack (CLO 4)

Analyze the time complexity and storage complexity of brute force attack and dictionary attack (CLO 4)

Explain and analyze different social engineering attacks and their countermeasures. (CLO 4)
