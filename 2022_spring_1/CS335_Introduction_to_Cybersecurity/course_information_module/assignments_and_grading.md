Discussions

There is one discussion questions in each unit. You must make an initial post by Wednesday 11:59pm Central Time in each unit for each question. Unless otherwise indicated, each post must adhere to the following guidelines:

Use your own words, no plagiarizing. You may copy and paste a sentence or two from another site (with quotes and appropriate attribution) to support your work, but in general these posts must be your own writing.

Write at least 100-150 words.

Provide at least 2 references (source and URL) asked to search for a topic or event. The words in the reference list do not count as part of 100-word requirement. URLs should be added with the link button in the editor so that fellow students can easily click on your references.

Be substantial so that your post can generate additional thoughts, discussions, concepts, etc.

Use correct spelling and grammar. The Canvas environment checks your spelling for you and it is courtesy to your fellow students to correct the mistakes it finds. Grammar doesn’t have to be perfect, but your posts should look like you have put some thought and care into them and not just typed a stream of consciousness as fast as you could.

Feel free to bring in your own personal experience. As long as it is relevant, a concrete example from your own experience, on which you can write with confidence, is interesting and provocative to the rest of the students in the class.

Review the responses to the course discussion topics by your classmates and provide feedback, using your own words, to at least 2 different postings in each discussion topic. Each response must be substantial (50 words or more) meaning that your responses must generate additional thoughts, discussion, concepts, etc. Post your responses by Sunday at 11:59pm Central Time.

Due Dates

Initial post: By Wednesday at 11:59 P.M., CT. 

Two peer responses: By Sunday at 11:59 P.M., CT.

Unit 4 Lab - Privillege Escalation

In this lab you will use the GENI lab environment to learn about and perform a basic privilege escalation attack. Privilege escalation is the action of infiltrating a system and granting yourself access privileges higher than what you have with the account/user you gained access to, in order to tamper with the system or files in some way, or to steal or modify stored information. In this lab, you will gain an understanding of how to perform privilege escalation with Apache and a reverse shell.

This final exam is to be taken via Park University Online website.

This final exam consists of approximately 24 multiple choice or True/False questions, as well as 6 short answer questions. 

It has a time limit of 120 minutes and can only be taken once.

It is proctored via Proctor U.

The exam must start before Sunday 9:00pm Central Time of Unit 8.

The exam is close book, close note.  

Scratch papers are allowed. An alternative to scratch paper is a white board, or a transparent sleeve with a blank sheet of paper in it that can be written on with a dry-erase marker, and then erased at the end of the exam.

Before taking this exam, it is recommended that you review all quizzes, homework, and labs.
